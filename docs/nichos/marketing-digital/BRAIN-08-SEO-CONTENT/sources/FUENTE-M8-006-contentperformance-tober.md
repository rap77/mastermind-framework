# FUENTE-M8-006: Content Performance Analysis — Marcus Tober

## Tesis Central

> El content SEO sin medición es content sin dirección. La mayoría de los marketers crean content, publican, y then…esperan mágicamente que rankee. Pero la realidad es que el éxito de content requiere medición CONTINUA y OPTIMIZACIÓN basada en data. Google cambia su algoritmo constantemente. Tu competencia mejora su content constantemente. Si vos no medís y mejorás, te quedás atrás. El framework de content performance tiene 4 pasos: (1) **Measure** — traffic, rankings, engagement, conversions, (2) **Analyze** — qué funciona, qué no, por qué, (3) **Optimize** — improvements basados en data, (4) **Repeat** — el ciclo nunca termina. La clave es establecer benchmarks (qué es "bueno" performance?) y KPIs (qué metrics importan REALMENTE para tu negocio?). Vanity metrics (rankings) < Business metrics (leads, sales).

## 1. Principios Fundamentales

> **P1: Content performance es multi-dimensional**
- Traffic solo no es suficiente
- Rankings solo no es suficiente
- Engagement solo no es suficiente
- Necesitás TODOS: traffic + rankings + engagement + conversions
> *Fuente: "Multi-Dimensional Performance" (Tober, 2023)*

> **P2: Lo que medís mejora, lo que no medís se estanca**
- Si no medís conversions, no vas a optimizar conversions
- Si no medís engagement, no vas a mejorar engagement
- Define KPIs ANTES de crear content
> *Fuente: "Measurement-Driven Improvement" (Tober, 2023)*

> **P3: Content decay es real**
- Content que rankea bien hoy puede rankear mal mañana
- Competitors create better content
- Google algorithm changes
- Necesitás actualizar content regularmente
> *Fuente: "Content Decay" (Tober, 2023)*

> **P4: A/B testing es la única forma de saber qué funciona realmente**
- Las "best practices" son guesses hasta que las testeás
- Lo que funciona para Site A puede NO funcionar para Site B
- Testeá todo: headlines, length, structure, CTAs
> *Fuente: "Testing Culture" (Tober, 2023)*

> **P5: Content score predicts performance**
- Searchmetrics y otras tools calculan "content score"
- Content score combina: length, readability, keywords, structure, multimedia
- Score alto = higher probability de rankings
> *Fuente: "Content Scoring" (Tober, 2023)*

## 2. Frameworks y Metodologías

### Framework 1: Content Performance Measurement

**Fuente:** "Content Performance Analysis" (Tober, 2023)
**Propósito:** Medir performance de content comprehensivamente.
**Cuándo usar:** Mensualmente para analizar content.

**Dimensions of Measurement:**

```yaml
Dimension 1: Traffic Metrics
  - Organic sessions (visitors from search)
  - Unique visitors (new vs returning)
  - Page views (total views)
  - Traffic growth (month-over-month)

  Tools: Google Analytics

Dimension 2: Rankings Metrics
  - Average position (across all keywords)
  - Top 10 rankings (how many keywords in top 10?)
  - Ranking changes (improvements vs declines)
  - Share of Voice (visibility in SERP)

  Tools: Google Search Console, Ahrefs, SEMrush

Dimension 3: Engagement Metrics
  - Time on page (how long they stay?)
  - Bounce rate (single page sessions)
  - Pages per session (depth of engagement)
  - Scroll depth (do they reach bottom?)

  Tools: Google Analytics, heatmaps (Hotjar)

Dimension 4: Conversion Metrics
  - Lead generation (form fills, demo requests)
  - Email sign-ups
  - Product purchases
  - Content downloads (guides, templates)

  Tools: Google Analytics, CRM

Dimension 5: Off-Page Metrics
  - Backlinks acquired
  - Social shares
  - Brand mentions
  - Domain Authority growth

  Tools: Ahrefs, Moz, Mention.com
```

**Performance Dashboard Template:**

```yaml
Monthly Content Report:

Top 10 Pages (by traffic):
  1. [URL] - [X sessions] - [Rankings] - [Conversions]
  2. [URL] - [X sessions] - [Rankings] - [Conversions]
  ...
  10. [URL] - [X sessions] - [Rankings] - [Conversions]

Bottom 10 Pages (by traffic):
  1. [URL] - [X sessions] - [Action needed]
  2. [URL] - [X sessions] - [Action needed]
  ...
  10. [URL] - [X sessions] - [Action needed]

Summary Metrics:
  - Total organic traffic: [X] ([+X%] vs last month)
  - Top 10 rankings: [X] keywords
  - Average position: [X]
  - Conversions: [X] ([+X%] vs last month)
  - New backlinks: [X]
```

### Framework 2: Content Audit Framework

**Fuente:** "Content Auditing" (Tober, 2023)
**Propósito:** Auditar content para identificar oportunidades.
**Cuándo usar:** Trimestralmente o cuando rankings stagnan.

**Step 1: Content Inventory**

```yaml
What to Track:
  - All content pages (URL, title, publish date)
  - Traffic (last 30 days, last 90 days)
  - Rankings (current position, best position)
  - Backlinks (number, quality)
  - Engagement metrics (time on page, bounce rate)
  - Conversions (if applicable)

Tools:
  - Google Analytics (custom report)
  - Google Search Console (export all pages)
  - Ahrefs (backlink data)
  - Spreadsheet (consolidate all data)
```

**Step 2: Categorize Content**

```yaml
Category 1: High Performers (Top 20%)
  - High traffic + high engagement + conversions
  - Action: Keep fresh, create more like this

Category 2: Mid Performers (Middle 60%)
  - Moderate traffic/engagement/conversions
  - Action: Optimize for higher performance

Category 3: Low Performers (Bottom 20%)
  - Low traffic + low engagement + zero conversions
  - Action: Delete, consolidate, or update

Category 4: Declining Content
  - Previously good, now declining
  - Action: Investigate why (competitors? algorithm?) and update
```

**Step 3: Optimization Action Plan**

```yaml
For High Performers:
  - Update regularly (add fresh data, examples)
  - Create spin-off content (deep dives on sub-topics)
  - Build more internal links
  - Promote more aggressively

For Mid Performers:
  - Optimize title/meta for higher CTR
  - Improve internal linking
  - Add multimedia (images, videos)
  - Expand content (make it more comprehensive)
  - Build backlinks

For Low Performers:
  - Delete if: thin, obsolete, no traffic, no backlinks
  - Consolidate if: similar topics exist (merge into comprehensive guide)
  - Update if: good topic but poor execution (rewrite, improve)

For Declining Content:
  - Analyze competitors (what changed?)
  - Check if search intent changed
  - Update with fresh data/examples
  - Rebuild backlinks
```

### Framework 3: Content Scoring System

**Fuente:** "Content Scoring" (Tober, 2023)
**Propósito:** Calcular quality score de content.
**Cuándo usar:** Antes de publicar para predecir performance.

**Content Score Components:**

```yaml
Component 1: Length (20%)
  - < 300 words: 0/10 points
  - 300-500 words: 3/10 points
  - 500-1000 words: 5/10 points
  - 1000-1500 words: 7/10 points
  - 1500-2000+ words: 10/10 points

Component 2: Readability (20%)
  - Flesch score 60-70: 10/10 points
  - Flesch score 50-60 or 70-80: 7/10 points
  - Flesch score 30-50 or 80-90: 5/10 points
  - Flesch score < 30 or > 90: 3/10 points

Component 3: Keyword Optimization (15%)
  - Keyword in title: 3/5 points
  - Keyword in H1: 2/5 points
  - Keyword density 1-2%: 5/5 points
  - Secondary keywords included: 5/5 points

Component 4: Structure (15%)
  - H2/H3 structure: 3/5 points
  - Short paragraphs: 3/5 points
  - Bullet points: 3/5 points
  - Multimedia (images/videos): 3/5 points

Component 5: Internal Linking (10%)
  - 3-5 internal links: 5/5 points
  - 1-2 internal links: 3/5 points
  - 0 internal links: 0/5 points

Component 6: External Linking (10%)
  - 3-5 authority links: 5/5 points
  - 1-2 authority links: 3/5 points
  - 0 external links: 0/5 points

Component 7: Uniqueness (10%)
  - Original research/data: 10/10 points
  - Unique insights: 7/10 points
  - Curated content: 5/10 points
  - Generic content: 3/10 points

Total Score = Sum of all components (max 100 points)
```

**Score Interpretation:**

| Score Range | Prediction | Action |
|-------------|------------|--------|
| 80-100 | High probability of rankings | Publish as-is |
| 60-79 | Good probability | Minor improvements |
| 40-59 | Medium probability | Significant improvements |
| 0-39 | Low probability | Major rewrite or delete |

### Framework 4: A/B Testing Framework

**Fuente:** "A/B Testing Content" (Tober, 2023)
**Propósito:** Testear variations para optimizar performance.
**Cuándo usar:** Continuamente (siempre estar testing).

**What to Test:**

```yaml
Test 1: Headlines
  Variation A: Original headline
  Variation B: Different formula (question vs statement)
  Variation C: Different benefit

  Metric: CTR from SERP

Test 2: Content Length
  Variation A: 800 words
  Variation B: 1500 words
  Variation C: 2500 words

  Metric: Time on page, rankings

Test 3: Opening Hook
  Variation A: Problem agitation
  Variation B: Story opening
  Variation C: Statistic hook

  Metric: Bounce rate, engagement

Test 4: CTA Placement
  Variation A: CTA above fold
  Variation B: CTA middle of content
  Variation C: CTA end of content

  Metric: Conversion rate

Test 5: Visual Elements
  Variation A: Text only
  Variation B: Text + images
  Variation C: Text + video

  Metric: Engagement, time on page
```

**Testing Process:**

```yaml
Step 1: Hypothesis
  - "Changing headline to question format will increase CTR by 20%"

Step 2: Create Variations
  - Variation A (control)
  - Variation B (test)

Step 3: Run Test
  - Split traffic 50/50
  - Run for minimum 1,000 visitors or 2 weeks

Step 4: Analyze Results
  - Statistical significance (95% confidence)
  - Winner declared

Step 5: Implement Winner
  - Update content
  - Document learning
  - Move to next test

Tools:
  - Google Optimize
  - Optimizely
  - VWO
```

**Testing Best Practices:**

```yaml
Best Practice 1: Test One Variable at a Time
  - Don't test headline + opening + CTA simultaneously
  - Isolate variables to know WHAT caused improvement

Best Practice 2: Statistical Significance
  - Don't declare winner after 100 visitors
  - Wait for 95% confidence or minimum sample size

Best Practice 3: Document Everything
  - What did you test?
  - What was the result?
  - What did you learn?

Best Practice 4: Keep Testing
  - Never stop testing
  - What works today may not work tomorrow
  - Continuous optimization = continuous improvement
```

## 3. Modelos Mentales

### Modelo 1: La Ecuación de Content Performance

```
Performance =
  (Traffic × Rankings × Engagement × Conversions)
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────
             Competition × Algorithm Changes
```

**Key insight:** Performance es relativo a competition y algorithm changes.

### Modelo 2: El Ciclo de Optimización

```
Measure → Analyze → Optimize → Test → Implement → Repeat
   ↑                                              ↓
   └──────────────────────────── Learn ←────────┘
```

**Key insight:** Content optimization es ciclo continuo, no one-time project.

### Modelo 3: La Matriz de Acción de Content

```
           │ Low Traffic │ High Traffic │
─────────────────────────────────────────────
High Conv │  Optimize  │   Scale    │
           │  (promote) │  (more like│
           │            │   this)    │
─────────────────────────────────────────────
Low Conv  │   Delete   │  Fix CTA    │
           │(or merge) │  (problem   │
           │            │  identified)│
           └────────────┴─────────────┘
```

**Key insight:** Action depende de traffic + conversión performance.

## 4. Criterios de Decisión

### Decisión 1: ¿Update o Rewrite?

**Update si:**
- Content tiene potential (good topic, poor execution)
- Content ya tiene rankings/traffic
- Content tiene backlinks
- Refresh puede mejorar performance significativamente

**Rewrite si:**
- Content es thin (< 500 words)
- Content está desactualizado/obsolete
- Content tiene zero traffic/backlinks
- Topic cambió significativamente

### Decisión 2:¿A/B Test or Roll Out Improvement?

**A/B Test si:**
- Traffic suficiente (> 1000 visitors/month)
- Uncertainty about qué funciona
- Want data-driven decision
- Time/resources to run test

**Roll Out Improvement si:**
- Obvious fix (broken link, typo)
- Best practice established (industry standard)
- Traffic bajo (< 500 visitors/month)
- Urgent improvement needed

## 5. Anti-patrones

### Anti-patrón 1: "Looking at Rankings Only"

**Problema:** Solo medís rankings position.

**Consecuencias:**
- Rankings pueden mejorar sin traffic/conversions
- Missing opportunities para optimizar conversions
- Vanity metric > business metric

**Solución:** Medí todo: traffic, rankings, engagement, conversions.

### Anti-patrón 2: "Set and Forget Content"

**Problema:** Publicás content y nunca volvés a mirarlo.

**Consecuencias:**
- Content decay (competidores create better)
- Outdated information
- Lost rankings over time

**Solución:** Revisión trimestral de todos los content pieces.

### Anti-patrón 3: "Guessing Instead of Testing"

**Problema:** Hacés cambios basados en "lo que creo que funciona".

**Consecuencias:**
- Could be wrong
- Could make things worse
- Missing opportunity to learn what ACTUALLY works

**Solución:** A/B test everything. Data > guesses.
