# FUENTE-140: The Age of Influence — Neal Schaffer

## Tesis Central

> **"Influencer marketing isn't about finding people with large followings—it's about finding people with engaged communities that align with your brand values and target audience."**

---

## 1. Principios Fundamentales

> **P1: The Your Brand, Your Way (YBYW) Principle**
> Your employees are your most underrated influencers. They know your product better than anyone, and their advocacy is more authentic than paid influencers.
> *Fuente: [The Age of Influence, Chapter 3 (Schaffer, 2020)]*
> *Contexto: When building an influencer program, start internally before looking externally.*

> **P2: The Micro-Influencer Advantage**
> Micro-influencers (1K-100K followers) have higher engagement rates and more authentic relationships with their audience than mega-influencers.
> *Fuente: [The Age of Influence, Chapter 5 (Schaffer, 2020)]*
> *Contexto: When deciding between macro vs micro-influencers for campaigns.*

> **P3: Relevance Over Reach**
> An influencer with 10,000 highly engaged followers in your niche is more valuable than one with 1 million generic followers.
> *Fuente: [The Age of Influence, Chapter 6 (Schaffer, 2020)]*
> *Contexto: When vetting and selecting influencers for partnerships.*

> **P4: Authenticity is Non-Negotiable**
> Influencers must genuinely believe in your product. Forced partnerships damage both brand and influencer credibility.
> *Fuente: [The Age of Influence, Chapter 4 (Schaffer, 2020)]*
> *Contexto: When structuring influencer partnerships and content guidelines.*

> **P5: Influencer Marketing is Relationship Marketing**
> It's not a one-off transaction—it's about building long-term relationships with creators who become brand advocates.
> *Fuente: [The Age of Influence, Chapter 12 (Schaffer, 2020)]*
> *Contexto: When designing influencer program strategy and structure.*

## 2. Frameworks y Metodologías

### Framework 1: The Influencer Marketing ROI Framework

**Fuente:** [The Age of Influence, Chapter 15 (Schaffer, 2020)]
**Propósito:** Measure and justify influencer marketing investment
**Cuándo usar:** When presenting influencer marketing budgets to stakeholders

**Pasos/Componentes:**
1. **Define Clear Objectives:** Awareness, consideration, conversion, or retention
2. **Establish Baseline Metrics:** Current performance before influencer campaigns
3. **Track Multi-Touch Attribution:** Credit influencers for assist conversions
4. **Measure Engagement Quality:** Not just likes, but comments, shares, saves
5. **Calculate Earned Media Value (EMV):** What would the same reach cost via paid ads?
6. **Assess Long-Term Impact:** Brand lift, sentiment shift, audience growth

**Output esperado:** Comprehensive ROI report showing direct and indirect value of influencer partnerships

---

### Framework 2: The Influencer Vetting Process

**Fuente:** [The Age of Influence, Chapter 7 (Schaffer, 2020)]
**Propósito:** Systematically evaluate potential influencer partners
**Cuándo usar:** When building an influencer roster for campaigns

**Pasos/Componentes:**
1. **Relevance Check:** Does their content align with your brand/niche?
2. **Engagement Rate Analysis:** (likes + comments + shares) / followers × 100
3. **Audience Demographics:** Are their followers your target customers?
4. **Content Quality Assessment:** Is their content professional, consistent, on-brand?
5. **Brand Safety Review:** Check past content for controversies or conflicts
6. **Authenticity Test:** Do they promote products they actually use?
7. **Performance History:** Ask for case studies from previous brand partnerships
8. **Professionalism:** Response time, contract willingness, clear communication

**Output esperado:** Scored influencer evaluation with go/no-go recommendation

---

### Framework 3: The Influencer Campaign Execution Model

**Fuente:** [The Age of Influence, Chapter 10 (Schaffer, 2020)]
**Propósito:** End-to-end process for running successful influencer campaigns
**Cuándo usar:** When launching a new influencer marketing initiative

**Pasos/Componentes:**
1. **Campaign Briefing:** Clear goals, timeline, budget, deliverables, guidelines
2. **Creative Freedom Balance:** Provide direction, allow influencer's voice to shine
3. **Content Approval Process:** Review cycle that doesn't stifle authenticity
4. **Launch Coordination:** Coordinate posting dates, cross-promotion, tagging
5. **Community Management:** Monitor comments, engage with audience responses
6. **Amplification:** Repost influencer content on brand channels (with permission)
7. **Performance Reporting:** Share results with influencers, strengthen relationship

**Output esperado:** Executed campaign with measurable results and strengthened creator relationships

## 3. Modelos Mentales

### Modelo Mental 1: The Influencer Continuum

**Fuente:** [The Age of Influence, Chapter 2 (Schaffer, 2020)]
**El modelo:** Influencers exist on a spectrum from celebrity endorsers to niche experts to everyday customers. The most effective influencer strategies combine multiple tiers.
**Por qué funciona:** Different tiers serve different purposes—celebrities for awareness, micro-influencers for engagement, customers for authenticity.
**Cuándo usarlo:** When designing influencer strategy and budget allocation
**Limitaciones:** Over-reliance on one tier limits campaign effectiveness

---

### Modelo Mental 2: The Trust Transfer Theory

**Fuente:** [The Age of Influence, Chapter 4 (Schaffer, 2020)]
**El modelo:** Influencer marketing works because the influencer transfers their audience's trust to the brand. Each endorsement either strengthens or weakens this trust transfer.
**Por qué funciona:** Explains why authenticity matters more than reach. One inauthentic endorsement breaks the trust transfer permanently.
**Cuándo usarlo:** When evaluating whether an influencer partnership makes sense
**Limitaciones:** Doesn't account for new audience discovery without trust transfer

---

### Modelo Mental 3: The Content Ecosystem Model

**Fuente:** [The Age of Influence, Chapter 11 (Schaffer, 2020)]
**El modelo:** Influencer content should be integrated into the broader content strategy, not isolated. It becomes a content amplification engine, not just paid placement.
**Por qué funciona:** Maximizes ROI by repurposing, amplifying, and learning from influencer content
**Cuándo usarlo:** When planning content calendars and influencer campaigns together
**Limitaciones:** Requires close coordination between content and influencer marketing teams

## 4. Criterios de Decisión

### Trade-off: Macro-Influencers vs. Micro-Influencers

**Fuente:** [The Age of Influence, Chapter 5 (Schaffer, 2020)]

**Opción A: Macro-Influencers (100K+ followers)**
- **Ventajas:** Massive reach, professional content production, brand prestige
- **Desventajas:** Lower engagement rates, high cost, less authentic connection
- **Mejor para:** Brand awareness campaigns, product launches, major announcements

**Opción B: Micro-Influencers (1K-100K followers)**
- **Ventajas:** Higher engagement, niche authority, lower cost, authentic relationships
- **Desventajas:** Smaller reach, less production quality, more coordination required
- **Mejor para:** Conversion campaigns, niche products, ongoing advocacy, community building

**Recomendación del experto:** Start with micro-influencers for most brands. Only use macro-influencers when you have budget for reach and clear awareness objectives. The best strategies combine both tiers strategically.

---

### Trade-off: One-Off Campaigns vs. Long-Term Ambassadorships

**Fuente:** [The Age of Influence, Chapter 12 (Schaffer, 2020)]

**Opción A: One-Off Campaigns**
- **Ventajas:** Flexibility, test multiple creators, lower commitment, clear timelines
- **Desventajas:** No relationship building, higher cost per result, no cumulative benefit
- **Mejor para:** Testing new channels, seasonal promotions, specific product pushes

**Opción B: Long-Term Ambassadorships**
- **Ventajas:** Authenticity development, audience trust transfer, better rates, content library
- **Desventajas:** Higher commitment, relationship investment required, less flexibility
- **Mejor para:** Core brand messaging, ongoing product promotion, community building

**Recomendación del experto:** Prioritize long-term ambassadorships. The most effective influencer marketing programs treat influencers as true partners, not media buys. 70% ambassadorships, 30% campaign-based.

## 5. Anti-patrones

### Anti-patrón 1: The Vanity Metrics Trap

**Fuente:** [The Age of Influence, Chapter 15 (Schaffer, 2020)]
**Qué es:** Judging influencer success solely by follower count and likes
**Por qué la gente lo hace:** These metrics are visible and easy to track
**Consecuencias:** Wasted budget on influencers with fake or unengaged audiences
**Cómo evitarlo:** Focus on engagement rates, audience quality, and business metrics
**Qué hacer en su lugar:** Create custom tracking links, use promo codes, measure actual conversions

---

### Anti-patrón 2: Over-Scripting Influencer Content

**Fuente:** [The Age of Influence, Chapter 10 (Schaffer, 2020)]
**Qué es:** Giving influencers detailed scripts that remove their authentic voice
**Por qué la gente lo hace:** Desire for brand consistency and message control
**Consecuencias:** Content feels inauthentic, engagement drops, audience mistrust
**Cómo evitarlo:** Provide clear briefs and guardrails, but allow creative freedom
**Qué hacer en su lugar:** Focus on key messages and requirements, let influencers deliver in their style

---

### Anti-patrón 3: Neglecting Contract Details

**Fuente:** [The Age of Influence, Chapter 8 (Schaffer, 2020)]
**Qué es:** Running influencer campaigns without clear contracts covering usage rights, exclusivity, payment terms
**Por qué la gente lo hace:** Wanting to move quickly, viewing influencers as partners not vendors
**Consecuencias:** Legal disputes, unexpected costs, content ownership issues, FTC compliance problems
**Cómo evitarlo:** Always use contracts, even with small influencers. Cover all bases upfront.
**Qué hacer en su lugar:** Standard influencer contract template covering deliverables, payment, usage rights, exclusivity, FTC disclosure, cancellation terms

---

### Anti-patrón 4: Ignoring FTC Disclosure Guidelines

**Fuente:** [The Age of Influence, Chapter 9 (Schaffer, 2020)]
**Qué es:** Failing to ensure influencers properly disclose sponsored content
**Por qué la gente lo hace:** Wanting content to feel organic, fear of lower engagement
**Consecuencias:** FTC fines, brand damage, loss of consumer trust, platform penalties
**Cómo evitarlo:** Make #ad or "sponsored" disclosure a non-negotiable contract requirement
**Qué hacer en su lugar:** Educate influencers on disclosure requirements, review posts before publication
