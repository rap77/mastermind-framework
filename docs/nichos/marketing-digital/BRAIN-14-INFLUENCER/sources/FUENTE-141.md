# FUENTE-141: VeryGood Ventures Framework — Joe Gagliese

## Tesis Central

> **"The best influencer marketing happens when brands stop treating creators as media channels and start treating them as creative partners."**

---

## 1. Principios Fundamentales

> **P1: Creator-First Philosophy**
> The creator's voice and audience connection are the assets you're investing in, not just their reach. Protect and amplify, don't suppress.
> *Fuente: [VeryGood Ventures Masterclass, Module 1 (Gagliese, 2023)]*
> *Contexto: When structuring influencer partnerships and campaign briefs.*

> **P2: Platform Native Content**
> Each social platform has unique content norms. Influencers know what works on their platform—don't force TV ad scripts onto TikTok.
> *Fuente: [VeryGood Ventures Masterclass, Module 3 (Gagliese, 2023)]*
> *Contexto: When reviewing influencer content and providing creative feedback.*

> **P3: Quantified Authenticity**
> Authenticity can be measured. Watch time, comment sentiment, and save rates indicate authentic content better than likes.
> *Fuente: [VeryGood Ventures Masterclass, Module 5 (Gagliese, 2023)]*
> *Contexto: When evaluating influencer campaign performance and creator effectiveness.*

> **P4: The Portfolio Approach**
> Work with a diverse portfolio of creators across niches, platforms, and audience sizes. Don't bet the campaign on one influencer.
> *Fuente: [VeryGood Ventures Masterclass, Module 2 (Gagliese, 2023)]*
> *Contexto: When building influencer rosters and allocating campaign budgets.*

> **P5: Feedback Loops Over One-Way Briefs**
> The best campaigns emerge from collaborative briefs where creators contribute ideas, not just execute brand directions.
> *Fuente: [VeryGood Ventures Masterclass, Module 4 (Gagliese, 2023)]*
> *Contexto: When kicking off influencer campaigns and setting expectations.*

## 2. Frameworks y Metodologías

### Framework 1: The Creator Partnership Framework

**Fuente:** [VeryGood Ventures Methodology (Gagliese, 2023)]
**Propósito:** Structure influencer relationships for long-term success
**Cuándo usar:** When shifting from campaign-based to partnership-based influencer marketing

**Pasos/Componentes:**
1. **Alignment Phase:** Shared values, audience fit, creative chemistry
2. **Co-Creation Phase:** Collaborative briefs, creator input on messaging
3. **Execution Phase:** Creative freedom within guardrails, real-time optimization
4. **Amplification Phase:** Cross-post, repurpose, learn from what works
5. **Evaluation Phase:** Share performance data, iterate on partnership

**Output esperado:** Influencer relationships that compound over time vs one-off campaigns

---

### Framework 2: The Platform-Native Content Matrix

**Fuente:** [VeryGood Ventures Masterclass, Module 3 (Gagliese, 2023)]
**Propósito:** Match content formats to platform norms
**Cuándo usar:** When planning multi-platform influencer campaigns

**Platform Guidelines:**
- **TikTok:** 15-60s, native sound, trends, raw/authentic feel, hooks in first 2 seconds
- **Instagram Reels:** 30-90s, aesthetic visuals, trending audio, caption-driven storytelling
- **YouTube:** Long-form deep dives, intros/outros matter, SEO titles/descriptions
- **Instagram Stories:** Raw/in-the-moment, polls/questions, swipe-ups for conversion
- **LinkedIn:** Professional tone, carousel documents, thought leadership

**Output esperado:** Platform-appropriate briefs that let creators shine in their native environment

---

### Framework 3: The Authenticity Scoring System

**Fuente:** [VeryGood Ventures Internal Tools (Gagliese, 2023)]
**Propósito:** Evaluate whether influencer content feels authentic
**Cuándo usar:** When reviewing influencer submissions before publication

**Scoring Criteria (1-5 each):**
1. **Creator Voice Preservation:** Does it sound like them or a brand script?
2. **Audience Value:** Would the audience watch this even if it wasn't sponsored?
3. **Product Natural Fit:** Does the product fit naturally into their content world?
4. **Disclosure Quality:** Is the disclosure clear but doesn't kill the vibe?
5. **Production Quality Match:** Does it match their usual production level?

**Output esperado:** Authenticity score that predicts engagement and brand safety

## 3. Modelos Mentales

### Modelo Mental 1: The Creator Economy Flywheel

**Fuente:** [VeryGood Ventures Philosophy (Gagliese, 2023)]
**El modelo:** Successful influencer marketing creates a flywheel—great content → brand affinity → more creator interest → better content →更强品牌效应
**Por qué funciona:** Explains why top brands get the best creators without premium rates
**Cuándo usarlo:** When justifying long-term investment in influencer relationships
**Limitaciones:** Takes time to build the flywheel, not suitable for quick one-off campaigns

---

### Modelo Mental 2: The Attention Trust Equation

**Fuente:** [VeryGood Ventures Masterclass, Module 5 (Gagliese, 2023)]
**El modelo:** Influencer marketing works because creators have built trust with their audience. Brands rent that trust, but over-scripting or inauthentic partnerships withdraw from the trust bank account.
**Por qué funciona:** Explains why some influencer campaigns backfire and damage brands
**Cuándo usarlo:** When evaluating influencer partnership proposals and creative approaches
**Limitaciones:** Difficult to measure trust quantitatively, requires qualitative judgment

## 4. Criterios de Decisión

### Trade-off: Creative Freedom vs. Brand Control

**Fuente:** [VeryGood Ventures Methodology (Gagliese, 2023)]

**Opción A: Tight Creative Control**
- **Ventajas:** Message consistency, brand safety, predictable output
- **Desventajas:** Lower engagement, feels like ads, creator frustration
- **Mejor para:** Highly regulated industries, product launches with specific messaging

**Opción B: High Creative Freedom**
- **Ventajas:** Authentic content, higher engagement, creator enthusiasm
- **Desventajas:** Message inconsistency, brand safety risk, unpredictable
- **Mejor para:** Lifestyle brands, awareness campaigns, creative product categories

**Recomendación del experto:** Find the middle ground—"guardrails, not scripts." Provide clear must-haves (key messages, claims to avoid, FTC disclosure) but let creators determine how to deliver. The best content comes from creators who feel ownership.

## 5. Anti-patrones

### Anti-patrón 1: The TV Commercial Mindset

**Fuente:** [VeryGood Ventures Masterclass, Module 3 (Gagliese, 2023)]
**Qué es:** Approaching influencer content like traditional TV ads—scripted, polished, product-focused
**Por qué la gente lo hace:** Trained in traditional marketing, fear of losing control
**Consecuencias:** Content feels inauthentic, audiences skip, low engagement
**Cómo evitarlo:** Trust creators to know what works on their platform
**Qué hacer en su lugar:** Focus on key messages and let creators execute in their style

---

### Anti-patrón 2: Platform Agnostic Briefs

**Fuente:** [VeryGood Ventures Masterclass, Module 3 (Gagliese, 2023)]
**Qué es:** Sending the same creative brief to TikTok creators and YouTube creators
**Por qué la gente lo hace:** Efficiency, desire for message consistency
**Consecuencias:** Content feels out of place on each platform, underperforms
**Cómo evitarlo:** Customize briefs for each platform's content norms
**Qué hacer en su lugar:** Platform-specific guidelines while maintaining core message consistency
