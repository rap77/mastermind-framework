# FUENTE-147: Influencer Vetting and Brand Safety — Becky O.

## Tesis Central

> **"The most expensive influencer partnership isn't the one with the highest rate—it's the one that damages your brand reputation. Thorough vetting is insurance against PR disasters, fraud, and wasted budget."**

---

## 1. Principios Fundamentales

> **P1: Past Behavior Predicts Future Behavior**
> An influencer's past content is the best predictor of how they'll represent your brand. If they've posted controversial content before, they'll likely do it again.
> *Fuente: [Influencer Vetting Checklist (Becky O., 2023)]*
> *Contexto: When conducting initial influencer research and background checks.*

> **P2: Engagement Quality Matters More Than Quantity**
> 100K fake likes are worth less than 1K real comments. High follower counts with low engagement are red flags for bought followers.
> *Fuente: [Spotting Fake Influencers (Becky O., 2023)]*
> *Contexto: When verifying influencer audience authenticity.*

> **P3: Brand Safety is Non-Negotiable**
> One controversial post from an influencer can damage your brand for years. It's not worth the risk, no matter how big their audience.
> *Fuente: [Brand Safety Framework (Becky O., 2023)]*
> *Contexto: When evaluating potential influencer partners and setting campaign guidelines.*

> **P4: Audience Demographics Must Match Your Customer**
> An influencer's follower count is irrelevant if their audience isn't your target customer. Verify demographics before committing.
> *Fuente: [Audience Verification (Becky O., 2023)]*
> *Contexto: When assessing whether an influencer can reach your target market.*

> **P5: Contracts Should Protect You from Creator Risk**
> Your contract is your insurance. Include morality clauses, content approval rights, and exit strategies for problematic behavior.
> *Fuente: [Influencer Contract Essentials (Becky O., 2023)]*
> *Contexto: When structuring influencer agreements and partnerships.*

## 2. Frameworks y Metodologías

### Framework 1: The Influencer Vetting Process

**Fuente:** [Influencer Vetting Checklist (Becky O., 2023)]
**Propósito:** Comprehensive vetting process to assess influencer suitability
**Cuándo usar:** Before signing any influencer partnership or contract

**Phase 1: Initial Screening (30 minutes)**

**Basic Metrics Check:**
- Follower count: Is it aligned with your budget and goals?
- Engagement rate: Calculate (likes + comments) / followers × 100
  - <1%: Red flag (possible fake followers)
  - 1-3%: Normal for large accounts
  - 3-6%: Good engagement
  - >6%: Excellent engagement
- Recent post frequency: Are they consistently active?

**Quick Red Flag Check:**
- Scan last 20 posts for any obvious issues
- Check bio for any controversial affiliations
- Look for recent sponsorships with competitors

**Go/No-Go Decision:** If passes, proceed to Phase 2. If fails, discard.

---

**Phase 2: Deep Background Check (2-3 hours)**

**Content Analysis (Last 100 posts):**
1. **Controversial Content:** Any posts about politics, religion, polarizing topics?
2. **Offensive Content:** Profanity, sexual content, violent imagery?
3. **Brand Conflicts:** Partnerships with competitors or conflicting categories?
4. **Authenticity:** Do they disclose sponsored content properly?
5. **Comment Sentiment:** Are comments genuine or spammy?

**Audience Analysis:**
1. **Request media kit:** If they don't have one, that's a red flag
2. **Verify demographics:** Age, location, gender of their audience
3. **Check follower growth:** Sudden spikes suggest bought followers
4. **Analyze engagement patterns:** Are comments from real accounts or bots?

**Professional Vetting:**
1. **Google their name:** + "scandal", + "controversy", + "lawsuit"
2. **Check news results:** Any negative press coverage?
3. **Social search:** Twitter, Reddit for mentions of their name
4. **Better Business Bureau:** Any complaints if they sell products?

**Go/No-Go Decision:** If clean, proceed to Phase 3. If concerns found, reconsider.

---

**Phase 3: Reference Check (1-2 hours)**

**Past Brand Partners:**
1. **Contact 2-3 brands** they've worked with previously
2. **Ask specific questions:**
   - Did they deliver on time?
   - Was the content on-brand?
   - How was their communication?
   - Would you work with them again?
   - Any issues or concerns?

**Industry Reputation:**
1. **Ask industry contacts:** Anyone know this creator?
2. **Check agency databases:** Many agencies track creator reputation
3. **Search for public feedback:** Other brands' experiences

**Final Decision:** Approved → Proceed to contract. Concerns → Require additional protections or pass.

---

**Total Time Investment:** 3-6 hours per influencer for thorough vetting

**Output esperado:** Comprehensive vetting report with go/no-go recommendation

### Framework 2: The Brand Safety Scoring System

**Fuente:** [Brand Safety Framework (Becky O., 2023)]
**Propósito:** Quantify brand safety risk for each influencer
**Cuándo usar:** When comparing multiple influencer options

**Scoring Categories (1-10 each, 10 = safest):**

**1. Content Safety (30% weight)**
- **10:** Completely safe, family-friendly, non-controversial
- **7-9:** Mostly safe, occasional mild profanity or edgy humor
- **4-6:** Some risk, occasional political/religious content
- **1-3:** High risk, frequent controversial topics
- **Assessment:** Manual review of last 50 posts

**2. Audience Appropriateness (25% weight)**
- **10:** Perfect match to your target demographic
- **7-9:** Good overlap, minor demographic differences
- **4-6:** Partial overlap, some relevance
- **1-3:** Poor demographic fit
- **Assessment:** Media kit demographics + audience survey data

**3. Authenticity Score (20% weight)**
- **10:** Highly authentic, genuine engagement, trusted voice
- **7-9:** Authentic, minor promotional content
- **4-6:** Mixed authenticity, somewhat promotional
- **1-3:** Inauthentic, overly promotional, engagement seems fake
- **Assessment:** Engagement quality + comment sentiment analysis

**4. Professional Reliability (15% weight)**
- **10:** Excellent references, always delivers, professional
- **7-9:** Good references, mostly reliable
- **4-6:** Mixed reviews, some reliability concerns
- **1-3:** Poor references, unreliable
- **Assessment:** Reference checks with past partners

**5. Compliance Track Record (10% weight)**
- **10:** Perfect FTC disclosure, no compliance issues
- **7-9:** Generally compliant, occasional minor issues
- **4-6:** Sometimes forgets disclosure, needs reminders
- **1-3:** Frequent compliance problems
- **Assessment:** Review past sponsored content for disclosure

**Scoring Interpretation:**
- **45-50:** Excellent fit—move forward
- **40-44:** Good fit—acceptable
- **35-39:** Moderate risk—proceed with caution
- **30-34:** High risk—avoid
- **<30:** Unacceptable—do not engage

**Output esperado:** Quantitative brand safety assessment for comparison

### Framework 3: The Influencer Contract Protection Checklist

**Fuente:** [Influencer Contract Essentials (Becky O., 2023)]
**Propósito:** Contract clauses that protect brands from influencer risk
**Cuándo usar:** When drafting influencer partnership agreements

**Essential Clauses:**

**1. Morality Clause (CRITICAL)**
- Brand can terminate immediately if influencer engages in conduct that damages brand reputation
- Defines "damaging conduct" explicitly (illegal acts, controversial statements, offensive content)
- Includes clawback clause for fees paid if terminated for cause
- **Example:** "Influencer agrees not to engage in any conduct that could bring Brand into disrepute. Brand may terminate immediately and recover all fees if Influencer violates this clause."

**2. Content Approval Rights**
- Brand must approve all content before publication
- Clear timeline for approval (e.g., "Brand will respond within 48 hours")
- Revision process if content doesn't meet guidelines
- **Example:** "Influencer will submit all content for Brand approval at least 7 days before publication. Brand may request revisions for any reason."

**3. FTC Disclosure Requirement**
- Influencer must comply with all FTC guidelines
- Specific disclosure language required (e.g., "#ad", "Sponsored")
- Brand can withhold payment if disclosure is missing
- **Example:** "Influencer will clearly and conspicuously disclose sponsored content using #ad or 'Sponsored' at the beginning of the post."

**4. Exclusivity Clause (if applicable)**
- Influencer cannot work with competitors during partnership period
- Defines competitor categories
- May include "blackout period" after campaign ends
- **Example:** "Influencer agrees not to promote any competing brands for 30 days before and after the campaign period."

**5. Usage Rights**
- Brand can reuse influencer content on own channels
- Defined duration (e.g., "perpetual" or "12 months")
- Defined territories (e.g., "worldwide")
- **Example:** "Brand has perpetual, worldwide rights to repurpose content across all marketing channels."

**6. Payment Structure**
- Clear payment milestones and timing
- Payment contingent on content approval and compliance
- Holdback percentage (e.g., 20% held until campaign completion)
- **Example:** "Brand will pay 50% on content approval, 50% on successful campaign completion. 20% of total fee will be held as compliance guarantee for 60 days."

**7. Performance Metrics (if applicable)**
- Defined KPIs for campaign success
- Bonus structure for exceeding targets
- Make-good clause if underperformance
- **Example:** "Influencer will earn 20% bonus for content exceeding 100,000 views. If content falls below 50,000 views, Influencer will provide additional content at no cost."

**8. Indemnification**
- Influencer indemnifies brand for claims arising from influencer's actions
- Includes IP infringement, defamation, privacy violations
- **Example:** "Influencer agrees to indemnify Brand against any claims arising from Influencer's content or conduct."

**Output esperado:** Comprehensive contract protecting brand interests

## 3. Modelos Mentales

### Modelo Mental 1: The Influencer Risk Pyramid

**Fuente:** [Brand Safety Framework (Becky O., 2023)]
**El modelo:** Influencer risk exists on a pyramid: (1) Fraud (bought followers, fake engagement), (2) Brand safety (controversial content), (3) Reputation (creator scandals), (4) Legal (FTC compliance, IP issues), (5) Performance (under-delivery). Each level requires different mitigation.
**Por qué funciona:** Helps categorize and prioritize different types of influencer risk
**Cuándo usarlo:** When conducting influencer risk assessment and planning mitigation strategies
**Limitaciones:** Risk categories can overlap, requires expertise in each area

**Fuente:** [Influencer Vetting (Becky O., 2023)]
**El modelo:** Trust is the product of authenticity × consistency × transparency. An influencer who scores high on all three is trustworthy. Low scores on any dimension are red flags.
**Por qué funciona:** Provides a simple framework for assessing influencer trustworthiness

## 4. Criterios de Decisión

### Trade-off: Strict vs Flexible Vetting

**Fuente:** [Influencer Vetting Strategy (Becky O., 2023)]

**Opción A: Strict Vetting (Thorough Process)**
- **Descripción:** 3-6 hour vetting process for every influencer, multiple approval stages
- **Ventajas:** Maximum brand safety, minimized risk, quality partnerships
- **Desventajas:** Time-intensive, may miss opportunities, slower process
- **Mejor para:** Major campaigns, large budgets, risk-averse brands, flagship products

**Opción B: Flexible Vetting (Streamlined Process)**
- **Descripción:** 30-60 minute vetting, basic checks only, faster approval
- **Ventajas:** Faster execution, can work with more influencers, lower cost
- **Desventajas:** Higher risk, potential for brand safety issues
- **Mejor para:** Small campaigns, low budgets, experimental content, emerging creators

**Recomendación del experto:** Tier your vetting process based on investment and risk:
- **Major partners ($50K+):** Full 3-phase vetting (3-6 hours)
- **Mid-tier partners ($10K-$50K):** Phase 1 + Phase 2 (2-3 hours)
- **Micro/nano partners (<$10K):** Phase 1 only (30-60 minutes)
- **Affiliates:** Basic screening only

## 5. Anti-patrones

### Anti-patrón 1: Skipping Background Checks for "Big" Influencers

**Fuente:** [Brand Safety Mistakes (Becky O., 2023)]
**Qué es:** Assuming large influencers are safe because they're "professional"
**Por qué la gente lo hace:** Star power, FOMO, assumption that size = legitimacy
**Consecuencias:** Major PR disasters when big influencers have past controversies
**Cómo evitarlo:** Vet everyone, regardless of follower count
**Qué hacer en su lugar:** Larger influencers = more scrutiny, not less. They have bigger platforms to cause damage.

---

### Anti-patrón 2: Ignoring Engagement Quality

**Fuente:** [Fake Influencer Detection (Becky O., 2023)]
**Qué es:** Focusing only on follower count, not engagement authenticity
**Por qué la gente lo hace:** Follower count is visible and easy to compare
**Consecuencias:** Wasted budget on influencers with fake/bought followers
**Cómo evitarlo:** Always calculate engagement rate and analyze comment quality
**Qué hacer en su lugar:** Prioritize engagement rate over follower count. 10K highly engaged followers > 100K fake ones.

---

### Anti-patrón 3: Weak Morality Clauses

**Fuente:** [Contract Mistakes (Becky O., 2023)]
**Qué es:** Having generic morality clauses that don't clearly define what's prohibited
**Por qué la gente lo hace:** Want to avoid being "controlling," fear of losing influencer interest
**Consecuencias:** Can't terminate when problems arise, legal ambiguity
**Cómo evitarlo:** Be explicit about what constitutes problematic behavior
**Qué hacer en su lugar:** Detailed morality clause with specific examples of prohibited conduct.
