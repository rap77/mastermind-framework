# FUENTE-159: Community Moderation & Governance — Moderation Experts

## Tesis Central

> **"Great communities don't happen by accident—they're designed and protected through thoughtful governance, consistent moderation, and clear policies. The best moderation happens invisibly, creating an environment where members feel safe to participate."**

---

## 1. Principios Fundamentales

> **P1: Safety Precedes Participation**
- Members won't participate if they don't feel safe. Psychological safety is the foundation of all community engagement.
> *Fuente: [Community Safety Framework (Modoration Experts, 2023)]*
> *Contexto: When designing community guidelines and moderation systems.*

> **P2: Consistency is Critical for Fairness**
- Inconsistent enforcement destroys trust. Rules must be applied uniformly regardless of member status or relationship.
> *Fuente: [Moderation Consistency (Modoration Experts, 2023)]*
> *Contexto: When training moderators and enforcing community policies.*

> **P3: Transparency Builds Trust in Moderation**
- When members understand why moderation decisions are made, they trust the process even when they disagree with outcomes.
> *Fuente: [Transparent Moderation (Modoration Experts, 2023)]*
> *Contexto: When communicating moderation actions and policy enforcement.*

> **P4: Good Communities Design for Bad Actors**
- Assume some people will violate norms. Design systems that minimize harm and enable quick recovery.
> *Fuente: [Anticipatory Governance (Modoration Experts, 2023)]*
> *Contexto: When creating community platforms and policies.*

> **P5: Moderation Scales with Community Growth**
- What works for 100 members breaks at 1,000. What works at 1,000 breaks at 10,000. Moderation must evolve with community.
> *Fuente: [Scalable Moderation (Modoration Experts, 2023)]*
> *Contexto: When planning moderation infrastructure and team scaling.*

## 2. Frameworks y Metodologías

### Framework 1: The Community Governance System

**Fuente:** [Community Governance Blueprint (Modoration Experts, 2023)]
**Propósito:** Create comprehensive governance system for healthy community
**Cuándo usar:** When establishing or overhauling community governance

**Component 1: Community Guidelines (The Rules)**

**Essential Guidelines:**
1. **Respect:** Treat all members with dignity and respect
2. **No Harassment:** Zero tolerance for harassment, hate speech, discrimination
3. **Privacy:** Respect others' privacy and personal information
4. **Spam/Self-Promotion:** Clear limits on self-promotion
5. **Relevance:** Stay on-topic for the community purpose
6. **Copyright:** Respect intellectual property rights
7. **Confidentiality:** Don't share private communications

**Guideline Writing Best Practices:**
- **Clear and Specific:** What exactly is prohibited?
- **Examples:** Include "allowed" and "not allowed" examples
- **Context:** Explain WHY each rule exists
- **Consequences:** What happens if rules are broken?
- **Appeals Process:** How to appeal moderation decisions

**Component 2: Enforcement Policy (The Consequences)**

**Tiered Enforcement Approach:**

**Tier 1: First Offense (Minor)**
- **Action:** Private warning + content removal
- **Communication:** Personal message explaining what violated guidelines
- **Goal:** Education and correction
- **Example:** First time spamming, accidental guideline violation

**Tier 2: Second Offense or Moderate Violation**
- **Action:** Temporary suspension (3-7 days) + formal warning
- **Communication:** Clear explanation of suspension terms
- **Goal:** Time for reflection and behavior change
- **Example:** Repeat minor offense, first moderate offense

**Tier 3: Third Offense or Severe Violation**
- **Action:** Extended suspension (30-90 days) or permanent ban
- **Communication:** Final decision notice with appeal information
- **Goal:** Community protection
- **Example:** Severe harassment, hate speech, repeated violations

**Tier 4: Extreme Violation (Immediate Ban)**
- **Action:** Immediate and permanent ban
- **Communication:** Community notification (without identifying details)
- **Goal:** Immediate community safety
- **Example:** Threats of violence, illegal activity, doxxing

**Component 3: Moderation Team Structure**

**Team Roles:**
- **Head Moderator:** Overall strategy, policy decisions, escalation handler
- **Moderators:** Day-to-day moderation, content review, member communication
- **Auto-Mod:** Bots and automation for spam filtering, keyword blocking
- **Appeals Panel:** Separate team for reviewing contested moderation decisions

**Moderator Ratio Guidelines:**
- **0-1,000 members:** 1-2 moderators (can be volunteer)
- **1,000-10,000 members:** 3-5 moderators (mix of volunteer + paid)
- **10,000-100,000 members:** 5-15 moderators (mostly paid, some volunteer power-users)
- **100,000+ members:** 15+ moderators (dedicated moderation team)

**Component 4: Moderation Workflows**

**Content Review Workflow:**
1. **Flag/Report:** Member or system flags content
2. **Queue:** Content enters moderation queue
3. **Review:** Moderator reviews against guidelines
4. **Decision:** Approve, remove, or escalate
5. **Action:** Execute decision (remove, warn, suspend)
6. **Communication:** Notify member (if applicable)
7. **Documentation:** Log action for future reference
8. **Appeal:** Member can appeal (if applicable)

**Response Time Targets:**
- **Urgent (threats, illegal content):** <1 hour
- **High (harassment, severe violations):** <4 hours
- **Medium (spam, self-promotion):** <24 hours
- **Low (borderline cases):** <48 hours

**Output esperado:** Comprehensive governance system that scales and protects community

### Framework 2: The Conflict Resolution Protocol

**Fuente:** [Community Conflict Resolution (Modoration Experts, 2023)]
**Propósito:** Resolve member conflicts constructively
**Cuándo usar:** When managing disputes between members or with members

**Types of Community Conflicts:**

**Type 1: Interpersonal Disputes**
- **Description:** Two members having personal conflict
- **Example:** Heated argument, personal attacks
- **Resolution Approach:**
  1. **Separate:** Remove public posts, stop immediate conflict
  2. **Private Reach Out:** Contact both parties separately
  3. **Listen:** Understand each perspective without judgment
  4. **Identify Root Cause:** What's really going on?
  5. **Find Common Ground:** Shared interests or community values
  6. **Propose Resolution:** Mutually acceptable path forward
  7. **Follow Up:** Check in after resolution

**Type 2: Policy Disputes**
- **Description:** Members disagreeing with community rules or decisions
- **Example:** "This rule is unfair," "Why was X banned?"
- **Resolution Approach:**
  1. **Acknowledge:** Validate their perspective
  2. **Explain Rationale:** Share the "why" behind the policy
  3. **Listen to Feedback:** There may be valid concerns
  4. **Offer Channel for Change:** How policies can be reviewed/updated
  5. **Stand Firm or Adapt:** Either defend policy or commit to review
  6. **Communicate Decision:** Transparent explanation to community

**Type 3: escalations**
- **Description:** Situations that require leadership intervention
- **Example:** Severe toxicity, legal threats, PR risks
- **Resolution Approach:**
  1. **Assess Severity:** Determine risk level
  2. **Involve Leadership:** Head mod or community manager
  3. **Legal Review:** If legal issues involved
  4. **Decision:** Make decision considering community risk
  5. **Action:** Execute decisively
  6. **Communicate:** Appropriate level of transparency

**Conflict Prevention:**
- **Clear Guidelines:** Prevent misunderstandings
- **Proactive Moderation:** Address issues before they escalate
- **Community Culture:** Reinforce positive behaviors
- **Early Intervention:** Don't let conflicts fester
- **Member Empowerment:** Give members tools to resolve minor conflicts

**Output esperado:** Constructive conflict resolution that strengthens community

### Framework 3: Content Moderation at Scale

**Fuente:** [Scalable Moderation Systems (Modoration Experts, 2023)]
**Propósito:** Build moderation systems that scale with community growth
**Cuándo usar:** When planning moderation infrastructure

**Three-Tier Moderation Model:**

**Tier 1: Automated Moderation (Bots & Algorithms)**

**Handles (80-90% of issues):**
- **Spam Detection:** Keyword filters, pattern matching, ML classifiers
- **Toxic Content:** Hate speech detection, harassment AI
- **NSFW Content:** Image recognition for inappropriate content
- **Link Safety:** Malicious link detection, phishing
- **Rate Limiting:** Prevent spam floods
- **New Member Restrictions:** Limited permissions for new members

**Tools:**
- Spam filters (Akismet, custom filters)
- Toxicity detection (Perspective API, Content Moderation APIs)
- Auto-mod bots (Dyno, MEE6, Carl-bot for Discord)
- Custom automation (Zapier, Make)

**Tier 2: Community Moderation (Volunteer/Paid Mods)**

**Handles (10-18% of issues):**
- **Context-Dependent Cases:** Things AI can't judge
- **Member Disputes:** Interpersonal conflicts
- **Gray Areas:** Boundary violations
- **Appeals:** Reviewing automated decisions
- **Quality Control:** Improving automated systems

**Human Advantages:**
- Cultural context understanding
- Nuance and sarcasm detection
- Relationship-based judgment
- Flexibility for edge cases

**Tier 3: Professional Moderation (Paid Team)**

**Handles (2-5% of issues):**
- **Severe Violations:** Legal issues, threats, criminal activity
- **Sensitive Content:** CSAM, extreme harassment, doxxing
- **High-Stakes Decisions:** Permanent bans, PR-impacting situations
- **Policy Development:** Creating and updating guidelines
- **Escalations:** When community mods can't resolve

**Professional Requirements:**
- Training in trauma-informed moderation
- Legal knowledge (platform liability, reporting requirements)
- Crisis management experience
- De-escalation skills
- Cultural competence

**Scaling Triggers:**
- **0-1K members:** Tier 1 only, light automation
- **1K-10K members:** Tier 1 + 2-3 human moderators
- **10K-100K members:** Tier 1 + 5-10 moderators + escalation path
- **100K+ members:** All tiers, dedicated moderation team

**Output esperado:** Scalable moderation system that maintains quality as community grows

## 3. Modelos Mentales

### Modelo Mental 1: The Trust Bank Account

**El modelo:** Every moderation action either deposits or withdraws from the community's trust in the moderation team. Fair, consistent actions deposit trust. Unfair, inconsistent actions withdraw trust. When the account hits zero, community leaves.
**Por qué funciona:** Explains why inconsistent moderation destroys communities faster than no moderation
**Cuándo usarlo:** When evaluating moderation health and community trust in leadership

---

### Modelo Mental 2: The Enforcement Feedback Loop

**El modelo:** Moderation decisions create member behavior. Strict moderation → cautious behavior → less engagement but more safety. Lenient moderation → risky behavior → more engagement but more conflict. Finding the balance is key.
**Por qué funciona:** Explains why over-moderation and under-moderation both kill communities
**Cuándo usarlo:** When calibrating moderation strictness for community culture

---

## 4. Criterios de Decisión

### Trade-off: Automated vs Human Moderation

**Fuente:** [Moderation Strategy (Modoration Experts, 2023)]

**Automated-First:**
- **Ventajas:** Fast, consistent, scalable, cost-effective
- **Desventajas:** False positives, lacks nuance, can feel robotic
- **Mejor para:** Large communities, spam, clear-cut violations

**Human-First:**
- **Ventajas:** Nuanced, contextual, builds relationships
- **Desventajas:** Slow, expensive, inconsistent, doesn't scale
- **Mejor para:** Small communities, complex issues, high-stakes decisions

**Recomendación del experto:** Hybrid approach. Automation handles 80-90% of clear cases (spam, obvious violations). Human moderators handle the 10-20% requiring judgment. Use automation to scale, humans for quality.

## 5. Anti-patrones

### Anti-patrón 1: Public Shaming for Rule Violations

**Fuente:** [Moderation Mistakes (Modoration Experts, 2023)]
**Qué es:** Publicly calling out members who break rules
**Por qué la gente lo hace:** Wants to make example, set deterrent
**Consecuencias:** Creates fear culture, humiliates member, community feels unsafe
**Cómo evitarlo:** Correct privately, communicate consequences (not details) publicly
**Qué hacer en su lugar:** "A member was banned for violating our harassment policy. We don't tolerate harassment." (No names, no details)

---

### Anti-patrón 2: Inconsistent Enforcement Based on Member Status

**Qué es:** Enforcing rules loosely for popular/powerful members, strictly for others
**Consecuencias:** Destroys trust, creates hierarchy, community resents moderation
**Qué hacer en su lugar:** Rules apply equally to everyone, including moderators and founders

---

### Anti-patrón 3: Reactive-Only Moderation

**Qué es:** Only moderating when problems arise, no proactive systems
**Consecuencias:** Always playing catch-up, conflicts escalate before intervention
**Qué hacer en su lugar:** Proactive moderation—establish clear guidelines before problems, address issues early, be visible presence
