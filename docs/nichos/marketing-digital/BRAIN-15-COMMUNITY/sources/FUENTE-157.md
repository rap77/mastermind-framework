# FUENTE-157: Customer Advocacy — Influitive Team

## Tesis Central

> **"Your happiest customers want to advocate for you—they just need the structure, incentives, and opportunities to do it at scale. An advocacy program systematizes word-of-mouth and turns community members into a marketing channel."**

---

## 1. Principios Fundamentales

> **P1: Advocacy is Natural, Just Not Systematic**
- Your best customers already recommend you. An advocacy program makes this advocacy systematic, measurable, and scalable.
> *Fuente: [Advocate Marketing Framework (Influitive, 2023)]*
> *Contexto: When designing advocate programs from community.*

> **P2: Different Members Want Different Advocacy Roles**
- Some want to write reviews, some want to speak at events, some want to refer friends. Offer multiple advocacy paths.
> *Fuente: [Advocate Personas (Influitive, 2023)]*
> *Contexto: When designing advocate program activities and tiers.*

> **P3: Recognition Matters More Than Rewards**
- While rewards help, the best advocates are motivated by recognition, access, and relationship with the brand.
> *Fuente: [Advocate Motivation (Influitive, 2023)]*
> *Contexto: When structuring advocate incentives and recognition.*

> **P4: Make Advocacy Frictionless**
- The easier it is to advocate, the more they will. One-click reviews, pre-written social posts, automated referrals.
> *Fuente: [Advocate Experience Design (Influitive, 2023)]*
> *Contexto: When designing advocate program technology and processes.*

> **P5: Close the Loop with Advocates**
- Show advocates the impact of their advocacy. Did their referral convert? Did their case study get views? Close the feedback loop.
> *Fuente: [Advocate Engagement (Influitive, 2023)]*
> *Contexto: When maintaining advocate motivation and retention.*

## 2. Frameworks y Metodologías

### Framework 1: The Community-to-Advocate Pipeline

**Fuente:** [Advocate Program Design (Influitive, 2023)]
**Propósito:** Turn community members into systematic brand advocates
**Cuándo usar:** When building advocate marketing program from community

**Stage 1: Identify Potential Advocates**
- **Source:** Community members who are highly engaged
- **Signals:** High participation, positive sentiment, helping others, creating content
- **NPS:** Members who rate 9-10
- **Behavior:** Members who already refer naturally, defend brand, provide feedback
- **Screening:** Must be genuine users, positive experience, willing to advocate

**Stage 2: Invite and Onboard**
- **Personal Invitation:** "You've been such a great community member—would you be interested in our advocate program?"
- **Exclusive Access:** Frame as VIP/exclusive opportunity
- **Welcome Kit:** Explain program, benefits, expectations, first mission
- **First Quick Win:** Easy first activity (share a post, write a review)

**Stage 3: Activate and Engage**
- **Challenges:** Regular advocacy activities with points/rewards
- **Content Library:** Pre-approved social posts, graphics, messaging
- **Leaderboards:** Recognize top advocates
- **Personal Goals:** Set personalized advocacy targets

**Stage 4: Recognize and Reward**
- **Points System:** Earn points for activities, redeem for rewards
- **Tiers:** Bronze → Silver → Gold → Platinum advocates
- **Recognition:** Featured on website, speaking opportunities, VIP events
- **Rewards:** Gift cards, products, experiences, cash (where allowed)

**Stage 5: Evolve to Partnership**
- **Top Advocates:** Become true partners (co-creation, consulting, equity)
- **Advisory Roles:** Customer advisory board, beta testing, product feedback
- **Thought Leadership:** Speaking at events, co-authoring content
- **Ambassador Status:** Official brand ambassador role

**Pipeline Metrics:**
- **Identification Rate:** % of community members identified as potential advocates (target: 10-20%)
- **Acceptance Rate:** % who accept advocate invitation (target: 30-50%)
- **Activation Rate:** % who complete first advocacy activity (target: 70%+)
- **Retention Rate:** % active after 6 months (target: 60%+)

**Output esperado:** Systematic pipeline converting community members into measurable advocates

### Framework 2: The Advocacy Activity Menu

**Fuente:** [Advocate Activities (Influitive, 2023)]
**Propósito:** Design diverse advocacy activities for different advocate preferences
**Cuándo usar:** When planning advocate program challenges and activities

**Activity Categories:**

**1. Content Advocacy (10-50 points)**
- Write a product review (G2, Capterra, Google)
- Share brand post on social media
- Comment on blog post
- Create and share UGC
- Write guest blog post

**2. Referral Advocacy (50-200 points)**
- Refer a friend (with tracking link)
- Introduce to prospect (B2B)
- Share referral link with network
- Nominate for awards/recognition

**3. Event Advocacy (100-500 points)**
- Speak at customer event
- Host customer roundtable
- Participate in case study video
- Join panel discussion
- Lead user group

**4. Feedback Advocacy (20-100 points)**
- Complete product survey
- Beta test new feature
- Provide detailed feedback
- Join customer advisory board
- Participate in user research

**5. Community Advocacy (10-50 points)**
- Welcome new community members
- Answer questions in forum
- Mentor new users
- Host community event
- Create community resource

**Points System Design:**
- **Low effort, high value:** Higher points (referrals, case studies)
- **High effort, low value:** Lower points (surveys, social shares)
- **Balance:** Mix of easy and hard activities so all advocates can participate
- **Bonuses:** Double points on priority activities, monthly bonuses

**Reward Tiers:**
- **Bronze (0-500 points):** Digital rewards, gift cards ($25-50)
- **Silver (500-2000 points):** Premium rewards, products ($100-250)
- **Gold (2000-5000 points):** Experiences, events, VIP access ($250-500)
- **Platinum (5000+ points):** Exclusive access, co-creation, equity consideration ($500+ value)

**Output esperado:** Varied activities keeping advocates engaged with options for all commitment levels

## 3. Modelos Mentales

### Modelo Mental 1: The Advocate Value Pyramid

**Fuente:** [Advocate Economics (Influitive, 2023)]
**El modelo:** Most advocates provide low value (reviews, referrals), few provide high value (case studies, speaking). But both are needed. Design program for the pyramid—lots of easy activities for base, special activities for top.
**Por qué funciona:** Explains why 20% of advocates drive 80% of value, and why that's OK
**Cuándo usarlo:** When designing advocate program economics and resource allocation

## 4. Criterios de Decisión

### Trade-off: Open Advocate Program vs Invite-Only

**Fuente:** [Advocate Program Strategy (Influitive, 2023)]

**Opción A: Open Advocate Program**
- **Ventajas:** Scale, anyone can join, self-service
- **Desventajas:** Lower quality, more management, variable results
- **Mejor para:** Consumer brands, high-volume products

**Opción B: Invite-Only Advocate Program**
- **Ventajas:** Quality control, curated, higher engagement
- **Desventajas:** Limited scale, more manual, slower growth
- **Mejor para:** B2B, high-ticket, complex products

**Recomendación del experto:** Start invite-only for quality control. Once program is proven, open to broader community while maintaining VIP tier for top performers.

---

## 5. Anti-patrones

### Anti-patrón 1: All Rewards, No Recognition

**Qué es:** Advocate programs that only offer transactional rewards (cash, gift cards)
**Consecuencias:** Mercenary behavior, advocates disappear when rewards stop
**Qué hacer en su lugar:** Balance rewards with recognition, access, relationship. Top advocates motivated more by recognition than rewards.
