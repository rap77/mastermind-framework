# FUENTE-M1-007: Purple Cow & Tribes — Seth Godin

## Tesis Central

> En un world saturado de marketing noise, las safe choices son las riskiest. Being "good" es el fast track to being invisible. The only way to win is to be remarkable — literalmente, worth making a remark about. Marketing is no longer about ads y campaigns, es about creating products y ideas that people CHOOSE to talk about.

---

## 1. Principios Fundamentales

> **P1: Marketing es un producto, no un department**
> El mejor marketing del mundo no puede salvar un producto mediocre. The product itself IS the marketing. Si tu producto no es remarkable, no amount de marketing spend va a fix it.
> *Fuente: Purple Cow, Chapter 1 (Godin, 2003)*
> *Contexto: Evaluar cualquier feature o product decision: "¿Es esto remarkable? Would someone talk about it?"*

> **P2: La meta no es satisfacer al customer, es delight the customer**
* Satisfacer es table stakes. Delighting es lo que genera word-of-mouth. Los customers satisfechos no refieren; los customers delighted se vuelven evangelistas.*
> *Fuente: "Satisfied vs. Delighted" (Godin Blog, 2015)*
> *Contexto: Medir NPS no solo satisfaction scores. Track referrals y word-of-mouth como delight metric.*

> **P3: El riesgo más grande es ser safe (brown cow)**
* En el past, ser safe era safe. Now, being safe is risky porque sos invisible. Los winners son los que toman risks calculados para standout.*
> *Fuente: Purple Cow, Chapter 3 (Godin, 2003)*
> *Contexto: Evaluar cada initiative: "¿Es esto safe pero invisible o risky pero remarkable?" Elegir remarkable.*

> **P4: El marketing es about spreading ideas, not selling products**
* People don't spread products, they spread IDEAS. If your product embodies an idea worth spreading, it will spread. If it's just "stuff", nobody talks about it.*
> *Fuente: "Idea diffusion" (Godin Blog, 2012)*
> *Contexto: What's the IDEA behind your product? If you can't articulate it, you can't spread it.*

> **P5: Tribes > Mass market**
* Mass marketing is dead porque no hay mass market anymore. El future es tribes: comunidades de people con shared interests y values. Marketing es about leading tribes, no broadcasting to everyone.*
> *Fuente: Tribes, Chapter 2 (Godin, 2008)*
> *Contexto: Instead de "who is our target market?", ask "who is our tribe?" What brings them together?*

## 2. Frameworks y Metodologías

### Framework 1: The Purple Cow Test

**Fuente:** Purple Cow (Godin, 2003)
**Propósito:** Determinar si un producto/marketing initiative es remarkable.
**Cuándo usar:** Al evaluar features, campaigns, o product launches.

**The 10 Questions:**

1. ¿Es el producto/service tan diferente que es notable a primera vista?
2. ¿Es el producto/service diseñado para un niche específico (no para todos)?
3. ¿Es el pricing/offer memorable o es "commoditized"?
4. ¿El producto/service rompe una convención de la industria?
5. ¿Hay un story worth telling sobre el producto?
6. ¿El producto/service genera conversación?
7. ¿El producto/service es el único en su clase (o significantly different)?
8. ¿El producto/service es risky en forma calculated (no stupid risk)?
9. ¿El producto/service target a early adopters y innovadores (no mass market)?
10. ¿El producto/service crea FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out)?

**Scoring:** 8+ = Purple Cow (remarkable), 5-7 = Notable but not remarkable, <5 = Brown Cow (invisible)

**Key Insight:** The goal no es to answer yes to ALL, but to deliberately design products that score high on remarkable dimensions.

---

### Framework 2: The Tribe Leadership Model

**Fuente:** Tribes (Godin, 2008)
**Propósito:** How to build and lead a tribe (community) around your brand.
**Cuándo usar:** Al desarrollar community strategy o brand evangelism.

**The 3 Elements of Tribe Building:**

1. **Narrative (The Story)**
   - A shared story that unites the tribe
   - What do we believe?
   - What are we against?
   - Example: Apple tribe believes in "creative rebellion against the status quo"

2. **Connection (The Glue)**
   - Ways for tribe members to connect
   - Shared experiences, events, communication channels
   - The tribe must be able to talk to each other
   - Example: Apple events, Apple Stores, Apple user groups

3. **Leadership (The Heretic)**
   - Someone to challenge the status quo
   - Leaders create movements, not products
   - Leaders have faith in the tribe even when others doubt
   - Example: Steve Jobs as heretic challenging the PC establishment

**Key Insight:** Tribes need leaders who are heretics (challenge status quo), not managers (maintain status quo).

---

### Framework 3: The Idea Diffusion Model

**Fuente:** "Unleashing the Ideavirus" (Godin, 2001) + Blog
**Propósito:** How ideas spread from person to person.
**Cuándo usar:** Al diseñar viral marketing o word-of-mouth campaigns.

**The 5 Factors of Idea Spread:**

1. **Sneezers** (Influencers who spread ideas)
   - Who are the influencers en tu market?
   - What makes them want to share your idea?
   - Sneezers need social currency for sharing

2. **Hive** (The target group)
   - Who cares about this idea?
   - The hive must be tight-knit enough to spread ideas internally
   - Target hives, not mass markets

3. **Velocity** (How fast it spreads)
   - Frictionless sharing mechanisms
   - Built-in virality
   - Shareability: easy to explain, easy to share

4. **Vector** (The medium of spread)
   - How does the idea travel? (Word of mouth, social media, email?)
   - Optimize for the vector
   - Make the vector the message

5. **Smoothness** (Ease of adoption)
   - Low barrier to adoption
   - No "learning curve" pain
   - Fits into existing behaviors

**Output esperado:** Una estrategia para que tu idea se difunda orgánicamente.

## 3. Modelos Mentales

| Modelo | Descripción | Aplicación Práctica |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Brown Cow vs. Purple Cow** | Brown cow = common, invisible, safe. Purple cow = remarkable, visible, risky but memorable. En un field of brown cows, purple cow stands out. | Audit tu producto y competitors: Are you brown cow in a field of brown cows? Find your purple. |
| **The Dip** | El "dip" es el hard part antes del breakthrough. Most people quit when things get hard. Winners push through the dip. Los losers start, los winners terminan. | Cuando el growth plateau, no es failure, es the dip. Push through o pivot. Don't quit por plateau. |
| Permission Marketing | En lugar de interruptir customers (TV ads, cold calls), get permission to communicate con ellos. Email lists, followers, subscribers = permission assets. | Build permission assets (email list, social followers). They are more valuable than one-time customers. |
| **The Monk and the Riff** | Monks = deep experts en una cosa. Riffs = people who combine and remix existing ideas. Both are valuable, but riffing es faster than monkhood. | Decide: Are you a monk (expert, slow, deep) or riffraff (combiner, fast, broad)? Be intentional about it. |
| **The Turtle and the Hare** | En marketing, slow y steady gana. Brands que build slowly con remarkable products ganan a brands que spend big on ads but are unremarkable. | Focus on product remarkability, not ad spend. Remarkable products need less marketing spend. |

## 4. Criterios de Decisión

### Trade-off: Mass Marketing vs. Tribe Marketing

**Fuente:** Tribes (Godin, 2008)

**Opción A: Mass Marketing (broadcast to everyone)**
- **Ventajas:** Reach potencialmente ilimitado, familiar, proven in 20th century
- **Desventajas:** Expensive, low response rates, declining effectiveness, "invisible" in saturated markets
- **Mejor para:** Products con true mass appeal (Coca-Cola type products) — rare

**Opción B: Tribe Marketing (lead a community)**
- **Ventajas:** Higher engagement, word-of-mouth, lower cost, loyalty
- **Desventajas:** Smaller scale, requiere tiempo y nurturing, needs authentic leadership
- **Mejor para:** Products con strong point of view, brands que stand for something

**Recomendación de Godin:** Opción B para 99% de businesses. "Mass marketing is dead. Tribes are the future."

---

### Trade-off: Safe (Brown Cow) vs. Risky (Purple Cow)

**Fuente:** Purple Cow (Godin, 2003)

**Opción A: Safe Products (meets expectations, incremental improvement)**
- **Ventajas:** Easy to sell to existing customers, predictable, low risk
- **Desventajas:** Invisible, vulnerable to remarkable competitors, low word-of-mouth
- **Mejor para:** Late-stage companies en stable markets (cash cow optimization)

**Opción B: Remarkable Products (exceeds expectations, different)**
- **Ventajas:** Word-of-mouth, pricing power, customer loyalty, differentiation
- **Desventajas:** Riskier, harder to sell initially, may alienate some customers
- **Mejor para:** Startups, growth-stage companies, any business that needs to stand out

**Recomendación de Godin:** Opción B. "Safe is risky. Remarkable is safe."

## 5. Anti-patrones

### Anti-patrón 1: "Vamos a mejorar el producto basado en customer feedback"

**Fuente:** "The Customer is Not Always Right" (Godin Blog, 2014)
**Qué es:** Hacer todo lo que los customers piden.
**Por qué la gente lo hace:** "Customer-centricity" sounds good, customers explicitly tell you what they want.
**Consecuencias:** Products designed by committee, boring (customers ask for more of the same), no purple cow.
**Cómo evitarlo:** Listen to customers, but don't do everything they say. Customers know what they DON'T want, not what they WANT. Innovators figure out what customers want before customers know it themselves.
**Qué hacer en su lugar:** Use customer feedback to identify pain points, but design remarkable solutions yourself. Customers didn't ask for iPhone, but once they saw it, they wanted it.
