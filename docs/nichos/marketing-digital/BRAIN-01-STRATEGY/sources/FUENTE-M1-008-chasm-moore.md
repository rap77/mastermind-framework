# FUENTE-M1-008: Crossing the Chasm — Geoffrey Moore

## Tesis Central

> Existe un massive gap (chasm) entre early adopters de tecnología (visionaries, early adopters) y el mainstream market (early majority, late majority). Early adopters buy technology for technology's sake. Mainstream customers buy solutions para problemas reales. El chasm mata a la mayoría de las startups porque no logran adaptar su messaging, sales, y product strategy al mainstream market.

---

## 1. Principios Fundamentales

> **P1: El chasm existe y es real**
> Entre ~15% de market penetration (early adopters) y ~50% (mainstream) hay un gap where companies get STUCK. Early adopters toleran bugs y complexity. Mainstream customers no. El chasm es where most tech companies die.
> *Fuente: Crossing the Chasm, Chapter 1 (Moore, 1991)*
> *Contexto: Si tu producto es loved por early adopters but ignored por mainstream, estás en the chasm.*

> **P2: Early adopters y mainstream customers tienen diferente psychographics**
* Early adopters (visionaries, enthusiasts) want to be FIRST. They buy potential, not proof. Mainstream customers (pragmatists) want PROVEN solutions. They buy assurance, not potential.*
> *Fuente: Crossing the Chasm, Chapter 2 (Moore, 1991)*
> *Contexto: No puedes vender a pragmatists con el mismo messaging que usaste para visionaries.*

> **P3: El chasm se cruza through DOMINANCE de un niche**
* Para llegar al mainstream, no puedes attack the entire market. Tenés que DOMINAR un niche específico first, become the standard in that niche, y then expand to adjacent niches. This is called the "bowling alley" strategy.*
> *Fuente: Crossing the Chasm, Chapter 6 (Moore, 1991)*
> *Contexto: Choose one beachhead market, win it completely, then expand. No spread resources thin across multiple markets.*

> **P4: The whole product > The core product**
* Early adopters buy the core product (the technology). Mainstream customers buy the "whole product" = core product + ecosystem + support + training + services + complementary products. If you only have core product, you can't cross the chasm.*
> *Fuente: Crossing the Chasm, Chapter 8 (Moore, 1991)*
> *Contexto: Assess your whole product gaps. What's missing for mainstream adoption? Partnerships? Services? Support?*

> **P5: Post-chasm strategy is different from pre-chasm strategy**
* Pre-chasm: Sell to visionaries, emphasize features, be first. Post-chasm: Sell to pragmatists, emphasize solutions, be safe. The messaging, sales, pricing, and distribution all need to change.*
> *Fuente: Crossing the Chasm, Chapter 10 (Moore, 1991)*
> *Contexto: When you cross the chasm, you need to "grow up" — act like an established company, not a startup.*

## 2. Frameworks y Metodologías

### Framework 1: Technology Adoption Lifecycle

**Fuente:** Crossing the Chasm, Chapter 2 (Moore, 1991)
**Propósito:** Entender los 5 segmentos de adopción de tecnología.
**Cuándo usar:** Al diseñar go-to-market strategy o analizar por qué el growth se estancó.

**The 5 Segments:**

| Segment | % of Market | Psychographics | Buying Motivation |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Innovators** | 2.5% | Tech-obsessed, risk-takers | First access to new tech |
| **Early Adopters (Visionaries)** | 13.5% | Future-focused, want competitive edge | Breakthrough advantage |
| **Early Majority (Pragmatists)** | 34% | Practical, risk-averse | Proven solutions, references |
| **Late Majority (Conservatives)** | 34% | Skeptical, price-sensitive | Established standard |
| **Laggards** | 16% | Anti-technology, forced adopters | Only when necessary |

**Key Insight:** The chasm is between Early Adopters (13.5%) and Early Majority (34%). Visionaries ≠ Pragmatists.

---

### Framework 2: The Bowling Alley Strategy

**Fuente:** Crossing the Chasm, Chapter 6 (Moore, 1991)
**Propósito:** Un método para cruzar el chasm dominando nichos secuencialmente.
**Cuándo usar:** Para planificar la expansión de un producto technology.

**The Bowling Alley Process:**

1. **Pin (Target Beachhead Market)**
   - Choose ONE niche where you can DOMINATE
   - The niche must have: Urgent problem, No alternatives, Budget to pay
   - Goal: 100% market share in this niche
   - Example: Salesforce targeting "sales teams at tech startups"

2. **Strike (Dominar el Niche)**
   - Create a whole product for THIS niche
   - Build case studies, references, testimonials
   - Become "the standard" for this niche
   - Goal: Defensible position in this niche

3. **Expand (Bowling Alley)**
   - Use success in pin to expand to adjacent niches
   - Each adjacent niche learns from previous niche success
   - Momentum builds through reference accounts
   - Goal: Expand to 2-3 more niches

4. **Channel (Reach Mainstream)**
   - Once you have 2-3 niches dominated, expand to mainstream
   - Now you have proof points, references, case studies
   - Mainstream customers feel safe (you're "established")
   - Goal: Cross the chasm to mainstream

**Key Insight:** You cannot cross the chasm by attacking the entire mainstream market directly. You must win nichos first.

---

### Framework 3: The Whole Product Model

**Fuente:** Crossing the Chasm, Chapter 8 (Moore, 1991)
**Propósito:** Entender qué necesita el mainstream customer para adoptar.
**Cuándo usar:** Para evaluar product readiness para mainstream adoption.

**The Whole Product Components:**

1. **Core Product** = What you ship
2. **Expected Features** = Features customers expect (table stakes)
3. **Augmented Features** = Support, training, implementation
4. **Potential Features** = Future roadmap items
5. **Ecosystem Products** = Partners, integrations, marketplace
6. **Services** = Consulting, customization, managed services

**Pre-Chasm (Early Adopters):**
- Buy core product only
- Tolerate missing expected features
- Want potential features (future roadmap)
- Don't care much about ecosystem/services

**Post-Chasm (Mainstream):**
- Buy WHOLE PRODUCT (all 6 components)
- REQUIRE expected features (if missing, no adoption)
- Want proven features (no potential)
- Require ecosystem/services for confidence

**Key Insight:** To cross the chasm, you must upgrade from core product to whole product BEFORE mainstream adoption.

## 3. Modelos Mentales

| Modelo | Descripción | Aplicación Práctica |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| **The Chasm** | The gap between early adopters (13.5% penetration) and mainstream (50%+). Most companies die here. | Track your market penetration. If stuck at ~15%, you're in the chasm. Need bowling alley strategy. |
| **The Tornado** | After crossing the chasm, if you succeed, you enter "tornado" phase — hypergrowth where market standard emerges. This is where FOMO drives adoption. | Post-chasm, prepare for tornado. Scale fast, capture market share, establish standard. |
| **The Gorilla Game** | In the tornado phase, one company becomes "gorilla" (dominant with 50%+ share). Others become chimps/chimps. Be the gorilla or get niche. | Aim for gorilla position in your category. If you can't, be a dominant chimp in a niche. |
| **Market Maturity Curve** | Markets evolve: Early market → Chasm → Bowling alley → Tornado → Main street → Decline. Each phase requires different strategy. | Diagnose your market maturity. Apply wrong strategy for the phase = death. |
| **The D-Day Strategy** | Crossing the chasm is like D-Day invasion. Concentrate ALL resources on ONE beachhead. No diversification until beachhead won. | Allocate resources: 100% on ONE niche until dominated. Then expand. No half-measures. |

## 4. Criterios de Decisión

### Trade-off: Focus on Early Adopters vs. Cross the Chasm

**Fuente:** Crossing the Chasm, Chapter 3 (Moore, 1991)

**Opción A: Stay in Early Adopter Market**
- **Ventajas:** Easier customers, more forgiving, willing to pay for potential, less price-sensitive
- **Desventajas:** Limited market size, revenue plateau, vulnerable to mainstream-focused competitors
- **Mejor para:** Niche products, luxury/high-end technology, products that can command premium prices from early adopters

**Opción B: Cross to Mainstream (Chasm Strategy)**
- **Ventajas:** Massive market size, revenue growth, competitive defensibility
- **Desventajas:** Must change product/messaging/sales, higher competition, price pressure
- **Mejor para:** Ambitious startups, products with mainstream appeal, VC-backed growth companies

**Recomendación de Moore:** Opción B para growth companies. "If you want to be a big company, you must cross the chasm. There is no other path."

## 5. Anti-patrones

### Anti-patrón 1: "Vamos a vender a todo el mercado"

**Fuente:** Crossing the Chasm, Chapter 5 (Moore, 1991)
**Qué es:** Ignorar los segmentos y tratar de vender a todos.
**Por qué la gente lo hace:** Fear de missing out on customers, pressure to grow fast, sounds logical.
**Consecuencias:** Messaging is too generic (nobody feels targeted), sales team chases wrong leads, stuck in chasm forever.
**Cómo evitarlo:** Choose ONE beachhead niche. Target EVERYTHING to that niche until dominated.
**Qué hacer en su lugar:** Bowling alley. Win one niche 100%, then expand to adjacent niche using references from first win.

## Notas Adicionales

Geoffrey Moore's framework is ESPECIALLY relevant for SaaS y technology companies. The "chasm" explains why so many B2B SaaS companies get stuck at $1-5M ARR.

**Key Moore insight:** "Early adopters buy breakthrough. Mainstream buys assurance. Your job after the chasm is to provide assurance through references, case studies, and established presence."

**Application en marketing:**
- Pre-chasm messaging: "New! Innovative! First!" (appeals to visionaries)
- Post-chasm messaging: "Proven! Safe! #1 choice!" (appeals to pragmatists)
- The change in messaging is radical, not incremental.
