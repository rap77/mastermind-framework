# FUENTE-M6-009: El Libro de Google Ads — David Carrero

## Tesis Central

> Google Ads es una herramienta poderosa pero compleja. El modelo "Google Ads Mastery" significa: dominar Google Ads requiere entender 4 layers simultáneamente: (1) Technical setup (tracking, pixels, measurement), (2) Campaign structure (cómo organizar accounts/campaigns), (3) Creative optimization (ads que funcionan), (4) Bid management (pujar estratégicamente). La mayoría de advertisers domina 1-2 layers y falla en los otros. El experto en Google Ads es competente en los 4 layers simultáneamente. El error más común: enfocarse solo en bids (cuánto pagar) sin optimizar structure, creative, y tracking. Pero la realidad: poor structure + poor creative + poor tracking + good bids = poor performance. Excellent structure + excellent creative + excellent tracking + average bids = excellent performance.

## Key Frameworks

**Framework 1: Los 4 Layers de Google Ads Mastery**

**Layer 1: Technical Setup (Foundation)**
- Google Analytics 4 integration
- Google Tag installation
- Conversion tracking (purchase, lead, signup)
- Enhanced conversions (customer data upload)
- Audience builder (remarketing lists)

**Layer 2: Account Structure (Organization)**
- Campaigns organized by goal (awareness, consideration, conversion)
- Ad groups by theme/product/service
- Keywords grouped by intent
- Negative keywords implemented
- Ad extensions configured

**Layer 3: Creative Strategy (Messaging)**
- Responsive search ads (RSAs)
- Headlines (15-30 characters)
- Descriptions (90 characters)
- Display URL path
- Ad extensions (sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets)

**Layer 4: Bid Strategy (Investment)**
- Manual CPC: For control
- Enhanced CPC: Semi-automated
- Maximize clicks: For traffic
- Maximize conversions: For results
- Target CPA/ROAS: For efficiency

**Framework 2: Responsive Search Ads (RSAs) Best Practices**

**What are RSAs?**
- Google automatically combines headlines/descriptions
- Machine learning optimiza para performance
- Replaces expanded text ads (ETAs)

**Best Practices:**
- **Headlines:** Provide 10-15 options
  - Include brand name (2-3 headlines)
  - Include keywords (5-7 headlines)
  - Include benefits/offers (3-5 headlines)
  - Include CTAs (2-3 headlines)

- **Descriptions:** Provide 4-5 options
  - Unique value propositions
  - Social proof
  - Special offers
  - Call-to-actions

- **Pinning:** Pin key headlines to position 1, 2, or 3
  - Brand name in position 1
  - Key benefit in position 2
  - CTA in position 3
  - Let Google rotate others

**Framework 3: Google Analytics 4 + Google Ads Integration**

**Why GA4 matters:**
- Cookie-less future (privacy changes)
- Server-side tracking ready
- Better cross-device attribution
- Free forever (no 360 tiers)

**Setup Steps:**
1. Create GA4 property
2. Install GA4 tag on site
3. Link GA4 to Google Ads
4. Import conversions from GA4 to Google Ads
5. Enable enhanced tracking
6. Configure audiences in GA4
7. Use GA4 audiences for remarketing in Google Ads

**Key Insight:** GA4 is the future of Google tracking. Migrate from UA immediately.

## Decisiones Clave

**DC-1: ¿Responsive Search Ads o Expanded Text Ads?**

**RSAs (Recommended):**
- More ad variations (Google tests automatically)
- Better performance (usually)
- Easier management (one ad instead of multiple)
- Future-proof (Google pushing RSAs)

**ETAs (Limited use):**
- More control over exact ad copy
- Useful for A/B testing specific messages
- Being phased out by Google

**Recomendation:** Use RSAs for 90% of campaigns. Use ETAs only for specific testing.

**DC-2: ¿Cuándo conversion tracking upgrades?**

**Standard tracking (Minimum):**
- Purchase/sale completed
- Lead form submitted
- Phone call clicked
- Email signup

**Enhanced tracking (Recommended):**
- Enhanced conversions (upload customer data)
- Offline conversions (in-store sales)
- View-through conversions (brand impact)
- Cross-device conversions (user journey)

**Advanced tracking (Best performance):**
- Value-based bidding (LTV optimization)
- Customer match (CRM-based targeting)
- Seasonality adjustments (anticipate demand)

**DC-3: ¿Remarketing strategy?**

**Standard remarketing (Basic):**
- Show ads to all site visitors
- Simple messaging
- Lower bids (cold audience)

**Dynamic remarketing (Advanced):**
- Show products user viewed
- Personalized ads (product, price, image)
- Higher bids (warm audience)
- Better performance

**Remarketing lists to build:**
- All visitors (broad list)
- Product page visitors (specific)
- Cart abandoners (high intent)
- Past purchasers (upsell/cross-sell)

## Anti-patrones

- ❌ ANTI-PATTERN 1: Not using GA4 (still using Universal Analytics - deprecated)
- ❌ ANTI-PATTERN 2: Not setting up enhanced conversions (missing performance boost)
- ❌ ANTI-PATTERN 3: Ignoring ad extensions (10-20% CTR improvement available)
- ❌ ANTI-PATTERN 4: Same ad copy for all ad groups (lack relevance)
- ❌ ANTI-PATTERN 5: Not using audience targeting (missing remarketing opportunities)
