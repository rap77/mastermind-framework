# FUENTE-M6-002: Advanced Google Ads — Brad Geddes

## Tesis Central

> El éxito en Google Ads avanzado requiere scientific testing, no intuitive optimization. El modelo "Testing-Driven Optimization" significa: every change debe ser un experimento con hypothesis, test, measurement, y learning. La mayoría de advertisers hacen cambios basados en gut feeling → inconsistent results. Los advanced advertisers hacen cambios basados en data → consistent improvement. Los 3 pillars de advanced SEM: (1) Campaign structure facilitates learning, (2) Ad testing systematizes improvement, (3) Bidding strategy aligns con business goals. Si no estás midiendo everything con statistical significance, estás guessing, no optimizing.

## 1. Principios Fundamentales

> **P1: SEM es un laboratory, not a casino**
- Testea hypotheses, no guesses
- Mide statistical significance (95% confidence minimum)
- Documenta todo para aprender
> *Fuente: "Scientific SEM" (Geddes, 2020)*

> **P2: Campaign structure dictates learning speed**
- Poor structure = confused algorithm = slow optimization
- Good structure = clear signal = fast learning
- Structure before bidding, always
> *Fuente: "Structure First" (Geddes, 2020)*

> **P3: Ad testing debe ser systematic, not random**
- Test ONE variable at a time
- Run tests por 2-4 weeks minimum (statistical significance)
- Scale winners, kill losers promptly
> *Fuente: "Ad Testing System" (Geddes, 2020)*

> **P4: El bidding strategy debe match business maturity**
- New campaigns: Manual CPC for control
- Growing campaigns: Maximize conversions
- Mature campaigns: Target CPA/ROAS
- Wrong strategy = suboptimal results
> *Fuente: "Bidding Maturity Model" (Geddes, 2020)*

> **P5: Attribution modeling changes everything**
- Last-click attribution undervalues top-funnel keywords
- Multi-touch attribution reveals true keyword value
- Data-driven attribution is future, but requires conversion volume
> *Fuente: "Attribution Reality" (Geddes, 2020)*

## 2. Frameworks y Metodologías

### Framework 1: Advanced Campaign Architecture

**Fuente:** "Campaign Architecture" (Geddes, 2020)
**Propósito:** Estructurar campaigns para maximum learning y performance.
**Cuándo usar:** Al diseñar campaigns de Google Ads desde cero.

**The 3-Tier Structure:**

**Tier 1: Account Level Organization**

**Structure by Business Goal:**
- Campaign Type: Search, Display, Shopping, Video
- Business Unit: Product lines, service categories
- Geography: Countries, regions, cities
- Language: English, Spanish, etc.

**Best Practice:**
- Separate Search vs Display campaigns (different user intent)
- Separate high-priority vs low-priority products/services
- Separate by geography if bidding differs significantly

**Tier 2: Campaign Level Structure**

**Structure by Theme + Intent:**

**Campaign Structure A: By Product/Service**
```
Campaign: CRM Software
  └── Ad Group: CRM for Small Business
  └── Ad Group: CRM for Enterprise
  └── Ad Group: CRM Features (pricing, demo, etc.)
```

**Campaign Structure B: By Funnel Stage**
```
Campaign: Awareness (Informational keywords)
Campaign: Consideration (Comparison keywords)
Campaign: Conversion (Transactional keywords)
```

**Campaign Structure C: By Match Type (Advanced)**
```
Campaign: Brand Terms (Exact match only)
Campaign: High-Value Keywords (Exact/Phrase)
Campaign: Broad Match Testing (Broad match only)
```

**Key Rule:** Match type segmentation requires significant budget (minimum $1000-2000/month). For smaller budgets, use Product/Service structure.

**Tier 3: Ad Group Level Structure**

**Ad Group Size:**
- Ideal: 10-20 keywords per ad group
- Maximum: 30 keywords (beyond = diluted messaging)
- Minimum: 5 keywords (too small = not enough data)

**Ad Group Organization:**
- Keywords should be semantically related
- Ads should speak to all keywords in group
- Landing pages should be relevant to entire group

**Example of Good Ad Group:**

**Ad Group: CRM for Small Business**

**Keywords:**
- "crm software for small business"
- "best crm small business"
- "small business crm pricing"
- "affordable crm for small business"
- "crm tools for small business"

**Ads:**
- All ads speak to small business CRM needs
- Mention "small business" in headlines/descriptions
- Landing page specifically for small business CRM

### Framework 2: Scientific Ad Testing System

**Fuente:** "Ad Testing Methodology" (Geddes, 2020)
**Propósito:** Testear ads sistemáticamente con statistical significance.
**Cuándo usar:** Continuous improvement de campaigns.

**The Testing Process:**

**Step 1: Define Hypothesis**

**Bad Hypothesis:**
"I think changing the headline will improve CTR."

**Good Hypothesis:**
"Adding a number to the headline will increase CTR by 15% because numbers capture attention better than text-only headlines."

**Components:**
- Variable: Headline number inclusion
- Expected impact: +15% CTR
- Rationale: Numbers capture attention

**Step 2: Design Test**

**Test Variable: ONE element only**
- Headline: Test with/without number
- Description: Test long vs short
- Display URL: Test with/without keyword
- CTA: Test different call-to-actions

**Control: Keep everything else constant**

**Example:**
```
Control Ad (A):
Title: Best CRM Software for Small Business
Title: Manage Customer Relationships

Test Ad (B):
Title: #1 CRM Software for Small Business
Title: Manage Customer Relationships

(Only difference: "#1" vs "Best" in headline)
```

**Step 3: Run Test**

**Duration: Until statistical significance**
- Minimum: 2 weeks
- Recommended: 3-4 weeks
- Must achieve: 95% confidence level
- Must achieve: Minimum 100 clicks per ad

**Step 4: Analyze Results**

**Metrics to Compare:**
- CTR (Click-Through Rate)
- CPC (Cost Per Click)
- Conversion Rate
- CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)

**Statistical Significance Calculator:**
- Use A/B testing calculator
- Input: Impressions, clicks, conversions for each ad
- Output: Confidence level, winner determination

**Step 5: Implement Learning**

**If Test wins (95%+ confidence):**
- Make winner the new control
- Kill loser
- Plan next test

**If Test is inconclusive (<95% confidence):**
- Run test longer
- Or modify hypothesis and retest

**Key Rule:** Never make changes based on insufficient data. 100 clicks minimum per ad is the absolute minimum for CTR testing. 300-500 clicks preferred.

### Framework 3: Bidding Strategy Selection Framework

**Fuente:** "Bidding Strategy Matrix" (Geddes, 2020)
**Propósito:** Elegir la bidding strategy correcta.
**Cuándo usar:** Al configurar o cambiar bidding strategies.

**The Bidding Maturity Model:**

**Stage 1: New Campaign (0-30 conversions/month)**

**Recommended: Manual CPC**

**Why:**
- Full control over bids
- Learn what works before automating
- Avoid wasted spend from poor automation

**How to set:**
- Start with Google's suggested bid
- Adjust up/down based on performance
- Monitor daily initially, then weekly

**Stage 2: Growing Campaign (30-50 conversions/month)**

**Recommended: Maximize Clicks with bid cap**

**Why:**
- Google optimizes for clicks within your budget
- Bid cap prevents overspending
- Balance between automation and control

**How to set:**
- Set bid cap = maximum CPA you can afford
- Let Google optimize within that constraint
- Monitor conversion rate and CPA

**Stage 3: Mature Campaign (50+ conversions/month)**

**Recommended: Target CPA or Maximize Conversions**

**Why:**
- Sufficient conversion data for algorithm
- Google optimizes directly for your goal
- Best performance at scale

**Target CPA:**
- Set target CPA = your acceptable acquisition cost
- Google adjusts bids automatically to hit target
- Best when conversion value is consistent

**Maximize Conversions:**
- Let Google spend entire budget for max conversions
- No CPA cap (Google determines optimal)
- Best when growth > efficiency priority

**Stage 4: Advanced Campaign (100+ conversions/month + value tracking)**

**Recommended: Target ROAS or Maximize Conversion Value**

**Why:**
- Optimizes for revenue/value, not just conversions
- Requires conversion value tracking
- Best for e-commerce or businesses with varying deal sizes

**Target ROAS:**
- Set target ROAS (e.g., 400% = $4 revenue per $1 spend)
- Google optimizes bids to hit revenue target
- Best when average order value is consistent

**Maximize Conversion Value:**
- Let Google optimize for maximum revenue
- No ROAS cap (Google determines optimal)
- Best when maximizing revenue > efficiency priority

**Key Rule:** Move to next stage ONLY when current stage is mastered. Jumping ahead (e.g., Target CPA with 10 conversions/month) will result in poor performance.

## 3. Modelos Mentales

### Modelo Mental 1: The Quality Score Feedback Loop

**Fuente:** "Quality Score Dynamics" (Geddes, 2020)
**El modelo:** Quality Score es un feedback loop, not static.

**The Loop:**

**Initial State:**
- New ad → Low historical CTR
- Quality Score: 5/10 (starting point)
- CPC: Average

**Action: Improve ad relevance**
- Better headline, more compelling copy
- CTR increases from 2% to 5%

**Result:**
- Expected CTR component increases
- Quality Score improves from 5/10 to 8/10
- CPC decreases 20-30%

**Secondary Effect:**
- Lower CPC → higher ad position
- Higher ad position → higher CTR
- Higher CTR → Quality Score improves further

**Final State:**
- Quality Score: 9-10/10
- CPC: 30-50% lower than initial
- Ad position: Top 3
- Campaign performance: Significantly improved

**Key Insight:** Quality Score improvement creates a virtuous cycle. Small improvements in CTR lead to Quality Score gains, which lead to lower CPC, which leads to better positions, which lead to even higher CTR.

**Action:** Focus on CTR improvement first. Every 1% increase in CTR can lead to 0.5-1 point Quality Score improvement, which reduces CPC by 5-10%.

### Modelo Mental 2: The Keyword Value Spectrum

**Fuente:** "Keyword Valuation" (Geddes, 2020)
**El modelo:** No todas las keywords tienen el mismo valor.

**The Spectrum:**

**High-Value Keywords (Top 20% driving 80% of profit):**

**Characteristics:**
- High conversion rate
- High average order value
- Low CPC (good Quality Score)
- Consistent performance

**Strategy:**
- Bid aggressively (top of page bid)
- Monitor daily
- Protect from competitors
- Expand with similar keywords

**Mid-Value Keywords (Driving some profit):**

**Characteristics:**
- Moderate conversion rate
- Moderate average order value
- Moderate CPC
- Stable but not spectacular

**Strategy:**
- Bid moderately (first page bid)
- Monitor weekly
- Optimize gradually
- Test to improve performance

**Low-Value Keywords (Not profitable):**

**Characteristics:**
- Low conversion rate
- Low average order value
- High CPC (poor Quality Score)
- Inconsistent or declining performance

**Strategy:**
- Reduce bids (below first page)
- Add negative keywords to refine
- Pause if no improvement after 30 days
- Delete and focus budget elsewhere

**Key Insight:** Most advertisers bid uniformly across all keywords. Smart advertisers bid based on keyword value.

**Action:** Calculate profit per keyword (revenue - cost - CPC × clicks). Allocate budget proportionally to profit.

### Modelo Mental 3: The Seasonality Adjustment Model

**Fuente:** "Seasonal SEM" (Geddes, 2020)
**El modelo:** Search demand fluctuates por seasonality.

**The Seasonality Curve:**

**High Season (Demand Peak):**
- Search volume increases 2-5x
- Competition increases (CPCs rise)
- Conversion rate may increase or decrease

**Strategy:**
- Increase budgets to capture demand
- Accept higher CPCs (still profitable due to higher volume)
- Focus on top-performing keywords
- Use ad scheduling to avoid peak CPC times

**Low Season (Demand Trough):**
- Search volume decreases 50-70%
- Competition decreases (CPCs drop)
- Conversion rate typically stable or slightly lower

**Strategy:**
- Maintain presence but reduce budgets
- Take advantage of lower CPCs
- Test new keywords/ads (lower risk)
- Focus on brand keywords (higher conversion rate)

**Shoulder Season (Transition):**
- Search volume increasing or decreasing
- Competition adjusting
- Conversion rate normalizing

**Strategy:**
- Gradual budget adjustments
- Monitor competitors (new entrants or exits)
- Adjust bids based on performance data
- Prepare for high/low season

**Key Insight:** Most advertisers keep budgets flat year-round. Smart advertisers adjust budgets and bids based on seasonality.

**Action:** Analyze historical data to identify seasonal patterns. Adjust budgets and bids 2-4 weeks before seasonality hits.

## 4. Criterios de Decisión

### DC-1: ¿Cuándo split test ads vs rotate evenly?

**Split test (optimize):**
- When: Campaign has sufficient data (30+ conversions/month)
- Purpose: Let Google's algorithm find winner
- Best for: Ongoing optimization
- Action: Set ad rotation to "optimize"

**Rotate evenly:**
- When: Testing new ads, collecting data
- Purpose: Equal impression distribution
- Best for: Statistical testing
- Action: Set ad rotation to "rotate indefinitely" or "rotate evenly"

**Recommendation:** Use rotate evenly for testing, optimize for delivery.

**Source:** "Ad Rotation" (Geddes, 2020)

### DC-2: ¿Cuándo usar enhanced CPC vs manual bidding?

**Enhanced CPC:**
- When: Have some data but want control
- Purpose: Auto-adjust bids based on conversion likelihood
- Best for: Growing campaigns (30-50 conversions/month)
- Trade-off: Give up some control for automation

**Manual CPC:**
- When: New campaigns or need full control
- Purpose: Set exact bids for each keyword
- Best for: New campaigns, testing phase
- Trade-off: Time-consuming, requires expertise

**Recommendation:** Start with manual CPC. Move to enhanced CPC once you have baseline data.

**Source:** "Bidding Control" (Geddes, 2020)

### DC-3: ¿Cuándo usar display ads with search campaigns?

**Use search + display together:**
- When: Sufficient budget ($2000+/month)
- Strategy: Separate campaigns (not combined)
- Purpose: Search captures intent, display generates awareness
- Best for: Established brands with budget diversity

**Don't combine:**
- When: Limited budget
- Why: Different user intent, different metrics
- Risk: Display waste reduces budget for profitable search
- Recommendation: Search first, display after search is profitable

**Source:** "Search vs Display" (Geddes, 2020)

## 5. Anti-patrones

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 1: Changing bids daily**
- **El problema:** Ajustar bids todos los días based en daily fluctuations
- **El costo:** Never letting algorithm stabilize, inconsistent performance
- **la solución:** Changes weekly maximum, based on 7-day trends not daily noise
> *Fuente: "Daily Bid Changes" (Geddes, 2020)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 2: Not using ad extensions**
- **El problema:** Ignoring sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets
- **El costo:** Lower ad rank, higher CPC, lower CTR
- **la solución:** Always use all relevant ad extensions (increase ad real estate, improve CTR)
> *Fuente: "Ad Extension Neglect" (Geddes, 2020)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 3: Bidding on competitor keywords**
- **El problema:** Pujar por keywords de competidores (ej: "HubSpot CRM")
- **El costo:** Very high CPC, low conversion rate (users searching competitor brand)
- **la solución:** Focus on category keywords (ej: "best CRM software"), not competitor brands
> *Fuente: "Competitor Bidding" (Geddes, 2020)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 4: Not using negative keywords**
- **El problema:** Showing ads for irrelevant searches
- **El costo:** Wasted spend on low-quality clicks
- **la solución:** Add negative keywords weekly (free, cheap, demo, trial, download, etc.)
> *Fuente: "Negative Keywords Gap" (Geddes, 2020)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 5: Focusing on vanity metrics**
- **El problema:** Obsesionarse con position #1, impression share
- **el costo:** Overbidding for position #1 when position 2-4 is more profitable
- **la solución:** Focus on profitability (ROAS/CPA), not position
> *Fuente: "Vanity Metrics" (Geddes, 2020)*
