# FUENTE-M6-010: SEM Avanzado — Sergio Redondo

## Tesis Central

> El SEM a escala es un juego diferente. El modelo "Scale or Die" significa: hay 3 tipos de accounts en Google Ads: (1) Accounts que nunca scalean (stuck at $1-3k/month), (2) Accounts que scalean pero se rompen (reach $10k/month then profitability collapses), (3) Accounts que scalean sustainably (reach $50k+/month manteniendo profitability). La diferencia entre (2) y (3): systematic approach a scaling. Los 3 pillars de sustainable scaling: (1) Data infrastructure (perfect tracking, no broken funnels), (2) Automated optimization (scripts, smart bidding, rules), (3) Creative testing pipeline (constant new creatives). Sin data infrastructure, scaling = amplifying mistakes. Sin automation, scaling = human bottleneck. Sin creative pipeline, scaling = ad fatigue y collapse. Sustainable scaling requires los 3 pillars working together.

## Key Frameworks

**Framework 1: Data Infrastructure Foundation**

**Must-have antes de scaling:**

**1. Perfect Conversion Tracking**
- All conversion actions tracked (purchase, lead, signup, add to cart, etc.)
- No duplicate conversions
- Conversion value tracked (para ROAS bidding)
- Offline conversions uploaded (if applicable)
- Enhanced conversions enabled (customer data match)

**2. Google Analytics 4 Fully Configured**
- Custom dimensions (user segments)
- Custom metrics (business-specific KPIs)
- Events tracked (all interactions)
- Funnel analysis (where users drop off)
- Attribution modeling (data-driven preferred)

**3. CRM Integration**
- Lead to customer tracking (LTV calculation)
- Offline sales imported back to Google Ads
- Customer match lists (CRM-based remarketing)
- Value-based bidding (optimize por LTV, no solo conversions)

**4. Data Quality Score**
- Tracking accuracy: 95%+ (audited monthly)
- Conversion lag: <3 days (fast feedback loop)
- Data consistency: Same metrics across platforms (GA4, Google Ads, CRM)

**Framework 2: Scaling Methodology**

**The 3 Scaling Levers:**

**Lever 1: Horizontal Scaling (Expansion)**
- Add new keywords (similar to top performers)
- Add new ad groups (new themes/angles)
- Add new campaigns (new markets/geo)
- Add new platforms (Microsoft Ads, Amazon Ads)

**Lever 2: Vertical Scaling (Budget Increase)**
- Increase budget on winners (+20% every 3 days)
- Expand geo-targeting (new cities/regions)
- Expand device targeting (add mobile, tablet)
- Expand dayparting (add more hours/days)

**Lever 3: Efficiency Scaling (Optimization)**
- Improve Quality Score (reduce CPC 20-50%)
- Improve conversion rate (increase CPA efficiency)
- Improve AOV (more revenue per conversion)
- Improve LTV (more valuable customers)

**Key Rule:** Scale in order: Efficiency first → Horizontal → Vertical. Don't scale budget until efficiency is optimized.

**Framework 3: Automation Architecture**

**Automation Tiers:**

**Tier 1: Rules-Based Automation**
- Google Ads automated rules
- Scripts basicos
- Purpose: Routine maintenance, alerts
- Frequency: Daily execution
- Human oversight: Weekly review

**Tier 2: Algorithmic Automation**
- Smart bidding (Target CPA/ROAS)
- Smart creatives (RSAs, responsive display ads)
- Purpose: Optimization a escala
- Frequency: Continuous (24/7)
- Human oversight: Bi-weekly review

**Tier 3: Custom Automation**
- Custom scripts (Google Ads scripts API)
- Third-party tools (Optmyzr, Kenshoo, Marin)
- Purpose: Advanced optimization, custom workflows
- Frequency: Continuous + scheduled tasks
- Human oversight: Monthly strategic review

**Key Insight:** Automation is not "set and forget". Human oversight is critical at all tiers. The more automation, the more sophisticated the oversight needed.

## Decisiones Clave

**DC-1: ¿Cuándo escalar vertical vs horizontalmente?**

**Escalá verticalmente (increase budget) cuando:**
- CPA es estable y below target por 30+ days
- Quality Score es 7+ (room to bid higher sin CPC explosion)
- Campaign no está budget-constrained (impression share < 80%)
- LTV > 3x CPA (economics allows scaling)

**Escalá horizontalmente (new campaigns/keywords) cuando:**
- Current campaigns are budget-constrained (can't spend more efficiently)
- CPA es good pero volume is limited
- Have proven creative/landing page formula
- New markets/geo para expandir

**DC-2: ¿Cuándo pasar a smart bidding?**

**Permaneced con manual/Enhanced CPC cuando:**
- <50 conversions/month (insufficient data)
- Conversion tracking changed recently
- Seasonal extreme (smart bidding reacts slowly)
- Need tight control sobre bids

**Pasá a smart bidding cuando:**
- 50+ conversions/month (minimum data threshold)
- Conversion tracking stable y accurate
- Seasonality moderada o predecible
- Goal es efficiency over control

**DC-3: ¿Cuándo third-party tools vs native Google Ads?**

**Google Ads native (suficiente para):**
- Spend < $10k/month
- 1-2 accounts
- Basic optimization needs

**Third-party tools (necesario para):**
- Spend > $10k/month
- 5+ accounts or complex structures
- Need advanced features:
  - Cross-account management
  - Advanced bidding algorithms
  - Creative testing automation
  - Advanced reporting/analytics
  - Competitor intelligence

**Recommendation:** Start native. Add tools when complexity exceeds native capabilities.

## Modelos Mentales

**Modelo Mental 1: The Scaling Death Spiral**

**Qué es:** Accounts que scalean pero colapsan.

**El Cycle:**
1. Campaigns profitability at $5k/month
2. Increase budget to $10k/month
3. Performance stays good por 1-2 months
4. Ad fatigue kicks in (same creatives overused)
5. Quality Score declines (ad relevance drops)
6. CPA increases 50-100%
7. Profitability collapses
8. Campaigns paused or scaled back

**Cómo evitar:**
- Don't scale creative (same ads at higher spend = faster fatigue)
- Don't scale efficiency (maintain Quality Score while scaling)
- Creative pipeline (new ads constantly in testing)
- Efficiency monitoring (watch CPA/QS trends during scaling)

**Modelo Mental 2: The Data Infrastructure Flywheel**

**Qué es:** Better data → better decisions → better performance → more budget → better data infrastructure.

**El Cycle:**
1. Invest en tracking (GA4, CAPI, CRM integration)
2. Better data visibility (see true attribution)
3. Better bidding decisions (smart bidding has better inputs)
4. Better performance (lower CPA, higher ROAS)
5. More budget allocated (channel wins more budget)
6. More investment en data infrastructure (flywheel continues)

**Key Insight:** Most advertisers underinvest en data infrastructure. They scale campaigns on broken tracking = scaling mistakes.

**Action:** Antes de scaling spend, scale data quality.

## Anti-patrones

- ❌ ANTI-PATTERN 1: Scaling budget sin scaling creative (ad fatigue inevitable)
- ❌ ANTI-PATTERN 2: Moving to smart bidding too early (insufficient conversion data)
- ❌ ANTI-PATTERN 3: Not auditing tracking regularly (broken tracking = bad bidding decisions)
- ❌ ANTI-PATTERN 4: Scaling by multiplying budget (doubling budget overnight breaks campaigns)
- ❌ ANTI-PATTERN 5: Treating all campaigns equally (top 20% deserve 80% of attention)
