# FUENTE-M6-001: Ultimate Guide to Google Ads — Perry Marshall

## Tesis Central

> Google Ads no es sobre outbidding competitors, es sobre outsmarting them. El modelo "5-Word Ad Formula" significa: Google Search ads son tiny classifieds donde cada palabra cuenta. La clave del éxito está en: (1) Elegir las keywords correctas (high intent, low competition), (2) Escribir ads que capturen attention y generen clicks relevantes, (3) Enviar tráfico a landing pages optimizadas que conviertan, (4) Bidding estratégicamente basado en data, no intuition. Quality Score es el factor más importante: alto Quality Score = lower CPC = better ROI. La mayoría de advertisers focusean en CPC, pero los focuseados en ROI ganan.

## 1. Principios Fundamentales

> **P1: Google Search es intent-based advertising**
- Users buscan activamente soluciones
- Search ads capture intent, social ads generate interest
- El timing es perfecto: user busca → tu ad aparece
> *Fuente: "Search Intent" (Marshall, 2021)*

> **P2: Quality Score es el great equalizer**
- Quality Score 1/10 paga 5-10x más que Quality Score 10/10
- High Quality Score = lower CPC + higher ad position
- Focus on relevance, not just bidding
> *Fuente: "Quality Score Dominance" (Marshall, 2021)*

> **P3: El 80/20 rule aplica brutalmente en keywords**
- 20% de keywords generan 80% de conversions
- Tu job: encontrar ese 20% y matar el resto
- Broad match initially, then refine
> *Fuente: "Keyword 80/20" (Marshall, 2021)*

> **P4: Landing page es más importante que el ad**
- Great ad + bad landing page = wasted money
- Bad ad + great landing page = some conversions
- Optimizar landing page = 2-5x conversion rate improvement
> *Fuente: "Landing Page First" (Marshall, 2020)*

> **P5: SEM es un auction, pero no siempre highest bidder gana**
- Ad Rank = Bid × Quality Score + Ad Extensions
- Podés tener bid más alto pero aparecer abajo si Quality Score es bajo
- Quality Score beats bid en mayoría de casos
> *Fuente: "Ad Rank Reality" (Marshall, 2021)*

## 2. Frameworks y Metodologías

### Framework 1: El 5-Word Ad Formula

**Fuente:** "5-Word Ad Formula" (Marshall, 2021)
**Propósito:** Escribir Google Search ads que conviertan.
**Cuándo usar:** Al crear Google Search ads.

**The 5-Word Structure:**

**Word 1-2: The Hook (Capture Attention)**

Your goal: Detener el scroll y capturar attention.

**Effective Hooks:**
- **Questions:** "Need [solution]?"
- **Numbers:** "50% Off", "24 Hour Service"
- **Power Words:** "Free", "New", "Proven", "Guaranteed"
- **Problem statement:** "Tired of [problem]?"

**Examples:**
- "Need More Sales?"
- "Lose 10kg Fast"
- "Free Trial Today"

**Word 3-4: The Promise (Deliver Value)**

Your goal: Communicar el benefit principal.

**Effective Promises:**
- **Outcome:** "Double Your Leads"
- **Speed:** "Results in 24 Hours"
- **Savings:** "Save 50% on [service]"
- **Solution:** "Fix [problem] Fast"

**Examples:**
- "Generate More Leads"
- "Learn Guitar Fast"
- "Best SEO Tools"

**Word 5: The CTA (Call to Action)**

Your goal: Decidir la próxima acción.

**Effective CTAs:**
- **Direct:** "Buy Now", "Sign Up", "Call Today"
- **Soft:** "Learn More", "Get Info", "See How"
- **Urgency:** "Limited Time", "Today Only"

**Examples:**
- "Start Free Trial"
- "Get Your Quote"
- "Book Now"

**The Full Ad Example:**

```
Title: Need More Sales? (Hook)
Title: Generate More Leads (Promise)
Title: Start Free Trial (CTA)

Description: Proven lead generation system.
Get 50+ qualified leads monthly.
Start your free 14-day trial today.
```

**Key Rule:** Cada palabra debe tener un propósito. No fillers.

### Framework 2: The Keyword Strategy Matrix

**Fuente:** "Keyword Strategy" (Marshall, 2021)
**Propósito:** Elegir keywords correctas para SEM.
**Cuándo usar:** Al configurar campaigns de Google Ads.

**The Keyword Matrix:**

**Tier 1: High Intent + Low Competition (SWEET SPOT)**

**Characteristics:**
- Long-tail keywords (3-5 words)
- Clear purchase intent (buy, best, price, review)
- Lower search volume (100-1000/month)
- Lower CPC ($1-5)

**Examples:**
- "best crm for small business 2024"
- "buy running shoes online free shipping"
- "seo services pricing monthly"

**Strategy:**
- Bid aggressively (these convert well)
- Use exact match
- Monitor closely, adjust bids

**Tier 2: High Intent + High Competition (COMPETITIVE)**

**Characteristics:**
- Medium-tail keywords (2-3 words)
- Clear purchase intent
- High search volume (1000-10000/month)
- Higher CPC ($5-20)

**Examples:**
- "crm software"
- "running shoes"
- "seo services"

**Strategy:**
- Bid carefully (high CPC can kill ROI)
- Focus on Quality Score to lower CPC
- Use ad scheduling to avoid peak competition times

**Tier 3: Low Intent + Low Competition (AWARENESS)**

**Characteristics:**
- Informational keywords
- Research intent (how, what, why)
- Low CPC ($0.50-2)

**Examples:**
- "what is crm software"
- "how to choose running shoes"
- "seo basics guide"

**Strategy:**
- Use for top-funnel content marketing
- Bid conservatively
- Consider SEO instead of PPC (long-term)

**Tier 4: Low Intent + High Competition (AVOID)**

**Characteristics:**
- Short, generic keywords
- Unclear intent
- Very high CPC ($20-50+)
- Low conversion rate

**Examples:**
- "software"
- "shoes"
- "marketing"

**Strategy:**
- AVOID or bid very conservatively
- Focus budget on Tier 1 and 2
- Use negative keywords to filter

**Key Rule:** Focus 70% of budget on Tier 1 (High Intent + Low Competition). These generate best ROI.

### Framework 3: The Landing Page Optimization System

**Fuente:** "Landing Page System" (Marshall, 2020)
**Propósito:** Crear landing pages que conviertan tráfico de SEM.
**Cuándo usar:** Al diseñar landing pages para Google Ads campaigns.

**The 5-Element Landing Page:**

**Element 1: Headline Match (Critical)**

Your headline MUST match the keyword/search intent.

**Example:**
- Keyword: "best crm for small business"
- Headline: "The Best CRM for Small Businesses in 2024"

**Why it works:**
- User knows they're in the right place
- Reduces bounce rate
- Increases Quality Score

**Element 2: Clear Value Proposition**

Communicate value immediately.

**Framework: "For [target audience], [product] helps [benefit] without [pain point]."**

**Example:**
"For small businesses, CRM Pro helps manage customer relationships without the complexity of enterprise software."

**Element 3: Social Proof**

Build trust with proof elements.

**Types:**
- **Testimonials:** "CRM Pro helped us increase sales by 40%"
- **Case studies:** "How Company X went from 0 to 1000 customers"
- **Statistics:** "Trusted by 10,000+ businesses"
- **Logos:** Client logos, certifications

**Element 4: Single CTA (Call to Action)**

One clear action, not multiple.

**Effective CTAs:**
- "Start Free Trial"
- "Get Your Free Demo"
- "Download the Guide"
- "Book Your Consultation"

**Best practices:**
- Above the fold (visible without scrolling)
- Contrasting color (stands out)
- Action-oriented language
- No multiple competing CTAs

**Element 5: Mobile Optimization**

50-70% of search traffic is mobile.

**Mobile requirements:**
- Fast loading (<3 seconds)
- Easy to read (16px+ font)
- Simple form (fewer fields)
- Click-to-call button
- Sticky CTA button

**Key Rule:** Match landing page to keyword, not to ad. If user searches "best crm for small business", landing page should speak to small business CRM needs specifically.

## 3. Modelos Mentales

### Modelo Mental 1: The Quality Score Multiplier

**Fuente:** "Quality Score Economics" (Marshall, 2021)
**El modelo:** Quality Score actúa como multiplier en tu economics.

**The Math:**

**Scenario A: Low Quality Score (3/10)**
- Your bid: $5.00
- Quality Score: 3/10
- Actual CPC: $7.50 (paying 50% premium)
- Ad position: 5-7 (lower visibility)

**Scenario B: High Quality Score (10/10)**
- Your bid: $5.00
- Quality Score: 10/10
- Actual CPC: $2.50 (paying 50% discount)
- Ad position: 1-3 (top visibility)

**Key Insight:** High Quality Score payer MEJOR por less, y gets BETTER position.

**Components of Quality Score:**
1. **Expected CTR:** Does your ad get clicks?
2. **Ad Relevance:** Is your ad relevant to keyword?
3. **Landing Page Experience:** Is your landing page relevant and useful?

**Action:** Focus on improving Quality Score components. Every point improvement = 10-20% CPC reduction.

### Modelo Mental 2: The SEM Funnel

**Fuente:** "SEM Funnel Dynamics" (Marshall, 2020)
**El modelo:** Search captures users at different funnel stages.

**The SEM Funnel:**

**Stage 1: Awareness (Informational Keywords)**

**Keywords:** "what is", "how to", "why", "guide"
**Intent:** Researching, learning
**Objective:** Capture email/lead
**Offer:** Free guide, webinar, educational content

**Stage 2: Consideration (Comparison Keywords)**

**Keywords:** "best", "vs", "review", "comparison"
**Intent:** Comparing options
**Objective:** Position your product as best
**Offer:** Comparison chart, case study, free trial

**Stage 3: Decision (Transactional Keywords)**

**Keywords:** "buy", "price", "cheap", "discount", "deal"
**Intent:** Ready to purchase
**Objective:** Close the sale
**Offer:** Discount, bonus, urgency

**Key Insight:** Different keywords = different stages = different offers. Don't send all keywords to the same landing page with the same offer.

**Strategy:** Create separate campaigns/ad groups for each funnel stage with tailored messaging and offers.

### Modelo Mental 3: The Bid Management Matrix

**Fuente:** "Bid Strategy" (Marshall, 2021)
**El modelo:** Different keywords require different bid strategies.

**The Bid Matrix:**

**High-Value Keywords (Top 20% driving 80% of conversions):**
- **Bid strategy:** Maximize clicks or Target CPA
- **Bid level:** Aggressive (20-30% above suggested)
- **Monitoring:** Daily
- **Goal:** Maximum visibility for top performers

**Medium-Value Keywords (Driving some conversions):**
- **Bid strategy:** Maximize clicks
- **Bid level:** Moderate (at suggested bid)
- **Monitoring:** Weekly
- **Goal:** Steady performance

**Low-Value Keywords (No conversions):**
- **Bid strategy:** Manual CPC with low bids
- **Bid level:** Conservative (50% below suggested)
- **Monitoring:** Monthly
- **Goal:** Experimentation, kill if no improvement

**Key Insight:** Don't use the same bid strategy for all keywords. Customize based on performance data.

## 4. Criterios de Decisión

### DC-1: ¿Cuándo usar exact match vs phrase match vs broad match?

**Exact Match ([keyword]):**
- **When:** Keyword has proven conversion data
- **Goal:** High precision, control
- **CPC:** Higher (but more targeted)
- **Best for:** Top performing keywords

**Phrase Match ("keyword"):**
- **When:** Testing new keywords or moderate intent
- **Goal:** Balance between reach and precision
- **CPC:** Medium
- **Best for:** Most campaigns initially

**Broad Match (keyword):**
- **When:** Discovering new keywords
- **Goal:** Maximum reach, data gathering
- **CPC:** Lower (but less targeted)
- **Best for:** Initial research phase only

**Source:** "Match Type Strategy" (Marshall, 2021)

### DC-2: ¿Cuándo manual bidding vs automated bidding?

**Manual CPC:**
- **When:** Starting new campaigns, low data
- **Advantage:** Full control, learning
- **Disadvantage:** Time-consuming, requires expertise
- **Best for:** New campaigns, testing phase

**Automated (Maximize Clicks/Conversions):**
- **When:** Campaigns have 30+ conversions/month
- **Advantage:** Google's AI optimizes 24/7
- **Disadvantage:** Less control, black box
- **Best for:** Established campaigns with conversion data

**Target CPA/ROAS:**
- **When:** Campaigns have 50+ conversions/month
- **Advantage:** Optimizes for business goals
- **Disadvantage:** Requires accurate conversion tracking
- **Best for:** Scaling profitable campaigns

**Source:** "Bidding Strategy" (Marshall, 2020)

### DC-3: ¿Cuándo aumentar bids vs mejorar Quality Score?

**Aumentar bids cuando:**
- Quality Score is already high (7-10/10)
- Keyword is proven winner
- Want immediate visibility boost
- Have budget to absorb higher CPC

**Mejorar Quality Score cuando:**
- Quality Score is low (1-6/10)
- Paying too much per click (CPC too high)
- Ad position is low despite high bids
- Long-term campaign health

**Recommendation:** Always prioritize Quality Score improvement over bid increases. Better Quality Score = lower CPC permanently.

**Source:** "Bid vs Quality Score" (Marshall, 2021)

## 5. Anti-patrones

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 1: Bidding on generic keywords**
- **El problema:** "software", "shoes", "services" = too broad, low intent
- **El costo:** High CPC, low conversion rate, negative ROI
- **la solución:** Focus on long-tail, high-intent keywords (3+ words)
> *Fuente: "Generic Keywords Mistake" (Marshall, 2021)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 2: Sending all traffic to homepage**
- **El problema:** User searches "running shoes", lands on generic homepage
- **El costo:** High bounce rate, low conversion, poor Quality Score
- **la solución:** Send traffic to dedicated landing pages matching search intent
> *Fuente: "Homepage Mistake" (Marshall, 2020)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 3: Ignoring Quality Score**
- **El problema:** Focusing only on CPC and position
- **El costo:** Paying 2-5x more per click than necessary
- **la solución:** Optimize for Quality Score first (CTR, relevance, landing page)
> *Fuente: "Quality Score Neglect" (Marshall, 2021)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 4: Not using negative keywords**
- **El problema:** Showing ads for irrelevant searches
- **El costo:** Wasted spend on low-intent clicks
- **la solución:** Add negative keywords weekly (free, cheap, demo, etc.)
> *Fuente: "Negative Keywords Gap" (Marshall, 2020)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 5: Setting and forgetting campaigns**
- **El problema:** Launch campaign and never optimize
- **El costo:** Performance degrades over time, budget waste
- **la solución:** Weekly optimization: bids, keywords, ads, negative keywords
> *Fuente: "Set and Forget" (Marshall, 2021)*
