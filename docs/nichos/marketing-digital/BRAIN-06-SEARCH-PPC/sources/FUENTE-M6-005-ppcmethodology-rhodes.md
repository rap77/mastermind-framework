# FUENTE-M6-005: Pay-Per-Click Search Marketing — Mike Rhodes

## Tesis Central

> SEM efficiency comes from systems, not heroics. El modelo "One-Hour-a-Day PPC Management" significa: con los systems correctos, podés gestionar campaigns de $50k/month spend en solo 1 hour por día. La mayoría de advertisers pasan hours ajustando bids manualmente sin impact. Los systems-driven advertisers usan automation, scripts, y standardized workflows para get results better con less time. Los 3 pillars: (1) Standardized processes (misma checklists, mismo reporting), (2) Automation para tareas repetitivas, (3) Deep analysis en key metrics (no vanity metrics). El secret: no optimices todo. Optimizá lo que importa (20% de keywords/ads que drive 80% of results). Ignore el resto.

## Key Frameworks

**The PPC Management Workflow:**

**Daily (15 min):**
- Check budget pacing (on track?)
- Review emergency alerts (spike in CPA, drop in conversions)
- Approve/reject automated recommendations

**Weekly (30 min):**
- Performance review (top/bottom performers)
- Bid adjustments (based on 7-day trends)
- Negative keyword additions
- Ad testing analysis

**Monthly (15 min):**
- Strategic deep-dive (trends, seasonality)
- Budget allocation adjustments
- New campaigns/experiments launch
- Competitor analysis

**Key Insight:** Most advertisers spend 80% time on daily micromanagement. Reverse it: 20% daily, 60% weekly analysis, 20% monthly strategy.

**Quality Score Optimization Framework:**

**Component 1: Expected CTR**
- Ad position matters (top positions = higher CTR)
- Ad copy matters (compelling ads = higher CTR)
- Keyword relevance matters (specific ads = higher CTR)

**Component 2: Ad Relevance**
- Include keyword in ad copy (DKI helps)
- Align ad message with search intent
- Use ad extensions for more relevance signals

**Component 3: Landing Page Experience**
- Load time: <3 seconds ideal
- Mobile-friendly: responsive design
- Relevance: Page mentions keyword/product
- Content: Valuable, original, relevant

**The QS Improvement Checklist:**
- [ ] Audit current QS scores by keyword
- [ ] Identify low-QS keywords (<5/10)
- [ ] For each low-QS keyword, diagnose issue (CTR vs Relevance vs LP)
- [ ] Implement fix (new ad, new landing page, etc.)
- [ ] Monitor QS improvement over 2-4 weeks

**Campaign Structure Best Practices:**

**Ad Group Size:** 10-20 keywords ideal
- More than 30 = too diluted
- Less than 5 = not enough data

**Keyword Match Types:**
- Start with phrase match for testing
- Exact match for proven winners
- Broad match only for discovery (limited budget)

**Negative Keywords:**
- Add weekly from search query reports
- Categories: free, cheap, demo, trial, download, job, career
- Review monthly: remove negatives if they block good traffic

**The Audit Framework:**

**Monthly Audit Checklist:**

**Account Structure:**
- [ ] Campaigns aligned with business goals
- [ ] Ad groups semantically related
- [ ] No duplicate keywords across ad groups
- [ ] Negative keywords properly implemented

**Performance:**
- [ ] CPA within target (top 80% of keywords)
- [ ] ROAS > 2.0 for profitable keywords
- [ ] Quality Score > 7/10 for top keywords
- [ ] CTR > 2% for top keywords

**Technical:**
- [ ] Conversion tracking working (test regularly)
- [ ] No broken links (crawl site monthly)
- [ ] Landing pages mobile-friendly
- [ ] Tracking code installed correctly

**Key Decision Criteria:**

**DC-1: ¿Cuándo pausar keywords?**
- PAUSE: CPA > 3x target for 14+ days, zero conversions after 100 clicks
- KEEP: CPA < target, or high impression share for brand terms
- TEST: Keywords on the fence (CPA 1.5-2x target) - try different ad/landing page

**DC-2: ¿Cuándo increase bids?**
- INCREASE: Top 20% of keywords by profit, QS improving, CPA < target with room for more volume
- HOLD: Stable performance, meeting targets
- DECREASE: Bottom 20% of keywords, CPA increasing, QS declining

**DC-3: ¿Cuándo launch new campaigns?**
- LAUNCH: When current campaigns are maxed out (budget constrained, impression share limited)
- HOLD: When still optimizing existing campaigns
- AVOID: Launching new campaigns without fixing current issues (amplifies problems)

## Anti-patrones

- ❌ ANTI-PATTERN 1: Daily bid changes based on daily fluctuations (wait for 7-day trends)
- ❌ ANTI-PATTERN 2: Ignoring search query reports (missing negative keyword opportunities)
- ❌ ANTI-PATTERN 3: Focusing on position #1 at all costs (positions 2-4 often more profitable)
- ❌ ANTI-PATTERN 4: Not auditing conversion tracking regularly (broken tracking = bad decisions)
- ❌ ANTI-PATTERN 5: Managing all keywords equally (focus on top 20% that drive 80% of results)
