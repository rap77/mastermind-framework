# FUENTE-M9-003: Email Copywriting Framework (Val Geisler)

## Tesis Central

> **"El email copywriting no es sobre writing bonito — es sobre PSYCHOLOGY. Entender por qué la gente abre, clicks, y compra es más importante que ser un 'buen writer'."**

---

## 1. Principios Fundamentales

> **P1: El subject line es una PROMESA, el email body es el CUMPLIMIENTO**
> Si el subject line dice "5 ways to double revenue", el email MEJOR tener 5 ways reales. Broken promises = unsubscribes. Kept promises = trust.
> *Fuente: Val Geisler Email Copywriting Course, Module 2: "Subject Lines That Convert" (Geisler, 2020)*
> *Contexto: Nunca use clickbait que no puedes entregar.*

> **P2: La primer línea del email es MÁS importante que el subject line**
> La mayoría de email clients muestran preview text. Si tu primer línea es weak, el subject line fuerte no importa. La primer línea debe CONTINUAR la curiosidad del subject line.
> *Fuente: Val Geisler Email Copywriting Course, Module 3: "The Opening Hook" (Geisler, 2020)*
> *Contexto: Escribe subject line + primera línea COMO UN BLOQUE, no separados.*

> **P3: Los readers no leen emails, escanean emails**
> El eye tracking muestra que people hacen un F-pattern scan: subject line → preview → bold text → P.S. line → CTA. Si tu email es un wall of text → delete.
> *Fuente: Val Geisler Email Copywriting Course, Module 4: "Scannable Structure" (Geisler, 2020)*
> *Contexto: Usa bullets, bold text, short paragraphs para facilitar escaneo.*

> **P4: El CTA debe ser STUPIDLY simple**
> "Click here" es mejor que "Navigate to our platform to access the premium features which will allow you to..." People scan, no leen. Make the action OBVIOUS.
> *Fuente: Val Geisler Email Copywriting Course, Module 5: "CTAs That Convert" (Geisler, 2020)*
> *Contexto: One CTA por email. Max 2-3 words en el button copy.*

> **P5: El mejor email copy suena como un friend hablando, no una empresa vendiendo**
> Usa contracciones ("don't" en vez de "do not"). Usa "you" más que "we". Usa humor, vulnerabilidad, honesty. La gente compra de people, no brands.
> *Fuente: Val Geisler Email Copywriting Course, Module 1: "Voice and Tone" (Geisler, 2020)*
> *Contexto: Escribe como le hablarías a un friend en un café.*

## 2. Frameworks y Metodologías

### Framework 1: The 3-Sentence Email Structure

**Fuente:** Val Geisler Email Copywriting Course, Module 4: "Scannable Structure" (Geisler, 2020)

**Estructura mínima para CUALQUIER email:**

**Sentence 1: The Hook (Curiosity or Empathy)**
- "You know how frustrating it is when..."
- "I finally figured out why..."
- "Most [prospect type] get this wrong..."

**Sentence 2: The Bridge (Context or Story)**
- "Well, here's what I discovered..."
- "The solution is actually simpler than you think..."
- "I was chatting with [customer] who said..."

**Sentence 3: The CTA (One Clear Action)**
- "Click here to see it in action."
- "Reply 'YES' and I'll send you the details."
- "Watch this 2-min video to learn more."

**Cómo aplicar:** Para promotional emails, intenta decirlo todo en 3 sentences. Si puedes't, tu offer es demasiado complejo.

---

### Framework 2: The 7 Subject Line Triggers

**Fuente:** Val Geisler Email Copywriting Course, Module 2: "Subject Lines That Convert" (Geisler, 2020)

**Psychological triggers que generan opens:**

1. **Curiosity Gap** — "The #1 mistake you're making with [topic]"
2. **Negativity Bias** — "Why [common advice] is actually WRONG"
3. **Specific Number** — "7 emails that generated $50K each"
4. **Urgency/Scarcity** — "Last chance: doors close at midnight"
5. **Personalization** — "[Name], I have something for you"
6. **Question** — "Are you struggling with [pain point]?"
7. **Story Hook** — "I never thought I'd [unexpected outcome], but then..."

**Cómo aplicar:** Test al menos 2 subject lines por email usando diferentes triggers. La diferencia entre 20% y 40% open rate = 2x revenue.

---

### Framework 3: The P.S. Power Play

**Fuente:** Val Geisler Email Copywriting Course, Module 4: "Scannable Structure" (Geisler, 2020)

**El P.S. es el segundo subject line.** Muchos readers scan: subject → preview → bold → **P.S.** → delete.

**P.S. Formulas:**

1. **The Restatement CTA** — "P.S. Remember, you only have until midnight."
2. **The Bonus Reveal** — "P.S. I almost forgot: if you buy in the next hour, you also get X."
3. **The Risk Reversal** — "P.S. Don't forget: 30-day money-back guarantee. No risk."
4. **The Personal Note** — "P.S. I'm answering reader questions live on Friday. Reply with yours."
5. **The Curiosity P.S.** — "P.S. The trick in step 3 is what took me from $0 to $10K/month."

**Cómo aplicar:** Si tu email tiene una CTA, incluye UN P.S. que reforce la CTA o agregue value.

---

### Framework 4: The A/B Testing Matrix

**Fuente:** Val Geisler Email Copywriting Course, Module 6: "Optimization and Testing" (Geisler, 2020)

**Qué testear en orden de prioridad:**

| Priority | Element | Variation | Expected Impact |
|----------|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| 1 | Subject Line | Curiosity vs. Question | 20-30% open rate diff |
| 2 | Preview Text | Benefit vs. Hook | 10-15% open rate diff |
| 3 | CTA Button Color | Blue vs. Green | 5-10% CTR diff |
| 4 | CTA Copy | "Buy Now" vs. "Get Instant Access" | 5-10% CTR diff |
| 5 | Email Length | Short vs. Long | Test with your audience |
| 6 | Sender Name | Company vs. Person | Test with your audience |

**Cómo aplicar:** Test one variable at a time. Si cambias subject line + CTA simultáneamente, no sabrás qué causó el difference.

## 3. Modelos Mentales

### Modelo Mental 1: The Email Funnel (Micro-Journey)

**Fuente:** Val Geisler Email Copywriting Course, Module 1: "The Psychology of Email" (Geisler, 2020)

**Concepto:** Cada email es un mini-funnel. Subject line (open) → Preview (engage) → Opening hook (read) → Body (consider) → CTA (click).

**Implicación práctica:** Optimize cada step del micro-funnel. Si subject line es 10/10 pero opening hook es 2/10 → delete sin read. Si body es great pero CTA is weak → no click.

---

### Modelo Mental 2: The Split-Second Decision (Open/Not Open)

**Fuente:** Val Geisler Email Copywriting Course, Module 2: "Subject Lines That Convert" (Geisler, 2020)

**Concepto:** El decision de abrir un email toma < 0.5 segundos. Es emotional, no rational. No hay "thinking about it" — es gut reaction.

**Implicación práctica:** Subject line must trigger an IMMEDIATE emotional response. Curiosity, fear, greed, excitement — pick one. "Information" doesn't open emails.

---

### Modelo Mental 3: The Skeptic's Filter (Why Most Emails Fail)

**Fuente:** Val Geisler Email Copywriting Course, Module 3: "The Opening Hook" (Geisler, 2020)

**Concepto:** Tu reader tiene un skeptic filter permanent. "What's this person trying to sell me?" "Why should I care?" "Is this worth my time?" You must overcome skepticism in the first 3 seconds.

**Implicación práctica:** Lead with value, not pitch. Show you understand their problem. Prove you're different from the 50 other emails en su inbox.

## 4. Criterios de Decisión (Trade-offs Profesionales)

### Trade-off 1: One-Link vs. Multi-Link Emails (Revisit)

| One-Link | Multi-Link |
|---------|-----------|
| ✅ Higher CTR (focus) | ✅ Better para newsletters |
| ✅ Clearer analytics | ✅ Más content por email |
| ❌ Menos flexible | ❌ CTR diluido |

**Decisión:** One-link para sales sequences. Multi-link solo para curated newsletters.

**Fuente:** Val Geisler Email Copywriting Course, Module 4: "Scannable Structure" (Geisler, 2020)

---

### Trade-off 2: Plain Text vs. HTML (Revisit)

| Plain Text | HTML |
|-----------|------|
| ✅ More personal | ✅ Better tracking |
| ✅ Higher deliverability | ✅ Visual appeal |
| ❌ No tracking data | ❌ Lower deliverability |

**Decisión:** Test con tu audiencia. Geisler recomienda plain-text-with-tracking-links como sweet spot.

**Fuente:** Val Geisler Email Copywriting Course, Module 5: "CTAs That Convert" (Geisler, 2020)

## 5. Anti-patrones

### Anti-patrón 1: The "Click Here" CTA (Generic Call-to-Action)

**Síntoma:** CTAs como "Click here", "Learn more", "Get started".

**Por qué falla:** "Click here" no dice what happens after clicking. No hay benefit.

**Fix:** "Click here to get the template", "Watch the 2-minute demo", "Get instant access to X".

**Fuente:** Val Geisler Email Copywriting Course, Module 5: "CTAs That Convert" (Geisler, 2020)

---

### Anti-patrón 2: The "Buried Lead" (Main Point at the End)

**Síntoma:** Emails que empiezan con "Hi [Name], hope you're doing well. I wanted to reach out because..." y el valor está al final.

**Por qué falla:** Readers no scroll. Si el valor no está in the first 3 lines → delete.

**Fix:** Lead with value. "Here's the template I promised you..." + luego context.

**Fuente:** Val Geisler Email Copywriting Course, Module 3: "The Opening Hook" (Geisler, 2020)

---

### Anti-patrón 3: The "Wall of Text" (Un-scannable Content)

**Síntoma:** Bloques de texto de 5+ líneas sin bullets, bold, o spacing.

**Por qué falla:** Eye tracking muestra que readers escanean, no leen. Wall of text = overwhelming.

**Fix:** Max 2-3 sentences per paragraph. Usa bullets para lists. Usa **bold** para key phrases.

**Fuente:** Val Geisler Email Copywriting Course, Module 4: "Scannable Structure" (Geisler, 2020)

## Métricas Clave

- **Subject Line Length:** Ideal 30-50 characters
- **Preview Text:** Use it como second subject line
- **First Sentence Length:** Max 15 words
- **Email Length:** 150-250 words para promotional
- **CTA Count:** ONE CTA por email
- **CTR Benchmark:** 2-5% es average, 5%+ es excellent
