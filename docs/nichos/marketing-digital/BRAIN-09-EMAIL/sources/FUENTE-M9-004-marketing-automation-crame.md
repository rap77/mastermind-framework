# FUENTE-M9-004: Marketing Automation Framework (Jepsen Crame - MAI)

## Tesis Central

> **"Email automation no es sobre enviar más emails — es sobre enviarlos EN EL MOMENTO EXACTO cuando el subscriber necesita recibirlos. Timing > Content. Trigger-based > Broadcast."**

---

## 1. Principios Fundamentales

> **P1: El broadcast email está muerto, el trigger-based es el futuro**
> Broadcast emails (enviados a toda la lista al mismo tiempo) have < 20% open rates. Trigger-based emails (enviados basados en behavior específico) have 50%+ open rates.
> *Fuente: Marketing Automation Institute, Module 1: "Trigger-Based vs. Broadcast" (Crame, 2019)*
> *Contexto: Every email debe tener un trigger behavior específico.*

> **P2: El timing es más importante que el content**
> Un email mediocre enviado en el momento perfecto convierte más que un email brillante enviado 2 días tarde. Automation systems solve the timing problem.
> *Fuente: Marketing Automation Institute, Module 2: "Timing Optimization" (Crame, 2019)*
> *Contexto: Define triggers basados en behavior, no en calendar dates.*

> **P3: La segmentación es CONTINUA, no estática**
> No segmentes una vez y listo. Los subscribers se mueven entre segments dinámicamente basado en behavior. Email automation debe actualizar segments en real-time.
> *Fuente: Marketing Automation Institute, Module 3: "Dynamic Segmentation" (Crame, 2019)*
> *Contexto: Usa behavioral tags, no solo demographic data.*

> **P4: El lead scoring es mandatory para B2B, optional para B2C**
> En B2B, no puedes contactar a todos los leads personalmente. Lead scoring te dice quiénes están ready para sales outreach. En B2C, scoring es menos critical.
> *Fuente: Marketing Automation Institute, Module 4: "Lead Scoring Models" (Crame, 2019)*
> *Contexto: Define scoring model basado en YOUR sales cycle.*

> **P5: El email automation debe tener un OFF switch**
- Automation corre forever hasta que el subscriber completa el goal. Necesitas reglas claras de cuándo REMOVER a alguien de una sequence.
> *Fuente: Marketing Automation Institute, Module 5: "Sequence Management" (Crame, 2019)*
> *Contexto: Nunca dejes que un subscriber reciba emails irrelevantes porque una automation no tiene exit criteria.*

## 2. Frameworks y Metodologías

### Framework 1: The 5 Essential Email Automations

**Fuente:** Marketing Automation Institute, Module 1: "Essential Automations" (Crame, 2019)

**Todo negocio debe tener estas 5 automations mínimo:**

**1. Welcome Sequence (New Subscribers)**
- **Trigger:** User subscribes (lead magnet, newsletter signup)
- **Goal:** Build trust + set expectations + first value
- **Length:** 5-7 emails over 10-14 days
- **Content:** Who we are + what to expect + quick wins + soft pitch

**2. Abandoned Cart (Ecommerce)**
- **Trigger:** User adds item to cart but doesn't purchase
- **Goal:** Recover lost sales
- **Length:** 3 emails over 3 days
- **Content:** Reminder + social proof + urgency/scarcity

**3. Browse Abandonment (Ecommerce)**
- **Trigger:** User viewed product but didn't add to cart
- **Goal:** Push to consideration
- **Length:** 2 emails over 2 days
- **Content:** "Still thinking about X?" + benefit reminder

**4. Re-Engagement (Inactive Subscribers)**
- **Trigger:** No activity in 90+ days
- **Goal:** Win back OR remove from list
- **Length:** 3 emails over 7 days
- **Content:** "We miss you" + special offer + final warning

**5. Post-Purchase Nurture (Customers)**
- **Trigger:** Customer makes first purchase
- **Goal:** Generate repeat purchase
- **Length:** 4-6 emails over 30-60 days
- **Content:** Thank you + how to use + upsell/cross-sell + request review

**Cómo aplicar:** Implementa las 5 automations en este orden. Welcome sequence primero, luego abandoned cart (si eres ecommerce), luego las demás.

### Framework 2: The Trigger Library (Common Email Triggers)

**Fuente:** Marketing Automation Institute, Module 2: "Trigger Types" (Crame, 2019)

**Behavioral Triggers (más efectivos):**

| Trigger | Use Case | Open Rate |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| **Signup/Subscribe** | Welcome sequences | 50-70% |
| **Abandoned Cart** | Ecommerce recovery | 40-60% |
| **Website Visit (Specific Page)** | Lead nurturing | 30-50% |
| **Email Click (Specific Link)** | Interest-based follow-up | 50-70% |
| **Purchase** | Post-purchase sequence | 60-80% |
| **Inactivity (90 days)** | Re-engagement | 15-25% |
| **Milestone (Birthday/Anniversary)** | Personal touch | 40-60% |
| **Download (Lead Magnet)** | Content consumption | 35-55% |

**Cómo aplicar:** Mapea customer journey → identifica key behaviors → crea trigger para cada behavior.

### Framework 3: The Drip Campaign Structure

**Fuente:** Marketing Automation Institute, Module 3: "Drip Campaigns" (Crame, 2019)

**Estructura estándar para una drip sequence:**

**Email 1: Immediate Value (Day 0)**
- Goal: Deliver lead magnet + set expectations
- CTA: "Here's your download + what to expect next"

**Email 2: Quick Win (Day 1-2)**
- Goal: Show first small result
- CTA: "Try this one thing today"

**Email 3: Case Study (Day 3-4)**
- Goal: Social proof + relatability
- CTA: "See how [similar person] got result"

**Email 4: Deep Dive/Advanced (Day 5-7)**
- Goal: Provide deeper value
- CTA: "Learn more about [topic]"

**Email 5: Soft Pitch (Day 8-10)**
- Goal: Transition to offer
- CTA: "If you want more, here's what we have"

**Email 6+: Nurture/Occasional Pitch**
- Goal: Stay top of mind
- Frequency: Weekly or bi-weekly

**Cómo aplicar:** Usa esta estructura para ANY nurture sequence (lead magnet, webinar, free trial signup, etc).

## 3. Modelos Mentales

### Modelo Mental 1: The Automation Flywheel (Compounding Returns)

**Fuente:** Marketing Automation Institute, Module 1: "Automation ROI" (Crame, 2019)

**Concepto:** Las automations generan returns compounding. Una vez que la creas, funciona forever. Cada nueva automation se suma a las anteriores.

**Implicación práctica:** Prioriza building automations sobre one-off campaigns. El upfront effort es mayor, pero el long-term回报 es masive.

---

### Modelo Mental 2: The Subscriber Lifecycle (Not Static)

**Fuente:** Marketing Automation Institute, Module 4: "Lifecycle Marketing" (Crame, 2019)

**Concepto:** Los subscribers se mueven entre stages: Lead → Customer → Advocate → Churn. Email automation debe moverlos proactivamente al siguiente stage.

**Implicación práctica:** No trates a customer como un lead. No trates un advocate como un new subscriber. Different stages = different automations.

## 4. Criterios de Decisión

### Trade-off 1: Immediate vs. Delayed Trigger

| Immediate (Within 1 hour) | Delayed (1-7 days) |
|--------------------------|-------------------|
| ✅ Higher relevance (fresco en memoria) | ✅ Less intrusive |
| ✅ Higher conversion rates | ✅ Better para non-urgent |
| ❌ Puede feel aggressive | ❌ Lower urgency |
| **Use for:** Welcome, abandoned cart | **Use for:** Browse abandonment, nurture |

**Fuente:** Marketing Automation Institute, Module 2: "Timing Optimization" (Crame, 2019)

---

## 5. Anti-patrones

### Anti-patrón 1: The "Set and Forget" (No Ongoing Optimization)

**Síntoma:** Crear automation y nunca revisar metrics.

**Por qué falla:** Las automations se degradan con el tiempo. Lo que funcionó hace 6 meses puede no funcionar hoy.

**Fix:** Revisa automation metrics monthly. A/B testea continuamente.

**Fuente:** Marketing Automation Institute, Module 5: "Sequence Management" (Crame, 2019)

## Métricas Clave

- **Trigger Email Open Rate:** 50%+ (vs 20% para broadcast)
- **Trigger Email CTR:** 10%+ (vs 2.5% para broadcast)
- **Automation Completion Rate:** 60-80% de subscribers should complete la sequence
- **Time in Sequence:** Promedio de días que un subscriber toma en completar
- **Unsubscribe Rate por Automation:** < 1% por sequence
