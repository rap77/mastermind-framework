# FUENTE-M9-002: Customer Value Journey (Ryan Deiss - DigitalMarketer)

## Tesis Central

> **"El marketing no es sobre traffic — es sobre SYSTEMS que convierten ese traffic en customers, y customers en advocates. El Customer Value Journey es ese system."**

---

## 1. Principios Fundamentales

> **P1: El funnel lineal está muerto**
> El viejo modelo AIDA (Awareness → Interest → Desire → Action) es oversimplificado. Los customers no viajan en línea recta — saltan, se devuelven, se estancan. El Customer Value Journey refleja esta realidad no-lineal.
> *Fuente: DigitalMarketer Lab, Module 1: "The Customer Value Journey" (Deiss, 2018)*
> *Contexto: Deja de pensar en funnels lineares. Piensa en journeys circulares.*

> **P2: Email es el NERVIOSO del journey, no el músculo**
> El error es pensar que email "cierra ventas". Email conecta TODO — desde el primer touch hasta el upsell 6 meses después. Sin email, el journey se rompe.
> *Fuente: DigitalMarketer Lab, Module 3: "Email Marketing Strategy" (Deiss, 2018)*
> *Contexto: Email debe estar presente en CADA etapa del Customer Value Journey.*

> **P3: El CTR es una vanity metric, el OPE (Open-Engagement Rate) es lo que importa**
> A nadie le importa si tu email tiene 50% CTR si nadie compra. Lo que importa es cuánta gente avanza al siguiente stage del journey después de hacer click.
> *Fuente: DigitalMarketer Lab, Module 5: "Email Optimization" (Deiss, 2018)*
> *Contexto: Optimiza para journey progression, no para engagement vanity.*

> **P4: El money is in the BACKEND, no en el frontend**
> La mayoría de marketers obsesionan con adquirir customers (front-end). El real profit está en retener, upsell, y cross-sell (back-end). Email es EL channel para backend marketing.
> *Fuente: DigitalMarketer Lab, Module 7: "Retention and Advocacy" (Deiss, 2018)*
> *Contexto: Invierte igual o más en backend email sequences que en lead gen.*

> **P5: Segmentation beats creativity every time**
> Puedes tener el mejor copy del mundo — si se lo envías a la persona wrong, es spam. La segmentación (behavioral, demographic, psychographic) es el #1 predictor de success en email marketing.
> *Fuente: DigitalMarketer Lab, Module 4: "List Segmentation" (Deiss, 2018)*
> *Contexto: Nunca envíes broadcast a toda tu lista. Segment siempre.*

## 2. Frameworks y Metodologías

### Framework 1: The Customer Value Journey (8 Stages)

**Fuente:** DigitalMarketer Lab, Module 1: "The Customer Value Journey" (Deiss, 2018)

**Los 8 Stages:**

1. **AWARENESS** — El prospect se entera de tu existencia (ad, content, referral)
2. **ENGAGE** — El prospect interactúa por primera vez (site visit, blog read)
3. **SUBSCRIBE** — El prospect se convierte en lead (opt-in, lead magnet)
4. **CONVERT** — El lead hace la primera compra (tripwire, core offer)
5. **EXCITE** — El nuevo customer tiene un "wow moment" (product delivery, onboarding)
6. **ASCEND** — El customer compra más (upsell, cross-sell)
7. **ADVOCATE** — El customer se vuelve promotor (reviews, referrals)
8. **PROMOTE** — El customer promueve activamente (affiliate, case study)

**Email's Role en cada stage:**

| Stage | Email Objective | Example |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Awareness | Nurture with value | "5 ways to solve X" (educational) |
| Engage | Deepen relationship | "You checked out X, here's Y" |
| Subscribe | Welcome + set expectations | "Welcome to the family + what to expect" |
| Convert | Overcome objections + urgency | "Last chance: 50% off ends tonight" |
| Excite | Deliver quick wins | "Here's how to get the most out of your purchase" |
| Ascend | Reveal premium opportunities | "You're ready for the next level" |
| Advocate | Request social proof | "Can you spare 2 minutes for a review?" |
| Promote | Turn into partners | "Want to earn $$ by sharing us?" |

**Cómo aplicar:** Mapea cada email sequence a un stage específico del journey. No mezcles stages en una misma sequence.

### Framework 2: The 3-Part Email Sequence (Mini-Sequence)

**Fuente:** DigitalMarketer Lab, Module 3: "Email Marketing Strategy" (Deiss, 2018)

**Estructura universal para CUALQUIER objetivo:**

**Email 1: The Value Drop (80% value, 20% pitch)**
- Hook: Story o insight que genera curiosity
- Content: 3 actionable tips/takeaways
- CTA: Soft pitch (ej: "Want more like this? Click here")

**Email 2: The Case Study (Social Proof)**
- Hook: "Here's what happened when [person] applied this"
- Content: Before/After específico, testimonial
- CTA: Medium pitch (ej: "Get the same result [link]")

**Email 3: The Urgency/Scarcity Close**
- Hook: "This is your last chance"
- Content: Benefit recap + reminder de lo que pierden
- CTA: Hard pitch + urgency (ej: "Doors close at midnight")

**Cómo aplicar:** Usa esta estructura para launches, webinars, product announcements, lead magnet follow-ups.

### Framework 3: The Segment-First Matrix

**Fuente:** DigitalMarketer Lab, Module 4: "List Segmentation" (Deiss, 2018)

**Los 4 Quadrants de Segmentation:**

| Cold (No relationship) | Hot (Existing relationship) |
|-----------------------|----------------------------|
| **Top of Funnel** | **Back-End** |
| Goal: AWARENESS | Goal: ASCEND/ADVOCATE |
| Email: Educational content | Email: Exclusive offers |
| Frequency: 3-7 days | Frequency: 14-30 days |
| **Middle of Funnel** | **Retention** |
| Goal: CONVERT | Goal: EXCITE/RETAIN |
| Email: Objection handling | Email: Success stories |
| Frequency: 1-3 days | Frequency: 7-14 days |

**Cómo aplicar:** Antes de escribir CUALQUIER email, pregunta: "¿A qué quadrant pertenece este subscriber?" Luego escribe para ese quadrant.

### Framework 4: The Subject Line Formula Library

**Fuente:** DigitalMarketer Lab, Module 5: "Email Optimization" (Deiss, 2018)

**7 Subject Line Formulas probadas:**

1. **The "Who, What, Why"**
   - "[Name], here's why [result] is harder than you think"
   - Ej: "Maria, here's why scaling your agency is harder than you think"

2. **The "Open Loop"**
   - "The #1 mistake [prospect type] make [common activity]"
   - Ej: "The #1 mistake SaaS founders make with their pricing page"

3. **The "Negative Paradox"**
   - "Why [good thing] is actually BAD for [outcome]"
   - Ej: "Why 'working hard' is actually BAD for your productivity"

4. **The "Specific Number"**
   - "[X] ways to [achieve desired outcome]"
   - Ej: "7 ways to double your email open rate (tested)"

5. **The "Story Hook"**
   - "I never thought I'd [unexpected outcome], but then [trigger event]"
   - Ej: "I never thought I'd quit my job, but then this happened"

6. **The "Question"**
   - "Are you [common problem] without [desired solution]?"
   - Ej: "Are you generating leads without a system?"

7. **The "Curiosity Gap"**
   - "The truth about [myth/belief in your industry]"
   - Ej: "The truth about 'passive income' nobody tells you"

**Cómo aplicar:** Test 2-3 subject lines por email. Usa A/B testing. La diferencia entre 20% y 40% open rate = 2x resultados.

## 3. Modelos Mentales

### Modelo Mental 1: The Email Asset Tree (Long-term View)

**Fuente:** DigitalMarketer Lab, Module 2: "Building Your Email List" (Deiss, 2018)

**Concepto:** Tu email list no es un list. Es un ASSET que vale $$$. Como un árbol, toma tiempo crecer, pero una vez maduro, da frutos recurrentemente.

**Implicación práctica:** No sacrifiques tu list por short-term gains. No vendas data. No spam. No envíes bullshit. La confianza del subscriber es el asset más valioso.

---

### Modelo Mental 2: The Campaign vs. The Sequence (Distinction)

**Fuente:** DigitalMarketer Lab, Module 3: "Email Marketing Strategy" (Deiss, 2018)

**Concepto:** Un *campaign* tiene inicio y fin (launch, promo). Una *sequence* corre forever (welcome, nurture). The mistake es tratar campaigns como sequences.

| Campaign (Time-bound) | Sequence (Evergreen) |
|---------------------|---------------------|
| Launch, sale, event | Welcome, onboarding, nurture |
| End date pre-defined | No end date (evolves) |
| One-time recipients | New subscribers added continuously |
| Goal: Urgency + conversion | Goal: Relationship + trust building |

**Implicación práctica:** Build sequences first. Then plug campaigns into the sequence flow.

---

### Modelo Mental 3: The Bucket System (List Management)

**Fuente:** DigitalMarketer Lab, Module 4: "List Segmentation" (Deiss, 2018)

**Concepto:** Tu list tiene buckets. Los subscribers se mueven entre buckets basado en behavior. Never treat your list como un monolito.

**Los 5 Buckets:**

1. **Leads** — Subscribed, never bought
2. **Customers** — Bought once
3. **Multi-Buyers** — Bought 2+ times
4. **Active** — Opened/clicked in last 30 days
5. **Inactive** — No activity in 90+ days

**Implicación práctica:** Create separate sequences for each bucket. Don't send lead magnet emails to multi-buyers (insulting). Don't send $500 offers to leads (premature).

## 4. Criterios de Decisión (Trade-offs Profesionales)

### Trade-off 1: Daily vs. Weekly Email Sends

| Daily | Weekly |
|------|--------|
| ✅ Faster list fatigue (quedan inactivos) | ✅ Longer list health |
| ✅ More data/optimization opportunities | ✅ Higher anticipation per email |
| ❌ Higher unsubscribe rate | ✅ Better deliverability |
| ❌ Writer burnout (díficil maintain) | ✅ Sustainable long-term |
| **Use for:** Launches, time-sensitive promos | **Use for:** Nurture, evergreen content |

**Decisión:** Default = Weekly. Solo daily durante launches/events (7-10 days max).

**Fuente:** DigitalMarketer Lab, Module 5: "Email Optimization" (Deiss, 2018)

---

### Trade-off 2: Single-Link vs. Multi-Link Emails

| Single-Link | Multi-Link |
|------------|------------|
| ✅ Higher CTR (focus) | ✅ More options (más flexibilidad) |
| ✅ Easier to track (un solo goal) | ✅ Better para newsletter/content |
| ✅ Stronger CTA (no distractions) | ❌ Lower CTR per link (dilución) |
| ❌ No funciona para curated content | ❌ Harder to optimize (¿qué funcionó?) |

**Decisión:** Single-link para sales/sequences. Multi-link solo para newsletters/curated content.

**Fuente:** DigitalMarketer Lab, Module 6: "Email Design and Layout" (Deiss, 2018)

---

### Trade-off 3: HTML vs. Plain Text (Revisit)

| Plain Text | HTML |
|-----------|------|
| ✅ Better deliverability | ✅ Better tracking |
| ✅ Feels more personal | ✅ Visual appeal |
| ❌ No tracking data | ❌ Lower deliverability |
| ❌ Harder to stand out | ❌ Feels more "marketing" |
| **Use for:** B2B, high-ticket | **Use for:** B2C, ecommerce |

**Decisión:** Test con tu audiencia. DigitalMarketer encontró que plain-text-with-HTML-fallback es el sweet spot.

**Fuente:** DigitalMarketer Lab, Module 6: "Email Design and Layout" (Deiss, 2018)

---

### Trade-off 4: Long-Form vs. Short-Form Copy

| Long-Form (500+ words) | Short-Form (200 words) |
|----------------------|----------------------|
| ✅ Mejor para high-ticket | ✅ Mejor para low-ticket |
| ✅ Más storytelling | ✅ Más escaneable |
| ✅ Build authority/trust | ✅ Higher read rate |
| ❌ Lower completion rate | ❌ Difícil build depth |
| **Use for:** Core offers, launches | **Use for:** Daily tips, promos |

**Decisión:** Long-form para first purchase. Short-form para repeat purchases/engagement.

**Fuente:** DigitalMarketer Lab, Module 5: "Email Optimization" (Deiss, 2018)

## 5. Anti-patrones

### Anti-patrón 1: The "Spray and Pray" (Broadcasting to Entire List)

**Síntoma:** Enviar el mismo email a toda la lista sin segmentación.

**Por qué falla:** 80% de tu list NO está ready para tu oferta. Los irritas → unsubscribe. Los otros 20% que SÍ están ready → no reciben mensaje relevante porque está "diluido".

**Fix:** Segment always. Mínimo 3 segments (leads, customers, multi-buyers). Ideal = 10+ segments.

**Fuente:** DigitalMarketer Lab, Module 4: "List Segmentation" (Deiss, 2018)

---

### Anti-patrón 2: The "One-And-Done" (No Follow-Up Sequence)

**Síntoma:** Enviar un solo email y esperar conversion.

**Por qué falla:** Statistics show que el 50% de sales ocurren después del 5th contact. One email = leaving money on table.

**Fix:** Minimum 5-email sequence para cualquier offer. Day 1, 3, 5, 7, 10 es default sequence.

**Fuente:** DigitalMarketer Lab, Module 3: "Email Marketing Strategy" (Deiss, 2018)

---

### Anti-patrón 3: The "Link Farm" (Demasiados Links en Email)

**Síntoma:** Emails con 10+ links (social, blog, shop, about, etc).

**Por qué falla:** Paradox of choice. Más opciones = menos action. El reader se paraliza.

**Fix:** One primary CTA por email. Max 2 links total (primary + optional footer link).

**Fuente:** DigitalMarketer Lab, Module 6: "Email Design and Layout" (Deiss, 2018)

---

### Anti-patrón 4: The "Subject Line Tease" (Clickbait Without Substance)

**Síntoma:** Subject lines como "You won't believe this..." con contenido disappointing.

**Por qué falla:** Quemaste trust. La próxima vez, no abrirán. Email es un long-term game.

**Fix:** Subject line debe PROMETER y ENTREGAR. No hagas promises que no puedes cumplir en el email body.

**Fuente:** DigitalMarketer Lab, Module 5: "Email Optimization" (Deiss, 2018)

---

### Anti-patrón 5: The "Sales-Only" Diet (Solo Enviar Promos)

**Síntoma:** 100% de tus emails son ventas/sales/sales.

**Por qué falla:** Training al audience para ignorarte. Si cada email es "BUY NOW", tu list se vuelve numb.

**Fix:** Ratio 80/20: 80% value, 20% pitch. O incluso 90/10. Build trust antes de pedir.

**Fuente:** DigitalMarketer Lab, Module 7: "Retention and Advocacy" (Deiss, 2018)

## Métricas Clave

- **Open Rate:** Industry avg = 20% (benchmark para tu vertical)
- **Click-Through Rate:** Industry avg = 2.5% (CTR es vanity, OPE es lo que importa)
- **Conversion Rate:** Industry avg = 1% (email → purchase)
- **Unsubscribe Rate:** Should be < 0.5% per send
- **List Growth Rate:** Should be positive month-over-month
- **Revenue per Subscriber:** Key metric — track LTV de un email lead
