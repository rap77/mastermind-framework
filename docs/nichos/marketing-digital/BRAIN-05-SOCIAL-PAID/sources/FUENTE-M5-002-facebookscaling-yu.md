# FUENTE-M5-002: Facebook Ads Scaling Strategies — Dennis Yu

## Tesis Central

> El scaling de Facebook Ads no es about adding more budget, es about building systems. El modelo "Dollar-a-Day" significa: empezá con $1/day para testing, escalá a $10, $100, $1000 basado en DATA, no en intuición. La clave del scaling sostenible es el framework "Test, Scale, Optimize": (1) Testeá con budget pequeño, (2) Escalá lo que funciona incrementalmente, (3) Optimizá continuamente basado en los números. El error #1 que cometen los advertisers es escalar sin data, lo cual resulta en aumentar costs sin aumentar revenue proporcionalmente.

## 1. Principios Fundamentales

> **P1: El scaling es incremental, no binario**
- No vayás de $10 a $1000 en un día
- Escalá en increments de 20-30% cada 3-4 días
- Cada nivel de budget tiene diferentes mechanics
> *Fuente: "Incremental Scaling Framework" (Yu, 2021)*

> **P2: La regla del 3x para scaling**
- Antes de escalar, necesitás 3x statistical significance
- 100 conversions no es suficiente, necesitás 300+
- Sin data suficiente, estás gambling, no scaling
> *Fuente: "Minimum Data Requirements" (Yu, 2021)*

> **P3: El creative decay es real**
- Todo anuncio eventualmente fatiga (ad fatigue)
- El lifetime promedio de un creative es 2-4 semanas
- Necesitás un pipeline de nuevos creatives constantemente
> *Fuente: "Ad Fatigue Curve" (Yu, 2020)*

> **P4: El CTR es el leading indicator del performance**
- CTR cae antes que CPA sube
- Monitorear CTR te da early warning de problems
- Si CTR baja > 30%, es hora de nuevo creative
> *Fuente: "CTR as Leading Metric" (Yu, 2021)*

> **P5: El scaling vertical tiene un ceiling**
- No podés escalar un ad set infinitamente
- El ceiling depende de audience size y competition
- Después del ceiling, solo scaling horizontal funciona
> *Fuente: "Vertical Scaling Limits" (Yu, 2020)*

## 2. Frameworks y Metodologías

### Framework 1: El Método Dollar-a-Day

**Fuente:** "Dollar-a-Day Strategy" (Yu, 2020)
**Propósito:** Testing sistemático con budget mínimo.
**Cuándo usar:** Al lanzar nuevos campaigns o ad sets.

**Fase 1: The Discovery Phase ($1/day x 30 days)**

Por cada hypothesis (creative + audience + offer):
- Seteá budget de $1/day
- Corré por 7 days mínimo
- Kill lo que no funcione, keep lo que funcione

**Decision Criteria:**
- Keep if: CPA < 2x target, CTR > 1%, any conversion
- Kill if: Zero conversions after 7 days, CTR < 0.5%

**Resultado esperado:** De cada 30 ad sets:
- 5 winners (CPA < target)
- 10 break-even (CPA ~ 2x target)
- 15 losers (kill immediately)

**Fase 2: The Validation Phase ($10/day x 14 days)**

Tomá los 5 winners de Fase 1:
- Subí budget a $10/day (10x)
- Monitoreá daily: CTR, CPC, CPA, ROAS
- Si performance se mantiene > 80% de Fase 1 → pasá a Fase 3
- Si performance cae > 30% → volvé a Fase 1 con nuevo creative

**Fase 3: The Growth Phase ($100-$1,000/day)**

Para cada validated ad set:
- Subí budget en 20% increments cada 3 days
- STOP si: CPA > 2x target, CTR cae > 30%
- SCALE horizontalmente si: Vertical scaling hits ceiling

**Key Rule:** Never escalé más de 20-30% en un solo day. Facebook AI necesita 72 hours para re-optimizar.

### Framework 2: The Testing Grid Framework

**Fuente:** "Systematic Testing Grid" (Yu, 2021)
**Propósito:** Testear múltiples variables sin confounding factors.
**Cuándo usar:** Al lanzar nueva campaña con múltiples hypotheses.

**The Grid:**

| Creative | Audience A (Lookalike 1%) | Audience B (Interests) | Audience C (Broad) |
|----------|---------------------------|------------------------|-------------------|
| **Creative 1** | $1/day | $1/day | $1/day |
| **Creative 2** | $1/day | $1/day | $1/day |
| **Creative 3** | $1/day | $1/day | $1/day |

**Total:** 9 ad sets x $1/day = $9/day total investment

**Análisis after 7 days:**

**Step 1: Identify Winning Creative**
- Si Creative 2 gana en 2/3 audiences → Creative winner
- Si Creative 1 gana en 0/3 audiences → Kill creative

**Step 2: Identify Winning Audience**
- Si Audience A gana con 2/3 creatives → Audience winner
- Si Audience C pierde con 3/3 creatives → Kill audience

**Step 3: Scale Winning Combination**
- Winner: Creative 2 + Audience A
- Escalá este combination a $10/day
- Monitoreá por 14 days antes de siguiente scaling

**Key Rule:** Testeá UNA dimensión a la vez. No testees creative + audience + offer simultáneamente.

### Framework 3: The Creative Pipeline System

**Fuente:** "Creative Production Pipeline" (Yu, 2021)
**Propósito:** Mantener un flow constante de nuevos creatives.
**Cuándo usar:** Continuous operation, especially when scaling.

**The 3-Tier Pipeline:**

**Tier 1: Production (New creatives in development)**
- Goal: 5-10 new creatives por semana
- Format: UGC videos, static images, carousels
- Ownership: Marketing team + creative agency

**Tier 2: Testing ($1/day active tests)**
- Goal: 10-20 creatives en testing activo
- Duration: 7 days por creative
- Budget: $1/day por creative
- Metrics: CTR, CPC, first conversions

**Tier 3: Live (Scaled campaigns)**
- Goal: 3-5 winning creatives con significant budget
- Budget: $100-$1,000/day por creative
- Duration: Until ad fatigue (2-4 weeks)
- Metrics: ROAS, CPA, scale limits

**The Workflow:**

1. **Monday:** Review Tier 2 testing results from last week
2. **Tuesday:** Move winners to Tier 3, kill losers
3. **Wednesday:** Brief new creatives for Tier 1 production
4. **Thursday:** Launch new Tier 2 tests
5. **Friday:** Review Tier 3 performance, optimize or kill

**Key Insight:** Necesitás un pipeline porque los creatives en Tier 3 eventualmente fatigan. Cuando eso pasa, ya debés tener reemplazos listos en Tier 2.

**Performance Benchmarks:**

| Tier | CTR | CPC | CPA | ROAS |
|------|-----|-----|-----|------|
| Testing (Tier 2) | > 1% | <$1 | < 2x target | > 1.0 |
| Live (Tier 3) | > 2% | <$0.50 | < target | > 2.0 |

## 3. Modelos Mentales

### Modelo Mental 1: The S-Curve of Scaling

**Fuente:** "Scaling S-Curve" (Yu, 2020)
**El modelo:** El scaling sigue una curva en S, no una línea recta.

**The 3 Phases of the S-Curve:**

**Phase 1: The Flat Start (Early Stage)**
- Budget: $1-$10/day
- Performance: Erratic, high variance
- Duration: 1-2 weeks
- Strategy: Test, don't optimize yet
- Key Metric: Any conversion = good sign

**Phase 2: The Linear Growth (Growth Stage)**
- Budget: $10-$500/day
- Performance: Stable, predictable
- Duration: 2-8 weeks
- Strategy: Scale incrementally (20% every 3 days)
- Key Metric: ROAS > 2.0

**Phase 3: The Plateau (Maturity Stage)**
- Budget: $500-$10,000/day
- Performance: Diminishing returns
- Duration: 4-12 weeks
- Strategy: Scale horizontal (new campaigns) or optimize creative
- Key Metric: Marginal ROAS

**Key Insight:** Most advertisers fracasan porque esperan linear growth desde el inicio. Pero la realidad es que la Phase 1 (flat start) es NORMAL y necesaria.

**When to move to next phase:**
- Phase 1 → 2: 100+ conversions, CPA < target for 7 days
- Phase 2 → 3: Ad fatigue detected (CTR down 30%+)
- Phase 3 → new Phase 1: Launch new campaigns to reset the curve

### Modelo Mental 2: The Portfolio Effect

**Fuente:** "Campaign Portfolio Management" (Yu, 2021)
**El modelo:** Tus campañas son un portfolio, no silos individuales.

**The Portfolio Allocation:**

**Core Campaigns (60% de budget)**
- Purpose: Stable, predictable revenue
- Characteristics: Proven winners, 3+ months running
- ROAS Target: 2.0-3.0
- Optimization: Maintenance mode, minimal changes

**Growth Campaigns (30% de budget)**
- Purpose: Scale winners from Core or new tests
- Characteristics: 1-3 months running, scaling
- ROAS Target: 1.5-2.5
- Optimization: Active scaling, close monitoring

**Experimental Campaigns (10% de budget)**
- Purpose: Test new creatives, audiences, offers
- Characteristics: < 1 month running, testing phase
- ROAS Target: Any positive ROAS is acceptable
- Optimization: Rapid testing, kill losers fast

**The Rebalancing Act:**

Each month:
- Experimental winners → Move to Growth (increase budget 5x)
- Growth winners → Move to Core (increase budget 2x)
- Core fatigue → Kill or move back to Growth for optimization

**Key Insight:** Con un portfolio, even if some campaigns fail, your overall revenue stays stable. Diversificación = resiliencia.

### Modelo Mental 3: The Attribution Gap

**Fuente:** "Multi-Touch Attribution Reality" (Yu, 2020)
**El modelo:** Facebook last-touch attribution es solo una parte de la historia.

**The Reality of Customer Journey:**

```
First Touch: Facebook Ad → Website Visit (not attributed)
Second Touch: Instagram Ad → Add to Cart (not attributed)
Third Touch: Facebook Retargeting → Purchase (attributed 100%)
```

**The Problem:** Facebook atribuye 100% de la conversión al último anuncio, ignorando el impacto de los primeros dos touchpoints.

**The Solution:** Track the full funnel:

**Top Funnel Metrics (Awareness):**
- Impressions, Reach, CTR
- Cost per landing page view
- Email signups

**Mid Funnel Metrics (Consideration):**
- Add to cart, Initiate checkout
- Time on site, Pages per session
- Email open rates, click rates

**Bottom Funnel Metrics (Conversion):**
- Purchases, ROAS, CPA
- Return rate, Refund rate
- LTV (Lifetime Value)

**Key Insight:** Un anuncio con 0 attributed conversions puede estar generando mucho valor si está driving top-of-funnel traffic que luego convierte con otros anuncios.

**Action:** No mates un anuncio solo porque no tiene attributed conversions. Mirá el full funnel impact.

## 4. Criterios de Decisión

### DC-1: ¿Cuándo escalar vertical vs horizontalmente?

**Escalamiento Vertical (más budget en el mismo ad set):**
- **Trigger:** Ad set con 100+ conversions, CPA < target, 7+ days stable
- **How much:** +20-30% cada 3 days
- **Stop when:** CPA increases > 30% from baseline
- **Best for:** Ad sets con room to grow (audience size > 1M)

**Escalamiento Horizontal (nuevos ad sets/campaigns):**
- **Trigger:** Vertical scaling hits ceiling (CPA goes up with more budget)
- **How:** Duplica campaign con different: audience, creative, or placement
- **Stop when:** New ad sets don't achieve target CPA
- **Best for:** Maximum scale, diversificación

**Source:** "Scaling Decision Tree" (Yu, 2021)

### DC-2: ¿Cuándo matar un anuncio vs optimizarlo?

**Matar inmediatamente:**
- CTR < 0.5% después de 10,000 impressions
- CPA > 3x target después de 50+ clicks
- Creative is outdated or brand-damaging

**Optimizar:**
- CTR dropped 20-30% from baseline (ad fatigue starting)
- CPA increased 20-30% from baseline (optimization opportunity)
- High impressions but low conversions (landing page or offer issue)

**Keep milking:**
- Stable performance for 2+ weeks
- CPA < target even with slight CTR decline
- Seasonal or evergreen offer with long lifecycle

**Source:** "Campaign Lifecycle Management" (Yu, 2021)

### DC-3: ¿Cuándo usar Campaign Budget Optimization (CBO) vs Ad Set Budget Optimization (ABO)?

**Usá CBO (Facebook optimiza budget entre ad sets):**
- Cuando: Tenés 5+ ad sets en una campaign
- Goal: Maximizar conversions a campaign level
- Advantage: Facebook AI distribuye budget a best performers
- Risk: Less control, can't predict individual ad set spend

**Usá ABO (fijás budget por ad set):**
- Cuando: Querés control granular de spend
- Goal: Testing o audiences muy diferentes
- Advantage: Predictable spend per ad set
- Risk: Manual optimization required, potential inefficiency

**Recommendation:** Start con ABO for testing, move to CBO for scaling.

**Source:** "CBO vs ABO Framework" (Yu, 2020)

## 5. Anti-patrones

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 1: Scaling basado en vanity metrics**
- **El problema:** Escalás porque tenés muchos likes/shares, no conversions
- **El costo:** Revenue no escala con costs
- **La solución:** Focus on CPA and ROAS, engagement es secondary metric
> *Fuente: "Vanity Metrics Trap" (Yu, 2020)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 2: Changing too many variables at once**
- **El problema:** Cambiás creative + audience + budget simultáneamente
- **El costo:** No sabés qué causó el cambio en performance
- **La solución:** One variable at a time, 7 days between changes
> *Fuente: "Confounding Variables" (Yu, 2021)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 3: Ignoring statistical significance**
- **El problema:** Tomás decisiones con 5-10 conversions (random noise)
- **El costo:** Killás ganadores o scaleás perdedores
- **La solución:** Minimum 100 conversions before making decisions
> *Fuente: "Statistical Significance Errors" (Yu, 2020)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 4: Over-optimization**
- **El problema:** Hacés cambios daily porque la campaña no es "perfecta"
- **El costo:** Nunca dejás que Facebook AI learning phase complete
- **La solución:** Changes maximum 2x per week, 3-4 days apart
> *Fuente: "Learning Phase Interruption" (Yu, 2021)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 5: Scaling sin diminishing returns analysis**
- **El problema:** Escalás linealmente ($10 → $100 → $1000) sin considerar边际
- **El costo:** Vas de profitable a unprofitable sin darte cuenta
- **La solución:** Monitoreá marginal ROAS (revenue adicionál por cost adicionál). Stop cuando marginal ROAS < 1.0
> *Fuente: "Diminishing Returns Blind Spot" (Yu, 2020)*
