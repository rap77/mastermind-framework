# FUENTE-M5-008: Publicidad en Redes Sociales — Merce Cardus

## Tesis Central

> Las redes sociales no son silos aislados, son parte de un ecosistema integrado. El modelo "Multi-Platform Orchestration" significa: cada plataforma tiene un rol específico en tu customer journey, y deben trabajar juntas, no en competencia. Facebook es para retargeting y conversions, Instagram para awareness y consideration, LinkedIn para B2B y profesionales, TikTok para viral reach y Gen Z. La clave del éxito no es dominar una plataforma, sino orquestar múltiples plataformas en una estrategia cohesiva donde cada una hace lo que mejor sabe hacer.

## 1. Principios Fundamentales

> **P1: Cada plataforma tiene un superpoder diferente**
- Facebook: Retargeting y conversions (mejor pixel y data)
- Instagram: Awareness y visual storytelling
- LinkedIn: B2B y decision makers
- TikTok: Viral reach y Gen Z
> *Fuente: "Platform Superpowers" (Cardus, 2021)*

> **P2: El customer journey es multi-plataforma**
- Los usuarios no viven en una sola red social
- Un user puede verte en TikTok → Instagram → Facebook antes de comprar
- Tu estrategia debe ser multi-touch, no single-channel
> *Fuente: "Multi-Platform Journey" (Cardus, 2021)*

> **P3: La coherencia de marca es crucial cross-plataforma**
- Tu brand voice debe ser consistente en todas partes
- Visual identity coherente (colores, fonts, style)
- Pero adaptá el formato al native style de cada plataforma
> *Fuente: "Brand Coherence" (Cardus, 2021)*

> **P4: No intentés estar en todas partes desde el inicio**
- Mejor dominar 1-2 plataformas que ser mediocre en 5
- Empezá con donde tu target pasa más tiempo
- Expandí a otras plataformas cuando tengás profitability
> *Fuente: "Platform Focus" (Cardus, 2020)*

> **P5: El tracking cross-plataforma es esencial**
- Necesitás trackear el customer journey completo
- UTMs, tags, y attribution por plataforma
- Sin data, estás flying blind
> *Fuente: "Cross-Platform Tracking" (Cardus, 2021)*

## 2. Frameworks y Metodologías

### Framework 1: Social Media Platform Selection Matrix

**Fuente:** "Platform Selection Matrix" (Cardus, 2021)
**Propósito:** Elegir las plataformas correctas para tu negocio.
**Cuándo usar:** Al planificar estrategia de social media ads.

**The Matrix:**

| Plataforma | Target Age | Funnel Stage | Budget Needed | Creative Style | Best For |
|------------|------------|--------------|---------------|----------------|----------|
| **Facebook** | 30-60+ | Bottom (retargeting, conversions) | Medium | Conversational, value-driven | Retargeting, lead gen, sales |
| **Instagram** | 18-45 | Top-Mid (awareness, consideration) | Medium-High | Visual, aesthetic, aspirational | Brand awareness, product showcase |
| **LinkedIn** | 25-60 | Mid-Bottom (consideration, conversions) | High | Professional, B2B-focused | B2B, professional services |
| **TikTok** | 13-35 | Top (awareness, viral reach) | Low-Medium | Entertainment, native, authentic | Viral reach, Gen Z, trends |
| **YouTube** | 18-50 | Mid-Bottom (consideration, conversions) | High | Video, storytelling, educational | Video ads, tutorials, demos |
| **Twitter/X** | 25-50 | Top-Mid (awareness, engagement) | Low | Conversational, news-y, real-time | Real-time marketing, announcements |
| **Pinterest** | 25-55 | Top-Mid (awareness, consideration) | Medium | Visual, inspirational, planning | E-commerce, decor, food, fashion |

**Selection Framework:**

**Step 1: Define tu target audience**
- Age, gender, location, interests
- B2C vs B2B
- Consumer behavior (online habits)

**Step 2: Define tu funnel stage priority**
- Top (awareness): Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest
- Mid (consideration): Instagram, YouTube, LinkedIn
- Bottom (conversions): Facebook, LinkedIn, YouTube

**Step 3: Define tu budget**
- <$500/month: Facebook o TikTok (mejor ROI con poco budget)
- $500-2000/month: Facebook + Instagram
- $2000-5000/month: Facebook + Instagram + LinkedIn o TikTok
- $5000+/month: Full multi-platform strategy

**Step 4: Define tu product type**
- Visual products (fashion, beauty): Instagram, Pinterest
- B2B services: LinkedIn + Facebook retargeting
- E-commerce: Facebook + Instagram + Pinterest
- Apps/software: Facebook + TikTok + YouTube

**Key Rule:** Para la mayoría de negocios B2C en mercado hispano, empezá con Facebook + Instagram. Expandí a TikTok o LinkedIn cuando tengás campaigns profitability.

### Framework 2: Multi-Platform Campaign Orchestration

**Fuente:** "Campaign Orchestration" (Cardus, 2021)
**Propósito:** Coordinar campaigns cross-plataforma.
**Cuándo usar:** Al ejecutar campaigns en múltiples plataformas.

**The Orchestration Model:**

**Layer 1: Top Funnel (Awareness) - Instagram + TikTok**

**Objetivo:** Generar awareness y capturar attention

**Strategy:**
- **Instagram:** Feed posts, Stories, Reels showcasing brand aesthetic
- **TikTok:** Trending content, educational videos, behind-the-scenes

**Budget allocation:** 40% del total

**Metrics:**
- Impressions, reach, brand awareness
- Engagement rate (likes, comments, shares)
- Profile visits, follows

**Layer 2: Mid Funnel (Consideration) - Instagram + YouTube + LinkedIn**

**Objetivo:** Build consideration y capture leads

**Strategy:**
- **Instagram:** Carousel ads, product demos, influencer content
- **YouTube:** Video ads demonstrating product value
- **LinkedIn (B2B):** Document ads, thought leadership content

**Budget allocation:** 30% del total

**Metrics:**
- Website clicks, landing page visits
- Lead magnet downloads
- Time on site, pages per session

**Layer 3: Bottom Funnel (Conversions) - Facebook Retargeting**

**Objetivo:** Drive conversions y sales

**Strategy:**
- **Facebook Retargeting:** Users who visited site, engaged with content
- **Offer:** Discount, urgency, or bonus
- **Creative:** Product-focused, clear CTA

**Budget allocation:** 30% del total

**Metrics:**
- Conversions, sales, ROAS
- Cost per acquisition (CPA)
- Return on ad spend (ROAS)

**Key Insight:** No intentés vender directo en top funnel (Instagram/TikTok). Usa those platforms para awareness, luego cerrá la venta en bottom funnel (Facebook retargeting).

### Framework 3: Creative Adaptation Cross-Platform

**Fuente:** "Creative Adaptation" (Cardus, 2020)
**Propósito:** Adaptar creativos para cada plataforma.
**Cuándo usar:** Al crear content para multiple platforms.

**The Adaptation Framework:**

**Core Message (Same across platforms):**
- Value proposition
- Key benefits
- Brand voice

**Format Adaptation (Different per platform):**

**Facebook:**
- Format: Horizontal (1:1) o Square (4:5)
- Style: Conversational, value-driven
- Length: 15-60 seconds video, o static image
- Text overlay: Minimal
- Caption: Medium-long (can tell a story)

**Instagram:**
- Format: Square/Vertical (1:1, 4:5, 9:16)
- Style: Visual, aesthetic, aspirational
- Length: 15-30 seconds (Stories), 60+ seconds (Feed)
- Text overlay: Minimal, elegant
- Caption: Short-medium (visual-first)

**LinkedIn:**
- Format: Horizontal (1.91:1)
- Style: Professional, business-focused
- Length: 30-60 seconds
- Text overlay: Professional, statistics-driven
- Caption: Long-form (can tell detailed story)

**TikTok:**
- Format: Vertical (9:16)
- Style: Native, authentic, entertaining
- Length: 15-30 seconds (sweet spot)
- Text overlay: Bold, big font
- Caption: Short (let the video speak)

**Example: Product Launch**

**Core message:** "Product X saves you 2 hours daily"

**Facebook adaptation:**
- Video: Customer testimonial talking about Product X
- Style: Conversational, authentic
- CTA: "Learn how Product X can help you"

**Instagram adaptation:**
- Carousel: Before/after with Product X
- Style: Visual, aesthetic
- CTA: "See the transformation (swipe →)"

**LinkedIn adaptation:**
- Document ad: "5 Ways Product X Increases Productivity"
- Style: Professional, data-driven
- CTA: "Download the full case study"

**TikTok adaptation:**
- Video: POV: "When your boss asks how you finished everything by 3pm"
- Style: Humor + Product X demo
- CTA: "Link in bio to get Product X"

**Key Rule:** Same message, different format. No repurposes sin adaptación.

## 3. Modelos Mentales

### Modelo Mental 1: The Customer Journey Web

**Fuente:** "Customer Journey Web" (Cardus, 2021)
**El modelo:** El customer journey es un web, no un funnel lineal.

**The Web Model:**

```
Instagram Ad
    ↓
Website Visit
    ↓
Facebook Retargeting (no purchase)
    ↓
TikTok Ad (awareness renewed)
    ↓
Website Visit (second time)
    ↓
LinkedIn Ad (B2B consideration)
    ↓
Purchase (finally)
```

**Key Insight:** Unlike the traditional funnel (awareness → consideration → purchase in a linear path), real customer journeys are non-linear and multi-platform.

**Implication:** Tu attribution model debe credititar múltiples touchpoints, no just the last click. If a customer sees you on 3 platforms before purchasing, all 3 contributed to the sale.

### Modelo Mental 2: The Platform Flywheel

**Fuente:** "Platform Flywheel" (Cardus, 2020)
**El modelo:** Las plataformas se refuerzan mutuamente.

**The Flywheel Effect:**

**Phase 1: TikTok/Instagram (Awareness)**
- Genera awareness masivo
- Crea interest y curiosity
- User profile visit (follow)

**Phase 2: YouTube/LinkedIn (Consideration)**
- User searches for your brand
- Watches videos explaining your product
- Reads reviews and case studies
- Lead magnet download

**Phase 3: Facebook Retargeting (Conversion)**
- User sees retargeting ads
- Gets reminded of interest
- Receives discount or urgency
- Makes purchase

**Phase 4: Feedback Loop**
- Customer becomes advocate
- Leaves reviews, creates UGC
- Organic social sharing
- Fuels Phase 1 (awareness from social proof)

**Key Insight:** Each platform feeds the next. TikTok creates awareness, YouTube builds consideration, Facebook closes the sale, and user-generated content fuels more awareness.

### Modelo Mental 3: The Budget Optimization Model

**Fuente:** "Budget Optimization" (Cardus, 2021)
**El modelo:** Optimización de budget cross-plataforma.

**The Optimization Framework:**

**Month 1: Testing Phase ($1000 total)**
- Facebook: $400 (40%) - Testing baseline
- Instagram: $400 (40%) - Testing awareness
- TikTok: $200 (20%) - Testing viral reach

**Month 2: Optimization Phase ($2000 total)**
- Analyze results from Month 1
- Kill platforms with CPA > 2x target
- Scale platforms with CPA < target

**Example allocation after optimization:**
- Facebook: $1000 (50%) - Scale retargeting
- Instagram: $800 (40%) - Scale awareness
- TikTok: $200 (10%) - Maintain presence

**Month 3: Scaling Phase ($3000 total)**
- Double down on winners
- Maintain presence in non-performers for brand visibility
- Add new platform if opportunity

**Example allocation after scaling:**
- Facebook: $1500 (50%) - Aggressive scaling
- Instagram: $1200 (40%) - Aggressive scaling
- LinkedIn: $300 (10%) - New B2B channel testing

**Key Rule:** The 40/40/20 rule for initial testing: 40% main platform (Facebook), 40% secondary (Instagram), 20% experimental (TikTok/LinkedIn/etc.). Adjust based on performance.

## 4. Criterios de Decisión

### DC-1: ¿Cuántas plataformas usar simultáneamente?

**Usá 1 plataforma cuando:**
- Budget es <$500/month
- Tu target es muy específico y concentrado en una red
- Tu product es new y need validation first
- Tu team es small (no bandwidth para múltiples)

**Usá 2 plataformas cuando:**
- Budget es $500-2000/month
- Tu target usa activamente 2-3 plataformas
- Tu product is validated y ready para scaling
- Tu team puede manejar 2-3 plataformas

**Usá 3+ plataformas cuando:**
- Budget es >$2000/month
- Tu target está disperso across multiple platforms
- Tu product is established y necesita maximum reach
- Tenés team o agency para manejar multiple platforms

**Source:** "Platform Count Decision" (Cardus, 2021)

### DC-2: ¿Cómo medir ROI cross-plataforma?

**Step 1: Implement tracking completo**
- UTMs en todos los links por plataforma
- Facebook Pixel + LinkedIn Insight Tag + TikTok Pixel
- Google Analytics para cross-platform tracking

**Step 2: Attribution modeling**
- First-touch attribution (qué plataforma generó el lead)
- Last-touch attribution (qué plataforma cerró la venta)
- Multi-touch attribution (distribuye crédito across platforms)

**Step 3: Calculate platform-specific ROI**
- Revenue attributed to platform / Ad spend on platform
- Compare ROAS across platforms
- Identify underperformers and overperformers

**Step 4: Optimize based on data**
- Shift budget from low-ROAS to high-ROAS platforms
- But keep minimum presence on all platforms (brand visibility)
- Re-evaluate monthly

**Source:** "Cross-Platform Attribution" (Cardus, 2021)

### DC-3: ¿Cuándo unified creative vs platform-specific?

**Unified creative (same creative everywhere):**
- Pros: Saves time and money, consistent messaging
- Cons: Poor fit for some platforms, lower engagement
- Best for: Limited budget, simple products, short campaigns

**Platform-specific creative (adapted for each platform):**
- Pros: Better fit, higher engagement, native feel
- Cons: More expensive, time-consuming, complex coordination
- Best for: Sufficient budget, complex products, long-term campaigns

**Recommendation:** Start with unified creative for testing. Once validated, invest in platform-specific creatives for scaling.

**Source:** "Creative Strategy" (Cardus, 2020)

## 5. Anti-patrones

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 1: Cross-posting identical content everywhere**
- **El problema:** Same post on Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, TikTok
- **El costo:** Poor performance, non-native feel, low engagement
- **la solución:** Adapt format and style to each platform (same message, different delivery)
> *Fuente: "Cross-Posting Mistake" (Cardus, 2021)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 2: Ignorando el customer journey multi-plataforma**
- **El problema:** Asumiendo que un usuario convierte en el primer touchpoint
- **El costo:** Poor attribution, bad budget allocation decisions
- **la solución:** Track multi-touch journeys, credititar todas las plataformas que contribuyeron
> *Fuente: "Single-Touch Attribution" (Cardus, 2020)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 3: Spending evenly across platforms sin data**
- **El problema:** "Voy a poner $500 en cada plataforma y ver qué pasa"
- **El costo:** Wasted money on underperforming platforms
- **la solución:** Start with 40/40/20 rule, then reallocate based on performance data
> *Fuente: "Equal Allocation Mistake" (Cardus, 2021)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 4: Switching platforms too frequently**
- **El problema:** "TikTok no funcionó en 2 semanas, paso a LinkedIn"
- **El costo:** Never giving any platform enough time to work
- **la solución:** Minimum 60-90 days per platform before declaring success/failure
> *Fuente: "Platform Hopping" (Cardus, 2020)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 5: No having a consistent brand voice cross-platform**
- **El problema:** Brand sounds different on Instagram vs LinkedIn vs TikTok
- **El costo:** Confusing brand identity, weaker brand recognition
- **la solución:** Define brand voice guidelines, adapt but don't change personality
> *Fuente: "Inconsistent Brand" (Cardus, 2021)*
