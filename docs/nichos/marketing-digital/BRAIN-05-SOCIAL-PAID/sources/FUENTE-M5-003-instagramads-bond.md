# FUENTE-M5-003: The Ad Strategist — Amanda Bond

## Tesis Central

> Instagram es visual-first. El modelo "Scroll-Stopping Creative" significa: tu anuncio debe ser visualmente impactante en el primer segundo (stop the scroll), comunicar valor rápido (3 seconds), y tener un CTA claro (conversion). Instagram users expect high-quality aesthetics, pero también authenticity. La paradoja de Instagram Ads: lo que funciona en Feed (polished) no funciona en Stories (crudo), y lo que funciona en Stories no funciona en Reels (entertainment). Cada placement needs su propio creative approach.

## 1. Principios Fundamentales

> **P1: El primer segundo lo es todo**
- Instagram users scan 100+ posts por minute
- Tenés 1 segundo para capturar attention
- Sin un visual impactante, el scroll sigue
> *Fuente: "The One-Second Rule" (Bond, 2021)*

> **P2: Instagram es visual storytelling, no copywriting**
- El texto secundario, la imagen/video es primary
- Long copy en Instagram = death by scroll
- Show, don't tell
> *Fuente: "Visual-First Philosophy" (Bond, 2020)*

> **P3: Cada placement es un canal diferente**
- Feed: Static/pollished, square format
- Stories: Vertical, raw/authentic, 15s loops
- Reels: Entertainment-first, 15-60s, trending audio
- Shopping: Product-focused, seamless checkout
> *Fuente: "Placement Strategy" (Bond, 2021)*

> **P4: La estética de marca importa más que en Facebook**
- Instagram users punish ugly ads with instant skips
- Tu creative debe align con Instagram aesthetic standards
- Polished visuals = higher CTR en Feed
> *Fuente: "Instagram Aesthetic Standards" (Bond, 2020)*

> **P5: UGC funciona, pero tiene que ser GOOD UGC**
- Not all user-generated content performs igual
- Good UGC = authentic pero bien producido
- Bad UGC = amateur, baja tu brand perception
> *Fuente: "UGC Quality Spectrum" (Bond, 2021)*

## 2. Frameworks y Metodologías

### Framework 1: The Scroll-Stopper Formula

**Fuente:** "Scroll-Stopping Creative Formula" (Bond, 2021)
**Propósito:** Crear creativos Instagram que detengan el scroll.
**Cuándo usar:** Al diseñar Instagram Ads para cualquier placement.

**The 3-Second Rule:**

**Second 0-1: The Visual Hook**
Your image/video must have:
- **High contrast colors:** Bright colors, bold combinations
- **Faces or human elements:** Eyes create connection
- **Movement in video:** First frame must be dynamic
- **Text overlay bold:** 3-5 words max, big font

**Examples of Hooks:**
- Before/after transformation (side-by-side)
- Bold statement text ("STOP SCROLLING")
- Celebrity or influencer face
- Product in use, not just product shot

**Second 1-3: The Value Proposition**

Comunicá rápidamente:
- **What:** What is this about?
- **Why:** Why should I care?
- **How:** How does it help me?

**Visual techniques:**
- Text overlay with bullet points
- Demo showing the product working
- Lifestyle shot showing the benefit
- Testimonial snippet

**Second 3+: The CTA**

Clear next step:
- "Shop Now" button
- "Learn More" link
- "Sign Up" form
- "Swipe Up" (for accounts with 10k+ followers)

**Key Rule:** If your creative doesn't stop the scroll in second 1, the rest doesn't matter.

### Framework 2: Instagram Placement Strategy

**Fuente:** "Placement-Specific Creative" (Bond, 2021)
**Propósito:** Adaptar creativos para cada placement de Instagram.
**Cuándo usar:** Al crear campaigns con multiple placements activados.

**Placement 1: Instagram Feed (Static Images, Carousels, Video)**

**Best for:** Brand awareness, consideration

**Creative specs:**
- Format: Square (1:1) or Portrait (4:5)
- Style: Polished, aesthetic, high-quality
- Length: Static image, or 15-60s video
- Text overlay: Minimal, elegant

**Winning creative elements:**
- Clean composition
- Consistent filters/color palette
- Product as hero or lifestyle integration
- Minimal text overlay (optional)

**Performance benchmarks:**
- CTR: 1.5-3%
- CPC: $0.50-$1.50
- Best for: Top-mid funnel

**Placement 2: Instagram Stories (Vertical Video/Static)**

**Best for:** Conversions, retargeting

**Creative specs:**
- Format: Vertical (9:16)
- Style: Authentic, raw, behind-the-scenes
- Length: 15s loops (auto-plays on loop)
- Interactive elements: Polls, questions, stickers

**Winning creative elements:**
- First frame dynamic (movement, text)
- Direct, conversational tone
- Native stickers (link, poll, question)
- Vertical video with face-to-camera

**Performance benchmarks:**
- Tap-forward rate: < 20% (lower is better)
- Tap-through rate: > 2%
- Best for: Mid-bottom funnel

**Placement 3: Instagram Reels (Vertical Video, Trending Audio)**

**Best for:** Reach, brand awareness, viral potential

**Creative specs:**
- Format: Vertical (9:16)
- Style: Entertainment-first, fast-paced
- Length: 15-60s (sweet spot: 15-30s)
- Audio: Trending sounds or custom audio

**Winning creative elements:**
- Fast cuts, dynamic editing
- Trending audio or sounds
- Educational or entertaining content
- Native Reels style (not repurposed ads)

**Performance benchmarks:**
- Views: > 10,000 for $100 spend (good start)
- Engagement rate: > 5%
- Best for: Top funnel awareness

**Placement 4: Instagram Shopping (Product Catalog Ads)**

**Best for:** E-commerce conversions

**Creative specs:**
- Format: Square or portrait, product-focused
- Style: Clean product photography, lifestyle integration
- Features: Product tags, checkout integration
- Price display: Visible price (increases conversion)

**Winning creative elements:**
- Product as hero (clear, well-lit)
- Lifestyle context (product in use)
- Price visible (reduces friction)
- Multiple product angles (carousel)

**Performance benchmarks:**
- CTR: 2-4%
- Conversion rate: 1-3%
- Best for: Bottom funnel conversions

### Framework 3: The Instagram Aesthetic System

**Fuente:** "Instagram Aesthetic Standards" (Bond, 2020)
**Propósito:** Crear una estética de marca coherente para Instagram.
**Cuándo usar:** Al desarrollar brand identity para Instagram Ads.

**The 5 Elements of Instagram Aesthetic:**

**1. Color Palette (Base identity)**
- Primary brand colors (2-3 colors)
- Secondary accent colors (1-2 colors)
- Filters/presets consistent across all content
- Tools: Adobe Color, Coolors, Canva Pro

**2. Typography (Visual hierarchy)**
- Primary font (headlines, bold)
- Secondary font (body text, readable)
- Text overlay rules: max 3-5 words for headlines
- Font pairing: contrast but complementary

**3. Photography Style (Visual language)**
- Product photography: Studio vs lifestyle vs user-generated
- Tone: Minimalist vs colorful vs moody
- Composition: Centered vs rule-of-thirds vs asymmetrical
- Lighting: Natural vs studio vs dramatic

**4. Video Style (Motion identity)**
- Editing style: Fast cuts vs smooth transitions
- Animation: Motion graphics vs live-action vs mixed
- Music: Trending vs custom vs instrumental
- Intro/outro: Branded intros vs jump straight into content

**5. Grid Layout (Overall aesthetic)**
- Pattern: Checkerboard, rows, columns, or freeform
- Theme: Color-coded, product-focused, or lifestyle
- Consistency: Every post should fit the grid aesthetic
- Tools: Planoly, Later, Preview for grid planning

**Key Rule:** Consistency builds recognition. If your feed looks chaotic, users won't remember your brand.

## 3. Modelos Mentales

### Modelo Mental 1: The Instagram Funnel

**Fuente:** "Instagram User Journey" (Bond, 2021)
**El modelo:** Los usuarios de Instagram pasan por 4 stages de engagement.

**Stage 1: Discovery (Top Funnel)**
- User sees your content in Feed/Explore/Reels
- Goal: Stop the scroll, generate interest
- Metrics: Impressions, reach, profile visits
- Best placements: Feed, Reels, Explore

**Stage 2: Consideration (Mid Funnel)**
- User visits your profile, checks your highlights
- Goal: Provide value, build trust
- Metrics: Profile visits, website clicks, saves
- Best placements: Stories, Feed highlights

**Stage 3: Conversion (Bottom Funnel)**
- User clicks through to website or makes purchase
- Goal: Drive action
- Metrics: Clicks, conversions, purchases
- Best placements: Stories with link, Shopping

**Stage 4: Retention (Post-Purchase)**
- User becomes follower, repeat customer
- Goal: Build community, loyalty
- Metrics: Follower growth, engagement rate, retention
- Best placements: All (organic + paid)

**Key Insight:** Different placements work better for different funnel stages. Don't expect Reels (top funnel) to convert like Shopping ads (bottom funnel).

### Modelo Mental 2: The Content Quality Pyramid

**Fuente:** "Creative Quality Hierarchy" (Bond, 2020)
**El modelo:** No todo el contenido se crea igual. Hay una jerarquía clara.

**Base: UGC Crudo (User-Generated Content)**
- Definition: Authentic content from real users
- Production value: Low (cell phone, natural light)
- Performance: High authenticity, variable polish
- Best for: Stories, Reels, engagement

**Middle: Semi-Pro (Balance)**
- Definition: Professional but authentic-looking
- Production value: Medium (good lighting, basic editing)
- Performance: Good authenticity + good polish
- Best for: Feed, Stories, conversions

**Top: High-End Production**
- Definition: Agency-quality, highly polished
- Production value: High (studio, professional editing)
- Performance: High polish, lower authenticity perception
- Best for: Brand campaigns, Shopping, luxury

**Key Insight:** Instagram rewards BOTH authenticity (UGC) and polish (production). The sweet spot is semi-pro: authentic-looking but well-produced.

### Modelo Mental 3: The Trend Adoption Curve

**Fuente:** "Instagram Trends Lifecycle" (Bond, 2021)
**El modelo:** Las tendencias de Instagram tienen un ciclo de vida predecible.

**Phase 1: Emerging (Innovators only)**
- New feature, audio, or format just launched
- Risk: Unproven, may not work
- Reward: First-mover advantage, lower costs
- Duration: 1-2 weeks

**Phase 2: Trending (Early adopters jump in)**
- Feature/audio is gaining popularity
- Risk: Competition increasing
- Reward: Proven format, still reasonable costs
- Duration: 2-4 weeks

**Phase 3: Saturated (Everyone is doing it)**
- Feature/audio is everywhere, overused
- Risk: High costs, diminishing returns
- Reward: Still works but less effectively
- Duration: 4-8 weeks

**Phase 4: Dead (Move on)**
- Feature/audio is passé, users tune out
- Risk: Wasted budget, low performance
- Reward: None (avoid)
- Duration: Permanent

**Key Insight:** Jump on trends in Phase 2 (trending but not saturated). Too early (Phase 1) = unproven. Too late (Phase 3-4) = expensive and ineffective.

**Action Framework:**
1. Monitor Instagram trends weekly (Reels, audio, features)
2. Test trending formats in Phase 2 with small budget
3. Scale winners quickly before saturation
4. Kill formats when they reach saturation (Phase 3)

## 4. Criterios de Decisión

### DC-1: ¿Cuándo usar Reels vs Feed vs Stories?

**Usá Reels cuando:**
- Goal es reach y brand awareness
- Tenés entertaining or educational content
- Tu product works well con video demonstrations
- Budget es limitado (Reels CPMs still lower than Feed)

**Usá Feed cuando:**
- Goal es consideration y brand perception
- Tenés beautiful product photography o polished visuals
- Tu product es aesthetic (fashion, beauty, home decor)
- Budget permite higher CPMs por premium placement

**Usá Stories cuando:**
- Goal es conversions (especially retargeting)
- Tenés authentic, behind-the-scenes content
- Tenés a strong call-to-action (link, sticker)
- Tu audience está activo en Stories (check insights)

**Source:** "Placement Selection Framework" (Bond, 2021)

### DC-2: ¿Cuándo usar UGC vs Production Content?

**Usá UGC (user-generated style) cuando:**
- Tu product es consumer-focused, not luxury
- Tu brand values authenticity over polish
- Goal es engagement and relatability
- Testing new creatives (lower production cost)

**Usá Production Content cuando:**
- Tu product es premium or luxury
- Tu brand needs polish and professionalism
- Goal es brand perception and trust
- Scaling winning UGC concepts with better quality

**Source:** "Creative Style Decision" (Bond, 2020)

### DC-3: ¿Cuándo invertir en Instagram vs other platforms?

**Invertí en Instagram Ads cuando:**
- Tu product es visual (fashion, beauty, food, travel, fitness)
- Tu target audience is 18-44 years old
- Tu brand fits Instagram aesthetic (lifestyle-focused)
- Tenés high-quality visuals or can produce UGC

**Consider other platforms when:**
- Tu product is B2B (LinkedIn may be better)
- Tu target is older demographics (Facebook may be better)
- Tu product isn't visual (text-heavy, complex software)
- Tu brand doesn't fit Instagram culture

**Source:** "Platform Selection Criteria" (Bond, 2021)

## 5. Anti-patrones

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 1: Using the same creative across placements**
- **El problema:** Feed ad in Stories looks like an ad (instant skip)
- **El costo:** Low engagement, high CPA
- **La solución:** Create placement-specific creatives (vertical for Stories, square for Feed)
> *Fuente: "One-Size-Fits-None" (Bond, 2021)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 2: Text-heavy Instagram creatives**
- **El problema:** Long copy overlays cover the visual, ruin the aesthetic
- **El costo:** Users skip past text-heavy ads
- **La solución:** Maximum 3-5 words for text overlay, put copy in caption
> *Fuente: "Text-Overlay Overload" (Bond, 2020)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 3: Ignoring Instagram aesthetic standards**
- **El problema:** Ugly or low-quality creatives damage brand perception
- **El costo:** Low CTR, negative brand association
- **La solución:** Maintain consistent aesthetic, invest in good visuals
> *Fuente: "Aesthetic Negligence" (Bond, 2021)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 4: Posting ads without engaging with comments**
- **El problema:** Ads with unanswered comments look abandoned
- **El costo:** Lost trust, lower conversion rates
- **La solución:** Assign someone to engage with ad comments daily
> *Fuente: "Comment Engagement Gap" (Bond, 2020)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 5: Jumping on trends too late (saturation)**
- **El problema:** Using trending audio/format after everyone else has used it
- **El costo:** Ad looks unoriginal, users scroll past
- **la solución:** Monitor trends weekly, jump in during Phase 2 (trending but not saturated)
> *Fuente: "Late Trend Adoption" (Bond, 2021)*
