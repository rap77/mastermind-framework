# FUENTE-M5-005: LinkedIn Ads — AJ Wilcox

## Tesis Central

> LinkedIn es diferente. El modelo "B2B Intent Advertising" significa: LinkedIn users no están para entretenerse, están para networking y career growth. Tu anuncio debe respetar ese contexto: professional, value-driven, y non-disruptive. La clave del éxito en LinkedIn Ads es targeting preciso por job title + company + industry, combinado con content que habla a business pain points, no consumer desires. LinkedIn CPMs son 5-10x más altos que Facebook, pero el value de un B2B lead es 10-100x mayor. No comparés LinkedIn con Facebook; son herramientas para不同的 objectives.

## 1. Principios Fundamentales

> **P1: LinkedIn es B2B-first, no B2C**
- Users están en mindset professional, no entertainment
- Tu anuncio debe align con ese context
- Consumer-focused ads fail on LinkedIn
> *Fuente: "B2B Mindset" (Wilcox, 2021)*

> **P2: LinkedIn targeting es el mejor del mundo para B2B**
- Job titles, companies, industries, skills
- No hay platform con targeting B2B más preciso
- Pero targeting sin buen mensaje = wasted money
> *Fuente: "LinkedIn Targeting Superiority" (Wilcox, 2020)*

> **P3: LinkedIn CPMs son altas, pero el valor también**
- Average CPM: $30-$80 (vs $5-$15 on Facebook)
- Pero B2B lead value: $500-$10,000+ (vs $50-$200 for B2C)
- Focus on ROI, not absolute CPA
> *Fuente: "LinkedIn CPM Reality" (Wilcox, 2021)*

> **P4: El formato Sponsored Content es el king de LinkedIn**
- Native in-feed format, no disruptive
- Best balance of reach and engagement
- Carousel y Document Ads funcionan muy bien
> *Fuente: "Sponsored Content Dominance" (Wilcox, 2020)*

> **P5: LinkedIn's learning phase es MUY lento**
- Necesita 7-14 days para optimizar (vs 3 days for Facebook)
- No hagas cambios constantes
- La paciencia es critical en LinkedIn
> *Fuente: "LinkedIn Learning Speed" (Wilcox, 2021)*

## 2. Frameworks y Metodologías

### Framework 1: LinkedIn Ad Format Selection

**Fuente:** "LinkedIn Ad Formats Guide" (Wilcox, 2021)
**Propósito:** Elegir el formato correcto de LinkedIn Ads.
**Cuándo usar:** Al configurar campaigns en LinkedIn Campaign Manager.

**Format 1: Sponsored Content (Single Image) - RECOMMENDED**

**Best for:** Awareness, consideration, lead gen

**Specs:**
- Format: Horizontal (1.91:1) or Square (1:1)
- Style: Professional, clean, business-focused
- Text: 600 characters (intro) + 300 characters (headline)
- CTA: "Sign Up", "Learn More", "Register", etc.

**Winning elements:**
- Professional photography or clean graphics
- Value-driven headline (not clickbait)
- Company logo visible (builds trust)
- Clear business benefit

**Performance benchmarks:**
- CTR: 0.3-0.7% (lower than social, but higher quality)
- CPC: $5-$15
- Best for: Most B2B use cases

**Format 2: Sponsored Content (Carousel) - HIGHLY RECOMMENDED**

**Best for:** Storytelling, multi-product campaigns

**Specs:**
- Format: Horizontal cards (swipeable)
- Cards: 2-10 cards per carousel
- Style: Visual storytelling with each card

**Winning elements:**
- Card 1: Hook (problem statement)
- Cards 2-8: Value/buildup
- Final card: CTA
- Consistent visual theme across cards

**Performance benchmarks:**
- CTR: 0.5-1.0% (higher than single image)
- CPC: $4-$12
- Best for: Product launches, multi-feature campaigns

**Format 3: Sponsored Content (Document Ad) - TRENDING**

**Best for:** Lead magnets, whitepapers, guides

**Specs:**
- Format: Full document preview (like a lead magnet)
- Style: Professional document (PDF-style)
- CTA: "Download" (gates content with form)

**Winning elements:**
- Valuable content (not sales pitch)
- Professional design
- Clear preview of what user gets
- Low-friction form (name, email, company)

**Performance benchmarks:**
- CTR: 0.8-1.5% (highest of LinkedIn formats)
- Lead quality: High (self-selected interest)
- Best for: Lead generation, content marketing

**Format 4: Message Ads (Sponsored InMail) - USE SPARINGLY**

**Best for:** Direct outreach, account-based marketing

**Specs:**
- Format: Direct message to LinkedIn inbox
- Style: Personalized, conversational
- Limit: 1 message per user every 45 days

**Winning elements:**
- Personalized (not generic)
- Value-first (not sales pitch)
- Short (100-150 characters)
- Clear but non-pushy CTA

**Performance benchmarks:**
- Open rate: 30-50%
- Response rate: 5-15%
- Best for: ABM, high-value accounts, events

**Caution:** Can feel intrusive if overused. Use sparingly.

**Format 5: Text Ads - NOT RECOMMENDED**

**Best for:** (None, use Sponsored Content instead)

**Specs:**
- Format: Small text ad on right rail
- Very limited character count
- Poor performance overall

**Recommendation:** Avoid Text Ads. Sponsored Content is vastly superior.

### Framework 2: LinkedIn B2B Targeting Framework

**Fuente:** "LinkedIn Targeting Strategy" (Wilcox, 2020)
**Propósito:** Crear audiencias B2B de alta precisión.
**Cuándo usar:** Al configurar targeting para LinkedIn Ads.

**Tier 1: Job Title Targeting (Most Precise)**

**Use when:** Sabés exactamente quién es tu decision maker

**Examples:**
- "CTO" OR "Chief Technology Officer" OR "VP Engineering"
- "Marketing Director" OR "Head of Marketing"
- "HR Manager" OR "HR Director" OR "HRBP"

**Pros:** Most precise targeting available
**Cons:** Smaller audiences, higher CPMs

**Tier 2: Company Targeting (Account-Based Marketing)**

**Use when:** Targeting specific companies (ABM)

**Examples:**
- Company name: "Microsoft", "Google", "Amazon"
- Company size: 1,001-5,000 employees
- Company industry: "Software & Technology", "Financial Services"

**Pros:** Perfect for ABM, target high-value accounts
**Cons:** Very small audiences, high CPMs

**Tier 3: Skills & Groups (Interest-Based)**

**Use when:** Broadening reach while maintaining relevance

**Examples:**
- Skills: "Project Management", "Data Analysis", "SEO"
- LinkedIn Groups: "Digital Marketing Professionals", "SaaS Founders"
- Schools: "Harvard MBA", "Stanford CS"

**Pros:** Larger audiences, still relevant
**Cons:** Less precise than job titles

**Tier 4: Demographics (Broad Qualifying)**

**Use as:** Layer to qualify other targeting

**Examples:**
- Job seniority: "Director", "VP", "C-Level"
- Years of experience: 10+ years
- Location: United States, San Francisco Bay Area
- Company size: 201-500 employees

**Recommendation:** Use demographics as a layer, not standalone

**The Winning Formula:**

**Tier 1 + Tier 4 (Most Precise)**
- Example: Job titles (CTO, VP Engineering) + Seniority (Director+) + Location (US)
- Result: < 100,000 audience size
- Best for: High-value ABM campaigns

**Tier 2 + Tier 1 (ABM Hybrid)**
- Example: Companies (Fortune 500) + Job titles (Marketing Directors)
- Result: 50,000-200,000 audience size
- Best for: Enterprise B2B campaigns

**Tier 3 + Tier 4 (Broad but Relevant)**
- Example: Skills (Digital Marketing) + Seniority (Manager+) + Industry (Tech)
- Result: 200,000-1,000,000 audience size
- Best for: Awareness, lead generation campaigns

**Key Rule:** Audience size matters. Aim for 100,000-500,000 for Sponsored Content campaigns. Too small (< 50k) = high CPMs, fatigue. Too large (> 1M) = waste impressions.

### Framework 3: LinkedIn B2B Offer Strategy

**Fuente:** "B2B Offer Framework" (Wilcox, 2021)
**Propósito:** Crear ofertas que funcionen en LinkedIn B2B context.
**Cuándo usar:** Al diseñar campaigns de LinkedIn Ads.

**Offer Type 1: Lead Magnet (Top of Funnel)**

**Best for:** Awareness, list building

**Examples:**
- Whitepaper: "2024 State of B2B Marketing Report"
- Guide: "The Ultimate Guide to [Topic]"
- Template: "[Number] Templates for [Task]"
- Webinar: "Live Workshop: [Topic]"

**LinkedIn-specific advice:**
- Title must sound professional (not clickbait-y)
- Focus on business value, not entertainment
- Gate with simple form (name, email, company)
- Deliver immediately after form submission

**Offer Type 2: Trial or Demo (Middle of Funnel)**

**Best for:** Consideration, product-qualified leads

**Examples:**
- Free trial: "14-day free trial, no credit card"
- Demo request: "See [Product] in action"
- Consultation: "Free 30-minute strategy call"
- Assessment: "Get your free [Topic] assessment"

**LinkedIn-specific advice:**
- Make CTA clear (not "Learn More", use "Request Demo")
- Show social proof (logos, testimonials, case studies)
- Reduce friction (no long forms for trial signup)
- Follow up fast (within 5 minutes)

**Offer Type 3: Direct Sales (Bottom of Funnel)**

**Best for:** Retargeting, bottom-of-funnel campaigns

**Examples:**
- Discount: "20% off your first year"
- Bundle: "Get [Product A] + [Product B] for $X"
- Limited-time: "Offer ends [Date]"
- Upgrade: "Upgrade to Pro and save 30%"

**LinkedIn-specific advice:**
- Use for retargeting ONLY (not cold audiences)
- Make urgency authentic (not fake scarcity)
- Show business value (ROI case, calculator)
- Clear pricing (no "contact us for pricing")

**Key Rule:** Match offer to funnel stage. Don't push direct sales offers to cold audiences. Start with lead magnets, nurture, then sell.

## 3. Modelos Mentales

### Modelo Mental 1: The LinkedIn B2B Funnel

**Fuente:** "B2B Funnel Dynamics" (Wilcox, 2020)
**El modelo:** El B2B funnel es más largo y más complejo que B2C.

**The LinkedIn B2B Funnel:**

**Stage 1: Awareness (LinkedIn Sponsored Content)**
- Objective: Get noticed by decision makers
- Content: Thought leadership, industry insights
- Metrics: Impressions, reach, engagement
- LinkedIn format: Sponsored Content, Document Ads

**Stage 2: Consideration (Lead Magnets, Webinars)**
- Objective: Capture contact information
- Content: Gated content (whitepapers, guides)
- Metrics: Leads, cost per lead
- LinkedIn format: Document Ads, Lead Gen Forms

**Stage 3: Evaluation (Demos, Trials)**
- Objective: Product demonstration
- Content: Product tours, trial offers
- Metrics: Demo requests, trial signups
- LinkedIn format: Sponsored Content with demo CTA, Message Ads

**Stage 4: Purchase (Direct Sales)**
- Objective: Close the deal
- Content: Discount offers, bundles
- Metrics: Closed deals, revenue
- LinkedIn format: Retargeting campaigns (conversion ads)

**Key Insight:** B2B funnel takes 3-12 months (vs 3-7 days for B2C). LinkedIn Ads is most effective in Stages 1-3 (awareness, consideration, evaluation). Stage 4 (purchase) often happens offline with sales team.

**Implication:** Don't expect LinkedIn Ads to directly drive purchases. Use LinkedIn for top-of-funnel and mid-funnel, then hand off to sales team.

### Modelo Mental 2: The LinkedIn Quality-Quantity Tradeoff

**Fuente:** "LinkedIn ROI Equation" (Wilcox, 2021)
**El modelo:** LinkedIn is about quality over quantity.

**The Tradeoff:**

**Facebook (B2C):**
- Volume: High (millions of impressions)
- Cost per lead: Low ($5-$50)
- Lead quality: Low (mixed intent)
- Close rate: Low (1-5%)
- **ROI can be good with high volume**

**LinkedIn (B2B):**
- Volume: Lower (thousands of impressions)
- Cost per lead: High ($50-$500)
- Lead quality: High (professional intent)
- Close rate: High (10-30%)
- **ROI can be good with high quality**

**Key Insight:** Don't compare LinkedIn directly to Facebook on cost metrics. Compare on ROI (revenue generated, not leads generated).

**Example:**
- Facebook: 100 leads × $20/lead × 3% close × $1,000 avg deal = $6,000 revenue ($2,000 ad spend, 3x ROAS)
- LinkedIn: 20 leads × $100/lead × 20% close × $10,000 avg deal = $40,000 revenue ($2,000 ad spend, 20x ROAS)

**Result:** LinkedIn looks more expensive per lead, but generates more revenue overall.

### Modelo Mental 3: The LinkedIn Saturation Curve

**Fuente:** "LinkedIn Audience Saturation" (Wilcox, 2020)
**El modelo:** LinkedIn audiences saturate faster than other platforms.

**The Saturation Curve:**

**Week 1-2: Introduction Phase**
- Audience sees your ad for first time
- Performance: Good (fresh impressions)
- CTR: 0.5-0.8%
- Frequency: 1-2x per user

**Week 3-4: Recognition Phase**
- Audience starts to recognize your brand
- Performance: Stable
- CTR: 0.4-0.7%
- Frequency: 3-5x per user

**Week 5-8: Fatigue Phase**
- Audience has seen your ad multiple times
- Performance: Declining
- CTR: 0.2-0.4%
- Frequency: 6-10x per user

**Week 9+: Saturation Phase**
- Audience is bored with your creative
- Performance: Poor
- CTR: < 0.2%
- Frequency: 10+x per user

**Key Insight:** LinkedIn audiences saturate in 6-8 weeks (vs 12-16 weeks on Facebook). Why? Smaller audiences, professional context (users pay closer attention).

**Action:** Rotate creatives every 4-6 weeks on LinkedIn to prevent saturation.

**Strategy:**
1. Launch Creative A (Weeks 1-4)
2. Introduce Creative B, keep A running (Weeks 5-6)
3. Kill A once B is established, launch Creative C (Weeks 7-8)
4. Continue rotation

## 4. Criterios de Decisión

### DC-1: ¿Cuándo usar LinkedIn Ads vs other platforms?

**Usá LinkedIn Ads cuando:**
- Selling B2B products/services (SaaS, enterprise software, professional services)
- Target audience is professionals/decision makers
- Average deal size is $1,000+
- Sales cycle is longer than 1 month

**No usá LinkedIn Ads cuando:**
- Selling B2C products (fashion, food, entertainment)
- Target audience is consumers (not professionals)
- Average deal size is <$100
- Need immediate sales (same-day conversions)

**Source:** "Platform Selection Framework" (Wilcox, 2021)

### DC-2: ¿Cuándo usar Document Ads vs other formats?

**Usá Document Ads cuando:**
- Tenés valuable gated content (whitepaper, guide, report)
- Goal es lead generation (not just awareness)
- Content is professional and business-relevant
- Want highest CTR on LinkedIn

**Usá Single Image/Carousel cuando:**
- Goal es awareness or consideration (not lead gen)
- No gated content to offer
- Promoting events, products, or brand
- Visual storytelling is key

**Source:** "Format Selection Criteria" (Wilcox, 2021)

### DC-3: ¿Cuándo usar Message Ads (Sponsored InMail)?

**Usá Message Ads cuando:**
- Running account-based marketing (ABM) campaigns
- Targeting very specific, high-value accounts
- Have personalized, valuable message
- Frequency is low (1 campaign every 45+ days per user)

**No usé Message Ads cuando:**
- Running broad awareness campaigns
- Message is generic or salesy
- High frequency campaigns (will feel intrusive)
- Low budget (Message Ads have higher minimums)

**Source:** "Message Ads Guidelines" (Wilcox, 2020)

## 5. Anti-patrones

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 1: Using B2C creative on LinkedIn**
- **El problema:** Casual, entertainment-focused ads look out of place on LinkedIn
- **El costo:** Low engagement, wasted spend
- **la solución:** Keep it professional, value-driven, business-focused
> *Fuente: "B2C Creative Mistake" (Wilcox, 2021)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 2: Expecting Facebook-like CTRs on LinkedIn**
- **El problema:** Comparing LinkedIn 0.5% CTR to Facebook 2% CTR and concluding LinkedIn "doesn't work"
- **El costo:** Pausing profitable LinkedIn campaigns prematurely
- **la solución:** Compare on ROI, not CTR. LinkedIn leads are worth 10-100x more.
> *Fuente: "CTR Comparison Error" (Wilcox, 2020)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 3: Changing LinkedIn campaigns too frequently**
- **El problema:** Adjusting bids/budgets daily because performance isn't "perfect"
- **El costo:** Never letting LinkedIn's learning phase complete (7-14 days)
- **la solución:** Make maximum 1 change per week. Patiencia is critical on LinkedIn.
> *Fuente: "Learning Phase Interruption" (Wilcox, 2021)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 4: Targeting too broadly on LinkedIn**
- **El problema:** Targeting "all professionals" or broad industries without job titles
- **El costo:** Wasted impressions on non-decision-makers, high CPA
- **la solución:** Always layer job titles + seniority + industry. Precision over reach.
> *Fuente: "Broad Targeting Mistake" (Wilcox, 2020)*

> **❌ ANTI-PATTERN 5: Not following up on LinkedIn leads fast enough**
- **El problema:** Generating leads from LinkedIn but waiting days to follow up
- **El costo:** Leads go cold, response rates drop dramatically
- **la solución:** Follow up within 5 minutes (or at least within 1 hour). Speed matters.
> *Fuente: "Lead Response Time" (Wilcox, 2021)*
