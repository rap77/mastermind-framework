# FUENTE-168: Partnership Models — Partnership Framework

## Tesis Central

> **"True partnerships aren't just contracts—they're shared business outcomes. When agency and client have skin in the game together, magic happens. But that requires maturity on both sides and models that align incentives."**

---

## 1. Principios Fundamentales

> **P1: Partnership Requires Mutual Investment**
- If only one side invests, it's not a partnership—it's a vendor relationship.
> *Contexto: When evaluating partnership depth and authenticity.*

> **P2: Shared Risk Creates Shared Commitment**
- When you both have something to lose and gain, alignment is automatic.
> *Contexto: When structuring partnership agreements and incentives.*

> **P3: The Best Partnerships Have Exit Strategies**
- Partnerships should benefit both parties but have clear exit terms if misaligned.
> *Contexto: When designing partnership contracts and agreements.*

---

## 2. Frameworks y Metodologías

### Framework 1: The Partnership Spectrum

**Fuente:** [Partnership Models Framework (Partnership Experts, 2023)]

**Level 1: Vendor (Transactional)**
- **Relationship:** Project-based, arms-length
- **Pricing:** Hourly or project fee
- **Risk:** All on client, none on agency
- **Reward:** Fixed fee, no upside
- **Term:** Project or short-term retainer

**Level 2: Service Provider (Ongoing)**
- **Relationship:** Retainer, integrated but still vendor
- **Pricing:** Monthly retainer
- **Risk:** Mostly on client
- **Reward:** Fixed retainer, maybe small bonus
- **Term:** 6-12 month commitment

**Level 3: Strategic Partner (Aligned)**
- **Relationship:** Deep integration, shared strategy
- **Pricing:** Value-based + performance bonus (20-30% upside)
- **Risk:** Shared—agency bonuses on performance
- **Reward:** Base fee + performance upside
- **Term:** 1-2 year commitment

**Level 4: Growth Partner (Shared Destiny)**
- **Relationship:** Integrated business partnership
- **Pricing:** Performance-based (50%+ upside), equity stake
- **Risk:** Truly shared—agency invests time/resources, client pays on results
- **Reward:** Commission on revenue growth, equity, profit sharing
- **Term:** 3+ year commitment or indefinite

## 3. Modelos Mentales

### Modelo Mental 1: The Partnership Trust Bank

**El modelo:** Partnerships operate on a trust bank account. Positive interactions deposit, negative interactions withdraw. Successful partnerships maintain positive balance. When balance hits zero, partnership ends.
**Por qué funciona:** Explains why partnerships that start well can fail over time

---

## 4. Criterios de Decisión

### Trade-off: Equity Partnership vs. Cash Partnership

**Equity Partnership:**
- **Ventajas:** Maximum alignment, long-term commitment
- **Desventajas:** Complex, illiquid, misaligned timelines
- **Best for:** High-growth startups, long-term plays

**Cash Partnership (Performance-based):**
- **Ventajas:** Simpler, faster compensation, flexible
- **Desventajas:** Shorter-term, less alignment
- **Best for:** Established companies, cash-flow businesses

**Recomendación:** Start with performance-based cash. Add equity for exceptional, long-term partnerships.
