# FUENTE-161: Value-Based Pricing — Alan Weiss

## Tesis Central

> **"Stop selling time and start selling value. Clients don't care how long it takes you—they care about the business outcome. Price based on value delivered, not hours worked. That's the difference between vendor and partner."**

---

## 1. Principios Fundamentales

> **P1: The Buyer Doesn't Care About Your Costs**
- Clients pay for value received, not effort expended. Your costs are your problem, not theirs.
> *Contexto: When shifting from cost-plus to value-based pricing.*

> **P2: Price is Consequence of Value, Not Cause**
- Establish value first, then price. Most agencies do it backwards—price based on costs then try to justify value.
> *Contexto: When developing proposals and pricing models.*

> **P3: The Subconscious Value Calculation**
- Clients calculate: Value (perceived) ÷ Price (risk) = Purchase Decision. Increase perceived value, lower risk perception.
> *Contexto: When structuring proposals and pricing conversations.*

> **P4: No Two Clients Should Pay the Same Price**
- Each client's situation is different. Customized pricing based on specific value delivered maximizes revenue.
> *Contexto: When moving from rate cards to value-based proposals.*

> **P5: Money-Back Guarantee Reduces Risk More Than Discounting**
- Guaranteeing results reduces perceived risk more than lowering price. And protects you from bad-fit clients.
> *Contexto: When structuring pricing and proposals.*

---

## 2. Frameworks y Metodologías

### Framework 1: The Value-Based Fee Formula

**Fuente:** [Value-Based Pricing (Weiss, 2023)]

**The Formula:**
- **Value Created:** What is the business outcome worth to the client?
- **Investment Required:** What will the client invest to achieve that outcome?
- **Fee Range:** 10-50% of value created, depending on impact

**Example:**
- **Outcome:** Increase conversion rate from 2% to 3%
- **Revenue Impact:** $1M additional revenue
- **Value Created:** $1M (conservative estimate)
- **Fee Range:** $100K-$500K (10-50% of value)
- **Factors:** Urgency, importance, client capability, risk

**Implementation Steps:**
1. **Quantify Business Impact:** Convert outcome to dollars
2. **Assess Client Factors:** Urgency, importance, risk tolerance
3. **Determine Fee Percentage:** Where in 10-50% range based on factors
4. **Propose Fixed Fee:** Not hourly, not time-based—fixed fee for outcome
5. **Tie to Outcomes:** Success metrics, not deliverables

## 3. Modelos Mentales

### Modelo Mental 1: The Risk-Reward Balance

**El modelo:** Clients constantly assess risk vs reward in your pricing. High fee with guarantee often feels safer than low fee with no guarantee.
**Por qué funciona:** Explains why premium pricing with strong guarantee can outsell bargain pricing

---

## 4. Criterios de Decisión

### Trade-off: Hourly vs. Project vs. Value-Based

**Hourly:**
- **Ventajas:** Safe for agency, clear scope
- **Desventajas:** Limits income, misaligned incentives, vendor status

**Project:**
- **Ventajas:** Predictable revenue, some upside
- **Desventajas:** Scope creep risk, still linked to time

**Value-Based:**
- **Ventajas:** Maximized revenue, aligned incentives, partner status
- **Desventajas:** Requires confidence, sales skill

**Recomendación:** Move to value-based as quickly as agency maturity allows.
