# The Mom Test

**Rob Fitzpatrick**

## 1. Principios Fundamentales

> **P1 - La pregunta "¿Te gustaría esto?" no genera data útil**: Tu mamá diría que sí a cualquier cosa que propongas porque te quiere y no quiere lastimarte. Los usuarios potenciales hacen lo mismo: son educados, no quieren ser groseros. El Mom Test es sobre cómo conversar para obtener verdad, no validación falsa.

> **P2 - Los problemas son reales, las soluciones son opiniones**: La gente puede confiablemente decirte qué problemas tiene. No puede confiablemente decirte qué solución pagará. Descubre problemas primero, propone soluciones después.

> **P3 - Las conversaciones son para learning, no para pitching**: Si sales de una conversación hablando más del 50% del tiempo, fallaste. No estás vendiendo, estás aprendiendo. Haga preguntas, escuche respuestas.

> **P4 - El pasado es un hecho, el futuro es fantasía**: "¿Cuánto pagarías por X?" es fantasía. "¿Cuánto pagaste la última vez que compraste X?" es hecho. Basa tus decisiones en comportamiento histórico, no speculation futura.

> **P5 - La especificidad mata la mentira social**: "¿Es un problema?" → "Sí". "¿Cuándo fue la última vez que tuviste este problema? ¿Qué hiciste? ¿Cuánto costó solucionarlo?" → Historia específica, comportamental, no manipulable por cortesía.

## 2. Frameworks y Metodologías

### The Mom Test Rules

**Rule 1: Talk about their past behavior, not future opinions**
- ❌ "Would you use X?"
- ✅ "When was the last time you X?"

**Rule 2: Talk about specifics in the past, not generalities**
- ❌ "Do you have trouble with X?"
- ✅ "Tell me about the last time X happened"

**Rule 3: Listen more than you talk**
- Ideal: 10% you, 90% them
- If talking > 50%, you're pitching, not learning

### Good vs Bad Questions

| Bad Questions (Mom Test Fail) | Good Questions (Mom Test Pass) |
|-------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| "Do you think you would use X?" | "When was the last time you X?" |
| "Would you pay $10 for X?" | "How much did you pay last time you X?" |
| "Is X a problem for you?" | "Tell me about the last time X was a problem" |
| "What features would you like?" | "What are you currently using to solve X?" |
| "Do you think this is a good idea?" | "How do you currently solve X?" |

### The Conversation Flow

```
1. Build rapport (brief, friendly)
2. Ask about their workflow (context)
3. Dig into pain points (specific stories)
4. Ask about current solutions (workarounds)
5. Explore the cost of the problem (money/time/emotion)
6. Thank and wrap up
```

**Never**: Pitch, explain solution, ask for opinion on your idea.

### Finding the Problem

**Signal (good problem indicator)**:
- "I just spent $200 on X solution"
- "I tried 3 different things and none worked"
- "I spent 4 hours yesterday dealing with this"

**Noise (bad problem indicator)**:
- "I guess it would be nice to have"
- "Sure, I might use it"
- "Yeah, that sounds cool"

**Red flag**: "Sure" = "I'm being polite, go away".

### The 3 Key Questions

Every customer conversation should answer:

1. **What problem are they trying to solve?**
   - Specific stories, not generalities
   - Context: When, where, how often

2. **How are they currently solving it?**
   - Existing solutions (competitors)
   - Workarounds (spreadsheets, manual processes)
   - "Good enough" solutions

3. **How much is it costing them?**
   - Money spent on existing solutions
   - Time spent on workarounds
   - Emotional cost (frustration, stress)

### Segmentation by Problem Intensity

```
    ┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
    │   Aware but not searching          │ ← Not ready yet
    │ "I guess I have this problem..."  │
    ├─────────────────────────────────────┤
    │   Actively searching               │ ← Perfect timing
    │ "I've tried X, Y, nothing works"   │
    ├─────────────────────────────────────┤
    │   Solved / Don't care              │ ← Too late
    │ "I already have a solution"       │
    └─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Target**: Actively searching segment (highest conversion).

### The Learning Framework

```
Conversation 1 → Learn about problem space
    ↓
Conversation 2 → Deep dive into specific story
    ↓
Conversation 3 → Validate solution approach (not features)
    ↓
Conversation 4 → Test pricing (based on past payment)
    ↓
Conversation 5 → Confirm you're solving the right problem for right people
```

**Each conversation should narrow your hypothesis.**

## 3. Modelos Mentales

### Modelo de "Complementary Lies"

**Lie 1**: Users lie to be nice ("Great idea!")
**Lie 2**: You lie to yourself ("They loved it!")

**The Mom Test breaks both lies**:
- Forces specific questions that break social niceties
- Requires you to listen to facts, not validation

### Modelo de "Problem Awareness Spectrum"

```
Unaware → Aware → Searching → Solving → Satisfied
   ↑                          ↑
Target    Don't target
```

**Marketing** creates awareness
**Your product** solves for those searching

**Mistake**: Selling to unaware (too early) or satisfied (too late).

### Modelo de "Feature vs Problem"

```
Feature: "A better way to organize files"
Problem: "I wasted 2 hours yesterday finding a document"
```

**Difference**:
- Features are hypothetical solutions
- Problems are verified pain
- Build solutions to verified problems

### Modelo de "Innovation Adoption Curve"

```
Innovators (2.5%)
    ↓
Early Adopters (13.5%) ← Target for initial validation
    ↓
Early Majority (34%)
    ↓
Late Majority (34%)
    ↓
Laggards (16%)
```

**Start with Early Adopters**:
- They have the problem acutely
- They're actively searching
- They're willing to try imperfect solutions

## 4. Criterios de Decisión

### When to Stop Talking to Customers

| ✅ Enough conversations when | ❌ Need more conversations when |
|------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| You've heard the same story 3x | Every conversation is different |
| You can predict their answers | Surprises every conversation |
| You know the exact problem | Problem is still vague |
| You know how they currently solve it | Unclear about current solutions |
| You know the cost of the problem | Don't know willingness to pay |

### Customer Segmentation Criteria

| Good segmentation | Bad segmentation |
|-------------------|------------------|
| "People who pay for X" | "People aged 25-34" |
| "People who tried 3+ solutions" | "People with smartphones" |
| "People who spent $100+ on this" | "People who live in US" |
| Behavior-based | Demographic-based |

### When to Pivot vs Persevere

| Pivot signal | Persevere signal |
|--------------|------------------|
| No one has the problem | Problem is real, intense |
- Problem exists but no one pays | People already pay for solutions |
| Can't reach the people who have the problem | Can reach target segment |
| Solution doesn't solve the problem | Solution works (early traction) |

### Pricing Validation

| Method | Why it works |
|--------|--------------|
| "How much did you pay last time?" | Past behavior = accurate |
| "What's your budget for this?" | Reveals price sensitivity |
| "What would you do if this cost $X?" | Tests price threshold |
| "Would you pay $X?" | ❌ Speculation, unreliable |

**Rule**: Never ask "would you pay". Always ask "what did you pay".

## 5. Anti-patrones

### Anti-patrón: "Pitching Disguised as Conversation"

**Problema**: "I'm building X, what do you think?"

**Solución**:
- Start with their problems
- Never mention your solution
- If they ask, say "I'm exploring options"

### Anti-patrón: "Asking Friends and Family"

**Problema**: They lie to be nice.

**Solución**:
- Talk to strangers
- Pay them for their time ($20 gift card)
- Anonymity helps honesty

### Anti-patrón: "Confirmation Bias"

**Problema**: Only hearing what you want to hear.

**Solución**:
- Write down predictions before conversation
- Compare to what you actually heard
- Seek disconfirming evidence

### Anti-patrón: "Solution First Conversations"

**Problema**: Describing your solution in detail.

**Solución**:
- Describe problem space
- Explore current solutions
- Keep solution vague ("We're exploring ways to help with X")

### Anti-patrón: "Asking About the Future"

**Problema**: "What would you pay for X?"

**Solución**:
- Ask about past behavior
- "How much did you spend last time?"
- "What did you try before?"

### Anti-patrón: "One Conversation"

**Problema**: Basing decisions on one customer conversation.

**Solución**:
- Minimum 5-10 conversations before decision
- Patterns emerge across conversations
- One person is anecdote, not data

### Anti-patrón: "Group Interviews"

**Problema**: Focus groups, group dynamics distort truth.

**Solución**:
- 1:1 conversations only
- People are more honest alone
- Group dynamics = social pressure

### Anti-patrón: "Email Surveys"

**Problema**: No follow-up questions, no depth.

**Solución**:
- In-person or video calls
- Can ask "why?" and dig deeper
- Observe body language (in-person)

### Anti-patrón: "Treating All Conversations Equally"

**Problema**: Giving same weight to everyone.

**Solución**:
- Weight by problem intensity
- Weight by buying authority
- Weight by representativeness
